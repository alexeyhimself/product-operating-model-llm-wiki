# Delivery Manager

> The role Cagan re-branded from "project manager" because the project-management certifications and behaviors had become so synonymous with Waterfall that the title was destructive in product orgs. The purpose is unambiguous — **get stuff shipped + handle production support**, freeing the PM to actually do product management ([[2014-07-13-cagan-the-delivery-manager-role|canon]]).

## Accountabilities
- **Impediment removal** — daily tracking of blockers across teams and dependencies; chasing them down.
- **Delivery tracking** — knowing when commitments will land and surfacing risk early.
- **Production support and triage** — for orgs with material run-time responsibilities, often a significant time investment.
- **Owning [[high-integrity-commitments|high-integrity commitment]] mechanics** — tracking dependencies, dates, and the cross-team coordination that makes a commitment honorable ([[2012-12-15-cagan-high-integrity-commitments|canon]]).
- **Not Agile coaching** — the role is not about teaching process; it's about clearing the path.

## Competencies — weak vs strong
| Competency | Weak | Strong |
|---|---|---|
| Impediment removal | Logs blockers in JIRA; pings the team | Chases blockers down personally; surfaces patterns to leadership |
| Technical depth | So non-technical the team finds explaining the blocker more expensive than dealing with it themselves | Technical enough that engineers willingly delegate context to them |
| Process posture | Waterfall-trained; tries to drive the team via ceremony | Adopts whatever cadence the team needs; mostly invisible |
| Commitment tracking | Reports status only after slippage | Surfaces drift early; tracks cross-team dependencies proactively |
| Relationship with PM | Either redundant with the PM or competing | Clear division of labor — DM does projects so PM can do product |

## Common failure modes
- **Waterfall-trained PMP** running Agile teams via mini-Gantt charts ([[2014-07-13-cagan-the-delivery-manager-role|canon: cause #1]]).
- **Agile-coach confusion** — the DM tries to coach process instead of chasing impediments ([[2014-07-13-cagan-the-delivery-manager-role|canon: cause #2]]).
- **Non-technical impediment-chaser** the team works around ([[2014-07-13-cagan-the-delivery-manager-role|canon: cause #3]]).
- **PM absorbing the role** — common in mid-sized orgs that "dismantled" delivery management; produces PM/project-manager mash-ups who can't do discovery ([[2019-06-28-cagan-coaching-managing-time|canon]]).

## Relationships
- **PM partnership** is the foundation. DM owns delivery mechanics, PM owns product judgment. The DM is what allows the PM to spend the canonical 4 hours/day on discovery ([[2019-06-28-cagan-coaching-managing-time|canon]]).
- **Trio interplay** — DM operates *alongside* the [[the-product-team-trio|trio]] (PM + designer + Tech Lead), not as a fourth voice in discovery. The trio decides what to build; the DM helps it ship.
- **Engineering leadership** — the DM is a peer relationship; not a reporting line into engineering.
- **Scale dependence** — in startups, the PM owns this. The role becomes load-bearing as engineering complexity / cross-team dependencies / production-support burden grow.
