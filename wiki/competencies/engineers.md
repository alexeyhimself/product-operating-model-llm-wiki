# Engineers (and the Tech Lead)

> Engineers are **the single most important source of innovation** in a strong product company — they're working with the enabling technology every day, so they're in the best position to see what's *just now possible* (Cagan, [[2020-04-20-cagan-the-most-important-thing|The Most Important Thing]]). The **Tech Lead** is the engineer in the [[the-product-team-trio|trio]], accountable for **feasibility risk** and **product delivery** ([[2023-03-15-cagan-product-model-competencies|Cagan, Product Model Competencies]]).

## Accountabilities (Tech Lead, and the engineering team)
- **Feasibility risk** — can we build and scale this with the staff, time, technology, and data we have? One of the [[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]].
- **Delivery** — building, testing, deploying, instrumenting, and monitoring the product. Owns the practice of [[small-frequent-uncoupled-releases|small/frequent/uncoupled releases]] and the underlying [[deployment-infrastructure|deployment infrastructure]].
- **Architecture and implementation** — naturally, but these alone are *not* what "empowerment" means.
- **Co-innovating in discovery** — engineers are first-class participants in [[product-discovery|discovery]], not just delivery (see [[2020-10-30-cagan-discovery-delivery]] for why one team must do both).

> **What engineering is *not* in this model:** mercenaries; an outsourced cost center; a "build what design hands us" pipeline; "the people we tell what to build at sprint planning."

## Empowerment of an engineer (canon)
From [[2020-04-20-cagan-the-most-important-thing|The Most Important Thing]]: empowerment of an engineer means you give them **the problem to solve and the strategic context**, and they're free to leverage technology to find the best solution.

Empowerment is **not**:
- letting engineers decide *how to code* (they should anyway)
- letting engineers determine the architecture (they should anyway)

**The first-time-at-sprint-planning test.** "If the first time your engineers see a product idea is at sprint planning, you are clearly a feature team, and your engineers are not empowered in any meaningful sense."

## Why this competency is the most important thing
- Engineers see what's just now possible (the technology is their daily medium).
- Most breakthrough products you love originated from an empowered engineer ([[empowered-engineers]]).
- "If you're just using your engineers to code, you're only getting about half their value."
- Engineers are the easiest tell for [[missionaries-vs-mercenaries|missionaries vs mercenaries]] — outsourced engineering is literal mercenary work.
- "Engineering-driven" cultures are usually the **easiest** to transform into world-class product companies — they have the hardest part right already.

## Common failure modes
- **Sprint-planning surprise** — engineers first see ideas as Jira tickets at sprint planning. The diagnostic above.
- **Outsourced engineering** — Cagan: "a strong tech-powered product company would no sooner outsource their engineers than they would outsource their CEO."
- **Engineers excluded from customer contact** — kills innovation; rebuild this with [[2020-09-04-cagan-discovery-problem-vs-solution|the engineer-in-the-room rule]] for customer visits.
- **Split into "delivery team"** — when discovery is done by one group and engineering "delivers" what's handed over. See [[2020-10-30-cagan-discovery-delivery]] — same anti-pattern as outsourcing.
- **Career-ladder anchored only on management** — strong companies have **dual-track ladders** and pay top engineers at VP levels.

## Relationships
- **The trio.** Tech Lead is a full peer with [[product-manager|PM]] and [[product-designer|designer]] in the trio. The PM owns value and viability, the designer owns usability, the Tech Lead owns feasibility. All three contribute across the full design of the solution.
- **PM.** Engineers depend on PM to bring [[product-strategy|strategic context]], data, customer insight, and business constraints into the room.
- **Leadership.** Engineering managers do the staffing and coaching; product leaders provide the strategic context and the team objectives ([[product-leadership]]).
- **Discovery & delivery.** Same team does both, continuously. The PM and designer spend most of their time in discovery; the engineers spend most of their time in delivery — but everyone is involved in both.
