# The Product Team Trio

> The **three core competencies** every empowered product team needs: a **[[product-manager|Product Manager]]** (value + viability), a **[[product-designer|Product Designer]]** (usability + experience), and a **Tech Lead** ([[engineers|engineers]], feasibility + delivery). Working as peers — not in a hierarchy — they own the [[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]] together and produce solutions that are valuable, usable, feasible, and viable. Canon: [[2023-03-15-cagan-product-model-competencies|Cagan, Product Model Competencies]].

## Why the trio (not a "feature factory" assembly line)
A solution that succeeds must clear **all four** of the [[the-four-big-risks|four risks]] — and no one role can carry them alone. The trio exists to make sure each risk has an owner *and* that the trio works the design of the solution together, in the **interplay** between value/viability, usability, and feasibility:

| Role | Risk owned | Accountable for |
|---|---|---|
| [[product-manager\|Product Manager]] | Value + Viability | **Outcomes** of the product |
| [[product-designer\|Product Designer]] | Usability | **Experience** — every interaction |
| Tech Lead ([[engineers\|engineers]]) | Feasibility | **Delivery** |

Cagan ([[2020-09-04-cagan-discovery-problem-vs-solution|Discovery: Problem vs. Solution]]): "It is the *collaboration* of product (representing value and viability), design (representing usability) and engineering (representing feasibility) that generates great products. It is the *interplay* between these three dimensions."

## How the trio actually works
- **Same team owns discovery and delivery** continuously ([[2020-10-30-cagan-discovery-delivery|canon — no split into a "discovery team" and a "delivery team"]]). PM and designer spend most of their time in discovery; engineers spend most of their time in delivery; **everyone participates in both**.
- **Engineers in the room from day one.** The first time engineers see a product idea must not be at sprint planning ([[2020-04-20-cagan-the-most-important-thing|empowered-engineer test]]).
- **PM is not the trio's boss.** Cagan: "What each of design and engineering brings to the table is just as essential as what the PM brings."
- **Designer owns prototyping & user testing**, PM owns value/viability and strategic context, Tech Lead owns architecture/feasibility/delivery — but no one role hands off; the design of the solution is co-produced.

## Common failure modes
- **PM-as-boss.** PM "defines the requirements" and hands them to designer/engineers. Same anti-pattern Cagan diagnoses in [[2020-10-30-cagan-discovery-delivery]].
- **No designer (or PM doing design).** Usability has no owner; UX defaults to whatever screens engineers produce.
- **No Tech Lead in discovery.** Engineering becomes a build pipeline; feasibility surprises late; no innovation surfaces from engineers.
- **One person, two hats.** Sometimes possible — Cagan allows that a single person can cover multiple skill sets, or two roles can suffice for a particular product. But this is the exception; default is three people.
- **Trio without a real team behind it.** The trio leads, but the whole engineering team must be engaged with the product, not isolated as deliverables.

## Relationships
- **Empowered teams.** The trio is the *competency core* of an [[empowered-product-teams|empowered product team]]. Without it, "empowerment" is just a label.
- **Product leadership.** Staffs and coaches the trio; provides the [[product-strategy|strategic context]]; does **not** make the design decisions for them.
- **Other team members.** Product ops, user researchers, data analysts, product marketing all support the trio without diluting accountabilities.
