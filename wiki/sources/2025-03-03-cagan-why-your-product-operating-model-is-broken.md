# Cagan — Why Your Product Operating Model is Broken (Lean Product Meetup)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · talk + Q&A, Lean Product Meetup (Dan Olsen), recorded 2025-02-11 · YouTube, published 2025-03-03 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zrGOIJzA2jM). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — the root source for [[founder-style-leadership]] and [[product-sense]].** Cagan reframes Brian Chesky's "Founder Mode" (via Paul Graham) as **founder-style leadership = product sense + coaching**, and gives the fullest public account of how product sense is *earned*.

## Notes for the coach
- **Founder-style leadership is NOT micromanagement, and NOT its opposite** (professional / laissez-faire / delegation "adult supervision" — the Scully/Apple cautionary tale). It's product sense + coaching, at scale. Not a "mode"; a *style*; it matters **more** as the company grows. Netflix "lead with (strategic) context" (Leslie Kilgore). Amazon "dive deep," Apple "experts lead experts / deep expertise," Google "substance first, develop others through coaching" — leadership principles that trace partly to [[bill-campbell]].
- **Product sense is earned, not innate.** Develop it by: meeting users/customers at every level (CEOs skip-level to IC engineers); immersing in the data (qual usage + quant over time); earning stakeholders' trust one-on-one (the eBay legal/IRS-marketplace story with Rob Chestnut); consuming analysts; and, where possible, brain-dumping the original founder. First 3 months are the critical onboarding window — true even for hires from top companies.
- **"You will be judged by your weakest product manager."**
- **Scaling: process vs people.** Steve Jobs's "disease of process people." Coaching, not process, is the answer to scaling founder-style leadership.
- **Domain expertise ≠ domain dogma** (Shreyas Doshi) — don't confuse "law vs lore"; compliance rarely blocks continuous deployment.
- Books referenced elsewhere in the talk family: *Good Strategy Bad Strategy* (Rumelt), *The Art of Action* (Bungay).
