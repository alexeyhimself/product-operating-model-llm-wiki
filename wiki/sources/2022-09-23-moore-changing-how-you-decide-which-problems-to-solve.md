# Moore — Changing How You Decide Which Problems To Solve

> **Citation:** Jon Moore (SVPG) · "Changing How You Decide Which Problems To Solve" · svpg.com, 2022-09-23 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/changing-how-you-decide-which-problems-to-solve/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Third of the three-part SVPG transformation sequence — the *Decide which problems* dimension. Walks the cascade: **customer-driven [[product-vision]]** → **insight-driven [[product-strategy]]** → **[[team-objectives]]** assigned to product teams → discovery + delivery. Introduces the *outcome-based roadmap* as the easy starting move, then argues that the harder, more important question is whether those are the *right* problems at all. Closes with the role of [[product-leadership]] — calling out that many transformations have a larger gap in leadership than in individual contributor competencies.

## Notes for the coach
- **Vision timeframe (canon):** software 2-5 years; device companies 5-10 years (also see [[2020-08-10-cagan-product-vision-faq]] for nuance).
- **Vision = about customers**, not money, priorities, or org chart. Useful when a user shows you a "vision" that's really an aspiration to grow revenue.
- **Product strategy ≠ business strategy ≠ GTM strategy.** Many companies have the first two, lack a real product strategy entirely. The third dimension makes this concrete.
- **Outcome-based roadmaps** are the easy first step (translate each existing feature into the underlying problem + measure of success); doesn't fix priority selection but does meaningfully improve team agency.
- **Quotable.** "If you chase two rabbits, you will not catch either one." (Russian proverb cited).
- **Product leadership gap.** Use this article when a transformation effort is failing at the leadership layer rather than at the trio layer.
