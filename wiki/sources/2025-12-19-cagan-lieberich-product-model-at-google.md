## Notes for the coach
- **"Experts lead experts."** Google's engineering management unit is the **Tech Lead Manager (TLM)** — a hands-on tech lead who also manages a few engineers; the PM analogue is the **Group Product Manager (GPM)**. This is the concrete meaning of "empowered teams don't require *less* management; they require *better* management."
- **Broadcast problems + multiple teams on one problem.** Rather than assigning a problem to one team, Google leaders sometimes broadcast a problem and let teams opt in, and often run **multiple teams at the same hard problem** — "a luxury most companies don't have."
- **Not all Google teams are empowered.** Cagan is explicit: some are feature teams (haven't earned trust, or a controlling leader). Useful antidote to "Google = all empowered."
- **OKRs were built for this.** Google didn't invent OKRs (Intel did) but is the poster child; OKRs "map directly" to empowered teams given problems + outcomes — and are "an incongruous technique that rarely provides value" for feature teams chasing roadmaps. Backs a future [[okrs]] page.
- **The AI-era test.** Google invented the transformer (2017 *Attention Is All You Need*); navigated Desktop→Mobile→"AI First" (2016); Gemini at 650M+ MAU. Cagan's "true test" = delivering results over time through disruption.
