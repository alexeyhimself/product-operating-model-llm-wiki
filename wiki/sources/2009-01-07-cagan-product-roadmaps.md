# Cagan — Product Roadmaps

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Roadmaps" · svpg.com, 2009-01-07 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-roadmaps/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon, early piece).** Critique of the feature-prioritization-spreadsheet roadmap pattern; reframes a good roadmap as a high-level path from where you are to the product vision, *not* a prioritized feature list. The article that grounds the broader Cagan stance against feature-roadmaps and seeds the eventual move to objective-based roadmaps.

## Notes for the coach
- **"Your job is not to prioritize and document feature requests."** Sharpest line.
- **A roadmap is not a spec.** When stakeholders treat it as one, you have a feature-team problem.
- **Public roadmaps** are marketing tools and should be high-level and conservative — protect maneuvering room.
- Predates [[2015-09-07-cagan-the-alternative-to-roadmaps|The Alternative to Roadmaps]]; the two together cover the critique + the replacement.
