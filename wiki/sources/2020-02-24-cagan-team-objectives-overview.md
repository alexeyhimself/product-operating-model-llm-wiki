# Cagan — Team Objectives: Overview

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Team Objectives – Overview" · svpg.com, 2020-02-24 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/team-objectives-overview/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here. Series intro (reviewed by Christina Wodtke & Felipe Castro).

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The intro to the nine-part **Team Objectives** series and the definitive statement of why Cagan **stopped recommending OKRs** in most companies: **OKRs are first and foremost an empowerment technique**, and three things break them — (1) feature teams (a cultural mismatch), (2) manager/individual objectives instead of *team* objectives, (3) leadership "missing in action." Correlation vs causation: top companies don't succeed *because* of OKRs; they use OKRs because the technique is built for the empowered-team model.

## Notes for the coach
- **Three prerequisites to get value from OKRs:** move to the empowered-team model; drop manager/individual objectives for team objectives; leaders must turn strategy into action.
- The clean rebuttal to "we tried OKRs and they didn't work": you can't overlay an empowerment technique on a feature-team culture. Root of the "OKR theater" idea already on [[product-strategy]].
