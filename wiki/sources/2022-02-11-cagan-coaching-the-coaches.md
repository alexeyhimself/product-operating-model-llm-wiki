# Cagan — Coaching the Coaches

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Coaching the Coaches" · svpg.com, 2022-02-11 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-the-coaches/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — community-building history.** 2022 announcement of an SVPG-hosted, free, three-day intensive in London (May 24-26, 2022) for independent product coaches and trainers from the UK/EU. Article is **largely historical** — the event happened in 2022 and is no longer being offered (per the note at the top of the source). What it backs is documentary: SVPG's **explicit acknowledgement that the coaching supply gap is the bottleneck** to broader product-model adoption, and SVPG's strategic move into community building to address it. Directly anticipates SVPG's later Feb 2026 model-as-coach pivot ([[2026-02-04-cagan-product-coaching-and-ai]]).

## Notes for the coach
- **The supply-gap thesis** — Cagan: "we have very limited supply compared to the demand… for the majority of companies that reach out to us, we can't help in any reasonable amount of time." This is the explicit motivation for both the Coach the Coaches program (2022) and the model-as-coach prescription (2026).
- **"The global rise of SAFe is proof enough"** — Cagan's blunt framing of why SVPG felt obligated to scale coaching beyond their own partners.
- **What SVPG selects for in coaches** — experience in PM, design, engineering, or product leadership (the [[product-coach|experience prerequisite]]); career intent toward coaching others; **not** facilitation-only backgrounds.
- **No SVPG certification, no SVPG materials license, no strings attached** — explicit. Useful when discussing what makes coaching legitimate vs. cargo-cult certification programs.
- **Historical caveat** — the article ends with workshop logistics for a 2022 event. Don't cite it as if the program is still open.
- **The article's framing of strong coaches** — "Someone that genuinely cares about you and your career, but that also has a deep understanding of the nature of product. Someone that understands that it's not about following some process, but rather about doing the daily work of discovery and delivery."
