# Cagan & Olsen — Straight Talk about Product Ops (Lean Product Meetup)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, fireside with Dan Olsen · Lean Product Meetup webinar, recorded 2022-01-20 · YouTube, published 2022-03-07 — [watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4ROQnrvb8LM). Slim provenance card; transcript **not reproduced**.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — root source for [[product-ops]].** Cagan's fullest public account of product ops: the ~six models ("classifying species"), the endorsed **force-multiplier model**, and the three pillars (user research / analytics / best-practices+tools). Also seeds [[product-marketing]] and [[data-product-management]].

## What it backs
- competencies: [[product-ops]] (root), [[product-marketing]] (the "rebranding" model; PM needs a PMM partner), [[delivery-manager]] (project/program management still matters)
- concepts: [[data-product-management]] (embed-vs-shared user research/analytics), [[team-topology]] ("highly aligned, loosely coupled")
- entities: [[martina-lauchengco]] (LOVED forthcoming), [[marty-cagan]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Force multiplier, not governance.** Endorsed model = take your best people, raise the bar on everyone. Danger models: PMO resurrection; band-aid for two-in-a-box / delegated leadership; junior staffing; tool standardization that locks teams in.
- **"Where were the managers?"** The 25-roadmap-formats problem is a management failure, not a reason to staff an org.
- Also touches PM-vs-PO ("never seen it work well"), design ops / CDO level, and team topology (Netflix/Amazon).
