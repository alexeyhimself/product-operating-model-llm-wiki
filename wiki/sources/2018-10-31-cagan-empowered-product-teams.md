# Cagan — Empowered Product Teams

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Empowered Product Teams" · svpg.com, 2018-10-31 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/empowered-product-teams/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The long-form narrative behind the empowered-team frame. Names the five **leadership** responsibilities (vision, strategy, principles, priorities, evangelism) and the three **management** responsibilities (staffing, coaching, objectives). Argues the limiting factor is rarely talent — it's *trust* — and reframes staffing around competence + character + diversity, not "cultural fit." Source of the "ordinary people, extraordinary teams" thesis and the three empowerment tests at the end.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** "Empowered product teams don't require *less* management, they require *better* management." — the single most useful line for leaders confused by empowerment.
- **Three empowerment tests** (verbatim closing): staffed with competence + character? assigned problems vs. features? accountable for outcomes vs. output?
- **No-asshole rule:** the All Blacks anecdote is Cagan's preferred reframe of "cultural fit."
- **Litmus test for empowerment:** the team is able to decide the best way to solve the problems they have been assigned (the objectives).
