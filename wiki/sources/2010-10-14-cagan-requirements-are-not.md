# Cagan — Requirements Are Not

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Requirements Are Not" · svpg.com, 2010-10-14 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/requirements-are-not/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** A short, sharp argument that "requirements" are hypotheses — customer asks are theories, stakeholder asks are personal theories, the true requirements emerge from observation and prototyping. The article that grounds the broader Cagan stance against requirement-doc culture.

## What it backs
- diagnostics: [[discovery-health]], [[delivery-health]]
- concepts: [[product-discovery]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** "Our only real requirement is to discover product solutions that work well for our users, our customers and our business."
- Useful against Waterfall residue inside Scrum (overweighted user stories treated as "the requirement").
- Form and function are intertwined — design and engineering choices change what's needed; spec-first is backwards.
