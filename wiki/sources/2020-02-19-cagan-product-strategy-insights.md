# Cagan — Product Strategy: Insights

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Strategy — Insights" · svpg.com, 2020-02-19 (Pillar 2 of 4) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-strategy-insights/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Pillar 2 of the strategy series. Names the four sources of insights: quantitative, qualitative, technology, industry. Distinguishes **evaluative** vs. **generative** qualitative insights. Argues that insights must be *shared* through leadership channels (head of product circulating top learnings in all-hands), not just discovered.

## Notes for the coach
- **Four sources** are the diagnostic anchor.
- **Generative vs. evaluative:** an important distinction many product teams miss — most discovery insights are evaluative; generative insights need explicit room to emerge.
- **"No paint-by-number"** disclaimer — useful when a user wants a strategy template.
- **Consultancies caveat** — usually weak at product strategy because they focus on business strategy and don't have deep enough context.
