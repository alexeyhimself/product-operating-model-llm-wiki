# Cagan — The Alternative to Roadmaps

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Alternative to Roadmaps" · svpg.com, 2015-09-07 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-alternative-to-roadmaps/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The companion to [[2009-01-07-cagan-product-roadmaps|*Product Roadmaps*]]: what to replace the feature-roadmap *with*. Two-component answer: **product vision** (holistic view of what the org is trying to accomplish) + **team objectives** (specific business problems each team must solve, typically via OKRs). Cites Patton's "Don't tell people what to do; tell them what you need accomplished" and the military "intent" concept.

## Notes for the coach
- **Two preserved purposes of old roadmaps**: highest-value priority and date-based commitments. Any alternative must address both — the former via team objectives, the latter via [[high-integrity-commitments]].
- **Squad and intent metaphors** make the autonomous-team model legible to military/enterprise audiences.
- **The litmus test:** look back over the year and grade your roadmap on the % of items that actually moved business outcomes.
