> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Empowered Engineers FAQ" · svpg.com, 2020-04-23 (companion to *The Most Important Thing*) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/empowered-engineers-faq/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Q&A companion to [[2020-04-20-cagan-the-most-important-thing|*The Most Important Thing*]] — answers the most common objections to empowered engineers: "engineers only want to code," "they'll over-engineer," "they're clueless about customers," "what's my PM role then?" Reveals that the most common objection is usually a PM/leadership problem disguised as an engineer problem.

## Notes for the coach
- **"My engineers only want to code"** — usually the PM excluded them; ask the engineers directly. Single most useful FAQ pattern.
- **SAFe + empowered engineers = polar opposites.** Useful when an org thinks SAFe is "scaled agile."
- **"Just hand off problem + measure"** — Cagan calls this out as ridiculous; the PM still owns value/viability.
- "Bring engineers to customers" — the single intervention that flips most of these dynamics.
