# Cagan — Missionaries vs. Mercenaries

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Missionaries vs. Mercenaries" · svpg.com, 2015-11-25 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/missionaries-vs-mercenaries/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Carries the John Doerr quote and Cagan's three-cause diagnosis of why orgs end up with mercenary teams: leadership (executives who think they know the answers), staffing (inherited mercenary org / outsourced engineers), and process (SAFe-like enterprise frameworks predicated on the mercenary model). The article that grounds [[missionaries-vs-mercenaries]] as a load-bearing concept.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** Doerr — "We need teams of missionaries, not teams of mercenaries."
- **Cagan vs. SAFe:** explicit — "I literally don't know of a single strong product company that uses it." Useful when a user defends SAFe as "scaled agile."
- **Outsourced engineers are the canonical mercenary pattern.** Engineers under contract to build what they're told *are* mercenaries; this is "epic waste."
- **The three causes compound** — leadership drives staffing drives process. Transformation needs to address all three.
