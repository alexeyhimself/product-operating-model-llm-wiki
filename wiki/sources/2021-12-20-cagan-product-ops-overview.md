## Notes for the coach
- **"If a company says 'we have Product Ops,' that's a fairly meaningless statement today"** — six definitions, nothing genuinely *new in decades*; but packaging/bundling can have real value.
- **The six models (most-damaging → most-valuable):** (1) **Reincarnated PMO** — command-and-control governance smuggled back in (SAFe, "Process People"); (2) **Two-in-a-Box PM** — a product ops manager doing the PM's "day-to-day" (same whack-a-mole as PM+PO / PM+BA / inbound-outbound / PM+APM); (3) **Delegated Product Leader** — coaching + strategy delegated to product ops because leaders won't; (4) **Production Operations rebranding** — keeping the product running in production, retitled (harmless); (5) **Product Marketing Manager rebranding** — product ops absorbs the harder modern PMM work (jury out, sympathetic); (6) **Force Multiplier** — the endorsed model.
- **Force Multiplier = three pillars** (paraphrasing Melissa Perri's *Escaping the Build Trap*): **Quantitative Insights** (data analysts; single source of truth; an *enablement* team, not analysis-as-a-service), **Qualitative Insights** (user researchers; coach teams to learn first-hand, don't do research *for* them), **Tools & Best-Practices Evangelism** (staff with a *principal* PM/designer, never a junior; onboarding, advanced discovery coaching, tool selection). Sits at the same org level as product management and product design.
- **Staffing warning:** all three pillars are non-trivial skill sets; junior staffing has the *opposite* effect (like CSPO training by people who never did the job).
- The three "damaging" models are old problems with fresh lipstick; two are harmless rebrands; only the force-multiplier is a real step forward.
