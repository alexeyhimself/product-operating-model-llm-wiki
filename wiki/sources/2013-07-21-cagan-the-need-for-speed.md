# Cagan — The Need for Speed

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Need for Speed" · svpg.com, 2013-07-21 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-need-for-speed/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's 10-reason argument for why speed is critical to product work — explicitly **decoupling speed from time-to-market**. Time-to-market isn't even in Cagan's top 10. The deeper reasons: (1) more iterations → more learning, (2) MVP focus, (3) leadership support, (4) team purpose, (5) happier customers, (6) better quality (counterintuitive), (7) live-data validation, (8) team morale, (9) constant hunt for better techniques, (10) sustainable differentiator. Closing rule: "speed does not mean working 15-hour days" — speed is a function of skills, techniques, and culture.

## Notes for the coach
- **Speed is the only sustainable differentiator (#10)** — flag for any "we'll out-feature them" strategy conversation. If they can build it, you can't differentiate on features; you can differentiate on *learning velocity*.
- **Counterintuitive: speed → better quality (#6)** — old big-batch QA model produced *worse* quality. Continuous integration/delivery with small batches is the *quality* path, not the corner-cutting path.
- **Speed → MVP focus (#2)** — useful for teams that over-engineer. The sense of urgency keeps the team honest about scope.
- **"Quality, time, or scope — pick any two"** — Cagan names this old waterfall maxim as the deeply-rooted misconception modern product work must dismantle.
