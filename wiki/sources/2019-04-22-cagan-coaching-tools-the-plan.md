## Notes for the coach
- **It's a curriculum, not a checklist.** Per skill, Cagan gives concrete coaching moves: e.g. product knowledge → ≥15 customer visits, a filled-in business-model canvas, "make a friend in finance," dogfooding; process → read *[[inspired|INSPIRED]]* then pressure-test with scenarios, CSPO for product-owner mechanics; people → observe real trio + stakeholder interactions and debrief one-on-one.
- **People skills are coached by observation.** Cagan's signature move: sit in on a one-hour problem/objective discussion with the PM, designer and engineer, then pull the PM aside to point out what built or eroded trust. "A one-hour meeting … will usually yield many good examples."
- **People skills can mostly be developed — if the person wants to.** "They do have to want to improve." If not, the manager helps them find a more suitable role.
- **Leadership must be earned, not titled.** The PM leads a team and stakeholders who don't report to them; the prerequisites (knowledge + skills + earned trust) are the path.
