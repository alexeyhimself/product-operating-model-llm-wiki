# Cagan — Product Management: Start Here

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Management – Start Here" · svpg.com, 2020-06-12 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-management-start-here/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's primer for aspiring PMs — a curated, ordered reading path. Reinforces the canon: PM owns **value and viability**; product-owner (CSPO) is a small administrative subset of the role; if you're in a feature-team org with stakeholders making the calls, you're not really in the PM role; the way to ramp is [[2019-04-08-cagan-coaching-tools-the-assessment|self-assessment]] → [[2019-04-22-cagan-coaching-tools-the-plan|create a plan]] → 3 months with weekly coaching. Also recommends six other product thinkers (Torres, Hester-Reilly, Perri, Castro, Patton, Doshi).

## Notes for the coach
- **CSPO vs. PM (canon):** "the product owner responsibilities are just a very small subset of the product manager responsibilities." Use when a user has only CSPO training and is confused about why their work isn't producing outcomes.
- **The "If your company is using SAFe" line.** Cagan is direct: "if you want to be a true product manager, I'm sorry to say this but you'll likely have to move to a company that knows the difference and why it matters." Useful when a user asks about working as a PM inside SAFe.
- **Ramp expectation (canon):** "about three months of real work for a new product manager to get to a basic level of competence" — and that's *with* an experienced manager coaching weekly. Helpful for setting expectations on both new PMs and their managers.
- **Wireframes/prototyping/user-research = designer's job, not PM's** (Cagan is firm here). Useful for PMs migrating from "I do the design" mode.
- **The author shortlist** is good provenance for the [[recommended-library]] — six thinkers Cagan personally endorses for PM development.
