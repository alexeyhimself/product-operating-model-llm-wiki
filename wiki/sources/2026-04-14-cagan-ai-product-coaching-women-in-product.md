# Cagan — AI Product Coaching (Women in Product, In The Lead)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, in conversation with Carmen Palmer · "In The Lead" (Women in Product / Product Rising) · YouTube, 2026-04-14 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H_ck5RXeI3M). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Notes for the coach
- **Three prompting keys** (verbatim mechanics): (1) tell the model you want the **product** model, not the project model — "that one piece of guidance is huge"; (2) tell it to act as a **coach** (challenge/critique, not affirmation); (3) tell it to **prioritize** the content of Teresa Torres, Shreyas Doshi, SVPG (models are trained mostly on project-model content and are otherwise self-contradictory).
- **Load strategic context:** product vision + product strategy + team topology + your role/team/industry (`svpg.com/examples`; *EMPOWERED* details strategic context).
- **The right question** isn't "is it a *great* coach?" but "is it better than what they get / good enough to reach competence?" — a lower bar, and yes. Time-to-competence was ~3 months with a good manager; Cagan guesses <half now, and it's 7×24.
- **Why coaching fails (canon):** managers never worked this way themselves; span of control is rising faster than coaching efficiency. Only a small subset of leaders coach.
- **Limits / why humans still matter:** surprisingly good at politics now, but at leadership level "there are things it doesn't even know it doesn't know" — **it doesn't know *you* or your specific execs** (the Jennifer Bailey / Bill Campbell example). Every product leader should use an AI coach *and*, ideally, a human product-leadership coach. Preserves the "still #1 fan of human coaching" tension.
- **New PM litmus test:** if you don't love prototyping and testing prototypes, product is not the role for you. "We're all builders" (Jeff Patton).
