# Cagan — 10 Major Misconceptions About Product at Startups (ProductX 2022)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, keynote to ProductX (Israel), delivered remotely · YouTube, published 2023-05-17 — [watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QbjFdoQRnIU). Slim provenance card; transcript **not reproduced**.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** A compact enumeration of ten common product misconceptions, most reinforcing existing pages with fresh emphases and anecdotes.

## What it backs
- concepts: [[product-discovery]] (solve *old* problems ~10× better — Ben Horowitz; ~90% of time on solution; fall in love with the problem not the solution; 50–100 iterations; ideas are cheap — Jobs's 1995 "Lost Interview"; iPhone = 3.5 yrs discovery), [[the-four-big-risks]] (customers don't know what's *just now possible* — Bezos "no customer asked for Prime/AWS")
- competencies: [[empowered-engineers]] (Bill Campbell; the Intuit "you're fired" story; "we don't hire smart engineers to tell them what to do"), [[product-manager]] (value + viability), [[product-marketing]] (both sides of product-market fit)
- concepts: [[product-management-theater]] / [[principles-over-process]] (product-owner "epidemic"; scale with coaching, not process — Steve Blank; Jobs's "disease of process people"; IBM)

## Notes for the coach
- The ten map cleanly onto existing pages; use this talk for **vivid examples** (buy-now-pay-later, Nokia/Motorola focus groups) rather than new doctrine.
