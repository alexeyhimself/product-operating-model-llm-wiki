# Cagan — Coaching: Owner vs. Employee

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Coaching — Owner vs. Employee" · svpg.com, 2019-06-24 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-owner-vs-employee/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Grounds the **owner mindset** as a coaching target distinct from competence: thinking like an owner means taking responsibility for *outcome*, not just activities. Cagan recounts the mentor briefing he received and uses it as the template for coaching PMs into ownership. Carries the Bezos shareholder-letter quote ("each of whom must think like, and therefore must actually be, an owner").

## Notes for the coach
- **Most owners act like missionaries; not all missionaries act like owners.** A subtle but useful distinction when coaching PMs who care but don't yet take responsibility for outcomes.
- **Equity compensation** is the structural counterpart — Cagan: if you want owner behavior, share ownership materially.
- **Canary in the coal mine** — executives judge the health of a product team by the PM's words and actions.
- The recurring "Why?" structure in the article is itself a coaching template.
