# Jones — The Product Designer Role

> **Citation:** Chris Jones (SVPG) · "The Product Designer Role" · svpg.com, 2016-08-30 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-product-designer-role/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** SVPG's canonical definition of the modern product designer. Identifies five defining attributes: (1) **product orientation** (continuous collaboration in all phases, sits with PM and engineers, measured on product success), (2) **holistic experience design** (UX > UI; customer journey across touchpoints), (3) **prototyping** (the designer's primary canvas; no longer requires engineers), (4) **user testing** (weekly cadence; broader than usability testing — also tests *value*), (5) **interaction and visual design** (both, with some skill in each). Closes with the warning that strong UX is the whole team's responsibility, not solely the designer's.

## Notes for the coach
- **Five attributes** of the modern product designer (product orientation, holistic UX, prototyping, user testing, interaction+visual). Use as a designer assessment rubric, mirroring [[pm-competency]] for PMs.
- **UX ≠ UI** (canon). Cite when an org reduces design to screen production.
- **Designer-as-prototyper.** Prototyping used to require engineers; modern designers own this. Useful when a PM wants to "spec the screens" — push that work to the designer's canvas instead.
- **User testing ≠ usability testing.** Designers test *value* too — will customers actually use or buy this? Many designers and PMs don't realize this is part of the designer's job.
- **Strong UX is the whole team's responsibility.** Engineers and PMs must care about UX too; don't let the team treat design as a single-person black box.
