# Cagan — The Hardest Challenges of Product Strategy (Product Direction #44)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan on *100 Product Strategies* / Product Direction with Nacho Bassino (#44) · published 2024-03-28 — [watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hCsJ3e0c5fM). Slim provenance card; transcript **not reproduced**.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — a deep source for [[product-strategy]].** Why most companies have no strategy (same reason they don't do discovery), the two elements of strategy, the business/product/discovery "flight levels," and the underused technique of assigning one problem to multiple teams.

## What it backs
- concepts: [[product-strategy]] (peanut-butter "strategy"; leaders pick outcomes → product finds levers/insights → place bets; business strategy as strategic-context *input*; **same problem to multiple teams**; confidence levels — 50% vs 90%, roofshot vs moonshot; opportunity solution tree — Torres)
- diagnostics: [[product-strategy-quality]] (Steve Jobs "focus is saying no to hundreds of good ideas")
- concepts: [[transformation]] ("transformation theater"; fund teams-for-quarters vs projects), [[product-leadership]] (leaders have more agency than they think — don't "fight back," demonstrate)
- entities: [[christian-idiodi]] (Africa product community), [[product-coaching]] (strategy coaching; the experience prerequisite; the SVPG coach network)

## Notes for the coach
- **Cagan ranks strategy above discovery in importance:** "even if you're terrible at discovery, as long as the money doesn't run out you might get there; if the strategy is bad it almost never works out."
- Empowerment ≠ teams choosing their own problems; the *problem* is assigned, the *solution* is discovered.
