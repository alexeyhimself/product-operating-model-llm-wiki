# Cagan — Alternatives To Product Managers

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Alternatives To Product Managers" · svpg.com, 2023-11-28 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/alternatives-to-product-managers/). ## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's explicit statement that *product management* (responsibility for value + viability) never goes away, but *product managers* have never been the only way to cover it. Names three observed alternatives to a dedicated PM and their trade-offs, using Apple as the worked example of the third.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** "there really isn't an alternative to product *management* — someone is doing this one way or another." Use when a leader proposes cutting the PM role without asking who inherits value + viability.
- **The three alternatives:** (1) founder/stakeholder-driven (→ feature teams; Cagan: "the root cause of most failed efforts"); (2) another trio member steps up ("triple threats" — not scalable, two full-time jobs at once); (3) product-leader-driven (Apple's model — works when product leaders are exceptionally strong and the portfolio is a small number of complex products; becomes a bottleneck, which is why "Apple is not known for moving quickly").
- **Warning embedded in the article:** don't copy Apple's model unless you already have product leaders with Apple-caliber depth (10–25 years); imitating the *structure* without the *talent* just recreates the founder/stakeholder-driven failure mode with extra steps.
