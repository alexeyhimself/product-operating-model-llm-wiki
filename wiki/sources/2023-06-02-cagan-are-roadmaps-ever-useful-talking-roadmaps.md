# Cagan — Are Roadmaps Ever Useful? (Talking Roadmaps)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, interviewed by Phil Hornby · Talking Roadmaps ep. 26 · YouTube, 2023-06-02 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sgRYEVRD_4w). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's most nuanced spoken treatment of roadmaps: "used properly, roadmaps are a helpful tool — the problem is almost no one uses them that way."

## What it backs
- concepts: [[outcome-based-roadmap]] (root video; ~95% of roadmaps are output roadmaps), [[feature-teams-vs-product-teams]] (roadmaps and feature teams go hand in hand)
- frameworks: [[high-integrity-commitments]] (the trustworthy roadmap = high-integrity commitments), [[prototypes]] (never give a date without a feasibility prototype)
- competencies: [[delivery-manager]] (tracks cross-org commitments/impediments), [[product-strategy]]

## Notes for the coach
- **When** you roadmap is the crux: feature teams *lead* with the roadmap (before they know anything); product teams produce it *after* discovery as a communication tool. The roadmap is the **output** of discovery, not the input (the input is problems to solve; OKRs are the discovery system).
- **~95% of roadmaps are output roadmaps** — the vehicle that drives feature teams. It's not the document's fault; it's [[feature-teams-vs-product-teams|feature teams vs product teams]].
- **Engineers: never give a date without a feasibility prototype** — the origin of t-shirt sizing; "know what you can't know." The classic 1–2-sprint → 4–5-sprint slip is almost always a skipped feasibility prototype.
- **Pledge to Customers** (empowered teams never accept proxies; only commit a date after understanding the need) — a backlog source worth ingesting later.
- One-liner: "as long as you put real effort into knowing what you're promising — real evidence you can build it and the customer will be satisfied — that roadmap helps everyone. If you haven't, you're not doing anyone any favours."
