# Cagan — Good Product Team / Bad Product Team

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Good Product Team / Bad Product Team" · svpg.com, 2014-06-13 (foreword excerpt from Jeff Patton's *User Story Mapping*) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/good-product-team-bad-product-team/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** A seventeen-bullet contrast between strong and weak product teams across vision, ideas, stakeholders, discovery, collaboration, engineering involvement, testing, customer contact, iteration mindset, commitments, instrumentation, releases, customer obsession, and outcomes vs. output. Inspired by Horowitz's *Good Product Manager / Bad Product Manager*. The most useful single page in canon for *spotting* the difference between empowered teams and feature teams.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** "Good teams celebrate when they achieve a significant impact to the business (outcome). Bad teams celebrate when they finally release something (output)."
- **Quote magnet:** "Bad teams gather requirements from sales and customers." — useful when stakeholder-driven roadmaps are defended as "customer-centric."
- The "show the prototypes to engineers during sprint planning so they can estimate" bullet is the cleanest single sign of mercenary engineering culture.
