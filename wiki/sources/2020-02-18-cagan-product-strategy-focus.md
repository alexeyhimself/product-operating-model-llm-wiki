# Cagan — Product Strategy: Focus

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Strategy — Focus" · svpg.com, 2020-02-18 (Pillar 1 of 4) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-strategy-focus/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Pillar 1 of the four-pillar strategy series ([[2020-02-17-cagan-product-strategy-overview|overview]]). Argues that orgs claim focus but typically have 20–30 "high-priority" items vs. 2–3. Carries Rumelt's good-strategy quote and the **Pandora prioritization process** as the canonical anti-example of *prioritizing without focusing*. Introduces org-level WIP limits.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet (Rumelt):** "Good strategy works by focusing energy and resources on one, or a very few, pivotal objectives whose accomplishment will lead to a cascade of favorable outcomes."
- **Pandora as the anti-example** — first cited here and recurring across canon.
- **WIP limits scale up to the org**, not just the team.
