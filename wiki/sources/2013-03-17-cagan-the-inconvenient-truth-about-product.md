# Cagan — The Inconvenient Truth About Product

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Inconvenient Truth About Product" · svpg.com, 2013-03-17 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-inconvenient-truth-about-product/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Names the **two inconvenient truths**: (1) at least half of our ideas don't work, (2) even ideas that do work require several iterations to deliver value. The framing that justifies product discovery as the core competency of strong organizations.

## What it backs
- diagnostics: [[model-maturity]], [[discovery-health]]
- concepts: [[product-discovery]], [[time-to-money]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Strong teams ≠ teams that have great ideas; strong teams = teams that *kill bad ideas fast*.** This is the article's core reframe.
- **Good teams assume 3/4 of ideas don't work** — useful when a stakeholder asks why so many discovery efforts get cut.
- Useful when a user defends a long roadmap with "we're confident in our ideas."
