# Cagan — Product Management Rules in Early Stage (June)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, interviewed on The June Podcast (Seed Stage) · YouTube, 2024-03-21 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WdRVh3Ye1U8). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The startup/pre-PMF angle: why founders shouldn't hire a PM too early, and why startups need product *leaders*, not PMs.

## What it backs
- competencies: [[product-manager]] (don't hire too soon; value + viability is the founder's job pre-PMF), [[founder-style-leadership]] (the product founder *is* the product manager pre-PMF)
- concepts: [[product-discovery]] ("pre-PMF = discovery vs optimization"), [[feature-teams-vs-product-teams]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Pre-PMF vs post-PMF** reframed as **product discovery vs product optimization**: "if you're not doing real discovery during product-market fit, good luck."
- Don't hire a PM at seed stage; startups need product *leaders*, not PMs; the first product hires should own go-to-market and pricing too. Reinforces the founder-as-product-manager thesis in [[founder-style-leadership]].
