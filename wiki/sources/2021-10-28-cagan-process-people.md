# Cagan — Process People

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Process People" · svpg.com, 2021-10-28 (borrows Paul Graham's "makers vs managers" framing; followed by a Q&A follow-up "Process People, Continued") — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/process-people/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The article that **names the third contributor type** in product organizations: alongside **makers** (designers/engineers/PMs on empowered teams) and **managers** (of makers), Cagan identifies a rising third category — **process people** — whose primary job is "not the makers or the product, but *the process*." The most current instance in 2021 is **product ops**. Written a **~2 months before** the [[2021-12-20-cagan-product-ops-overview|Product Ops Overview]] and the [[2022-02-07-cagan-straight-talk-about-product-ops|Straight Talk]] talk — this essay is the *conceptual frame* those later pieces sit inside.

## Notes for the coach
- **Bezos on process:** "Good process serves you so you can serve customers. But if you're not watchful, the process can become the thing. This can happen very easily in large organizations." Cagan quotes this to frame the *conditional* nature of process — necessary, but dangerous if left un-watched.
- **The three types:**
  - **Makers** — designers, engineers, PMs on empowered teams, plus supporting research/analytics roles. They design and build.
  - **Managers** — people managers of makers. Responsible for **staffing and coaching**.
  - **Process people** — a *new* third category. Not responsible for the makers, not responsible for the product — **responsible for the process itself.** Examples Cagan names: Product Owner and Scrum Master (Agile), Black Belt (Six Sigma), and — the 2021 instance — one of the common definitions of **product ops** (a whole team providing process, tools, and methods for the broader product org).
- **The PM ambiguity:** on a *feature team*, the PM is more of a stakeholder/project manager (a process-adjacent role); on an *empowered product team*, the PM is a maker (a less pure version than a designer/engineer, but still a maker). This is the article that most crisply distinguishes those two.
- **Two ways orgs scale:**
  1. **With leaders (managers).** Cagan's whole [[empowered]] book. What good companies do.
  2. **With process.** "Agile at Scale" — SAFe et al. What Cagan writes against.
- **Cagan's mixed feelings about the *people*:** "vast majority have good intentions." But in practice they cause more harm than good, for **four reasons**:
  1. **Never performed the maker or manager role in a strong product company** → "more enthusiasm than experience or knowledge" → wrong people in control of how teams work → **prime targets for consultants and vendors** who've realized process people are their best path into your company.
  2. **Hired as a substitute for weak managers** → managers not willing/able to coach → a process person is "a very poor substitute for a strong manager with the necessary expertise." Fix the manager.
  3. **Process becomes religion** → hard to reason with a fanatic → (Cagan's aside: process people have more free time than makers/managers, so they spend disproportionate time on social media preaching their favorite methodology).
  4. **No one right way of doing product** → standardizing process across a large org = **institutionalizing a poor fit for most teams** (this reason cites [[2018-07-26-cagan-tools-and-processes|Tools and Processes]] directly).
- **Regional pattern:** Cagan sees the **product owner problem** taking root in **Europe** and the **product ops problem** taking root in the **US** — both stem from the same misguided focus on process-as-scaling-mechanism.
- **The prescription:** consider *very carefully* the mix of makers, managers, and process people. **Every process person is a choice not to fund another engineer, designer, or PM.** Make sure the purpose is clear and that they are not there to compensate for managers who won't coach.
- **How this maps to the product-ops canon:** the six models Cagan later catalogues in [[2021-12-20-cagan-product-ops-overview|Product Ops Overview]] are the specific 2021-era manifestations of the general "process people" phenomenon. Models #1 (Reincarnated PMO), #2 (Two-in-a-Box PM), and #3 (Delegated Product Leader) are all *process-people patterns*. Model #6 (Force Multiplier) is the escape hatch — it staffs experienced *makers/managers* in a support role, not process people.
