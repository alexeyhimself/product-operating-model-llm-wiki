# Cagan — TRANSFORMED (Pre-Launch Announcement)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "TRANSFORMED" (pre-launch announcement) · svpg.com, 2023-02-20 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/transformed/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Supporting (announces a primary source).** Pre-launch announcement of the book *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]* (then planned for early 2024, ultimately published 2024). Documents:
- **Authorship lineage** — originally Lea Hickman, then Jon Moore, finally Cagan as primary author with Hickman, Moore, Idiodi, Jones as co-authors.
- **Book topics** — the four new core competencies, five core product concepts, set of product first principles, transformation tools & techniques, common objections, common problems.
- **Three first-person transformation case studies** — Hickman, Idiodi, and Moore each led major successful pre-Internet transformations before joining SVPG.
- **Why TRANSFORMED exists** — INSPIRED was for product teams; EMPOWERED was for product leaders; TRANSFORMED is for "the rest" — the vast majority of companies for whom the product way of working is foreign.
- **Role of coaching** — explicit acknowledgement that most transforming companies' leaders have never experienced the product model themselves, making external [[product-coaching|coaching]] essential. Article previews SVPG's [[2022-02-11-cagan-coaching-the-coaches|Coach the Coaches]] community effort.

## Notes for the coach
- **"The rest vs. the best"** — Cagan's frame for who needs transformation (legacy companies in non-tech-native industries). The wiki uses this same frame in [[overview]] and [[transformation]].
- **Case studies are SVPG-original first-person accounts** — distinct from typical "as told to" transformation case studies. Worth noting to a user comparing TRANSFORMED to other transformation books.
- **Cagan dedicating most of 2023 to writing** — context for the article cadence between Feb 2023 and 2024 launch (most SVPG articles in that window are TRANSFORMED chapter previews).
- **One CEO quote worth remembering:** transforming feels like "changing from driving on the left side of the road to the right — but gradually." Captures the cultural difficulty better than most metaphors.
