## Notes for the coach
- **The four-risks worked example.** Value → usability → feasibility → viability, each named and addressed in order on one real feature. The best single illustration in the wiki of the [[the-four-big-risks|four risks]] in practice.
- **The empowered-team payoff.** Daniel Ek (co-founder CEO) was *openly skeptical* of Discover Weekly and said so — but gave the team a problem to solve and let the work proceed. Canonical illustration that empowerment = leaders provide strategic context + hard strategy calls, teams own the solution.
- **Idea from the engineers.** Two ML engineers, not the CEO, saw the possibility (seeded by the *Play It Forward* hack-week add-on to *Year in Music* / *Wrapped*).
- **"High-integrity business case."** Once evidence justified rebuilding the playlist system to scale, the investment was defensible — SVPG's term; ties to [[high-integrity-commitments]].
- **The 5% rule.** Any empowered Spotify team can roll an experiment to up to 5% of users without permission (they used 1.5% ≈ 1M users). Concrete marker of real empowerment + delivery infrastructure.
- **Myth-correction.** When a user cites "the Spotify Model," point here: the durable lesson is the product principles, not the org chart.
- Results: 1B tracks streamed in the first 10 weeks; 71% added ≥1 song to a personal playlist.
