# Cagan — Moving from an IT to a Product Organization

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Moving from an IT to a Product Organization" · svpg.com, 2008-07-07 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/moving-from-an-it-to-a-product-organization/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon, foundational).** One of Cagan's earliest articles on the IT-to-product transformation — predates the "Product Operating Model" branding by 15 years but lays out the **10 concrete organizational changes** needed when a legacy company's customer-facing software becomes its core business. The taxonomy is still load-bearing today: clear product/IT line, real PMs, UX designers, product-grade engineers, QA engineers, site ops, revisited dev processes, online marketing, reprioritized customer experience, instrumentation. ## Notes for the coach
- **The framing of "tech company" is *how*, not *what*** — anticipates [[2023-09-12-cagan-who-is-product-operating-model-for|Who Is the Product Operating Model For?]] by 15 years. The criterion: is your software customer-facing (thousands–millions, each making a purchase decision) or internal-facing (employees, mandated use)?
- **"Don't outsource your customer-facing experience to agencies"** — Cagan's stance on agencies/consultancies that perpetuate IT-style thinking; still relevant when an org buys "digital transformation" consulting.
- **Why product software is harder than IT software** — Cagan names the demand differences: discretionary use (no mandated training courses), competitive switching (one click), scale (thousands of users vs. millions), outage stakes (revenue + CEO attention vs. annoyed employees).
- **Age caveat** — this is a 2008 article using vocabulary like "online marketing organization" and "interaction designers." The roles and emphases hold; the surrounding industry framing has evolved.
