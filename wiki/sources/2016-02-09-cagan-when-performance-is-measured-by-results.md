# Cagan — When Performance Is Measured By Results

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "When Performance Is Measured By Results" · svpg.com, 2016-02-09 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/when-performance-is-measured-by-results/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here. Cagan's foreword to Christina Wodtke's *Radical Focus* (OKRs), shared on svpg.com.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — historical/lineage.** The wiki's earliest-dated grounding of Cagan's own origin story for outcome-based performance management: his engineering start at Hewlett-Packard, HP's internal "MBO" (Management by Objectives) training, and the direct lineage from MBO → Intel's refinements → the modern OKR system.

## What it backs
- frameworks: [[okrs]] (the MBO → Intel → OKR historical lineage; the two founding principles)
- concepts: [[team-objectives]]
- principles: [[outcomes-over-output]] (the HP tagline is an early, plain-language statement of the same idea)

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet (motivation principle):** the General Patton line Cagan learned at HP — "Don't tell people how to do things, tell them what you need done and let them surprise you with their results." Useful shorthand for [[empowered-with-problems-to-solve]].
- **Quote magnet (measurement principle):** HP's own tagline of that era — "When Performance Is Measured By Results" — "you can release all the features you want, but if it doesn't solve the underlying business problem, you haven't really solved anything." An early, plain-language statement of [[outcomes-over-output]], predating Cagan's later OKR-specific writing by ~4 years.
- **Historical chain:** MBO (HP, Cagan's era) → refined at several companies, "most notably Intel" → the modern OKR system. Cite this article, not [[2020-02-24-cagan-team-objectives-overview|the 2020 Team Objectives series]], when a coaching conversation needs the *origin story* rather than the operational mechanics.
- Cagan also names the still-current failure mode here, eight years before the 2020 series formalized it: "groups of executives... come up with the quarterly roadmap... teams are just there to flesh out the details... progress is measured by output and not outcome."
- Does not introduce new mechanics beyond what [[okrs]] already covers via the 2020 series — its value is provenance and the founding quotes, not new technique.
