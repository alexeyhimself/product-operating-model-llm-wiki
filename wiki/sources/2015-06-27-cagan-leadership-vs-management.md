# Cagan — Leadership vs. Management

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Leadership vs. Management" · svpg.com, 2015-06-27 (largely excerpting Abbott & Fisher's *The Art of Scalability*, 2e) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/leadership-vs-management/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The canonical SVPG framing of leadership (pull — inspire, set direction, create vision) vs. management (push — measure, give feedback, remove obstacles, ensure right person/job/time). Both required; they are distinct competencies, not a "style." Most senior roles need both.

- competencies: [[product-leadership]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** "If leadership is a promise, management is action. If leadership is a destination, management is the direction."
- **"Management style"** is a category error — leadership and management are distinct disciplines a single person can develop.
- The West Point context grounds Cagan's frame on leadership development.
