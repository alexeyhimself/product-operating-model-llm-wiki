# Moore — Transformation Defined

> **Citation:** Jon Moore (SVPG, with Chris Jones) · "Transformation Defined" · svpg.com, 2022-05-31 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/transformation-defined/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — the ROOT of the three-dimensions framework.** Predates and frames the three deep-dives ([[2022-09-17-moore-changing-how-you-build|Build]] · [[2022-09-20-moore-changing-how-you-solve-problems|Solve]] · [[2022-09-23-moore-changing-how-you-decide-which-problems-to-solve|Decide]]) that were published 4 months later. Moore's move here is to **reject label-based framings** ("Agile transformation," "empowered teams," "product-led") and define transformation by *what is actually changing*: how you build and deploy; how you solve problems; how you decide which problems to solve. Notes Moore's recommendation that the three changes be pursued **in parallel** via pilot teams — a stance Cagan refined later (see Flag below).

## Notes for the coach
- **Cite this as the *root* of the three-dimensions framing** — the three Sep-2022 Moore articles are the deep-dives. Prior wiki framing attributed the framework to those three articles; this article is where Moore first names it.
- **Sequencing tension to flag** ([[transformation-politics|see also]]): Moore here says the three changes can be pursued in parallel via [[pilot-teams|pilot teams]]. Cagan later (in [[2024-11-21-cagan-transformation-politics|Transformation Politics]]) refines this: hold off on the *Decide* change until the *Solve* and *Build* changes have won stakeholder confidence. Both stances are canon; Cagan's later guidance is the operational rule for politically constrained orgs.
- **Useful quote-trio to defuse label arguments** ("transforming to Agile may be necessary but in no way sufficient" / "the heart of transformation is moving from feature teams to empowered product teams" / "the goal is to transform to a product-led company") — Moore lists these as *partial* truths, none holistic enough on its own.
