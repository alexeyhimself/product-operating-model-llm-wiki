# Cagan — The Most Important Thing

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Most Important Thing" · svpg.com, 2020-04-20 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-most-important-thing/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's answer to "if you could pick *just one thing* to change about how a feature-team org works, what would it be?" — the answer is **the empowered engineer**. Engineers, working with the enabling technology every day, are the best single source for innovation. Empowerment of an engineer means giving them the *problem to solve* and the *strategic context* — not just letting them pick the architecture (which they should anyway). The article is also the canonical link for the SVPG-linked competency page for [[engineers]] (TRANSFORMED references it as the engineering/Tech Lead chapter).

## Notes for the coach
- **"Empowerment ≠ letting engineers pick the architecture."** They should pick architecture and implementation anyway. Empowerment means **problem + strategic context**, with engineers determining the best solution. Cite when a user thinks "we let engineers choose their stack" satisfies the empowerment criterion.
- **The "first time at sprint planning" test.** "If the first time your engineers see a product idea is at sprint planning, you are clearly a feature team, and your engineers are not empowered in any meaningful sense." Cleanest one-line diagnostic for engineer-empowerment status.
- **Outsourced engineering is literal mercenary work.** Cagan: "a strong tech-powered product company would no sooner outsource their engineers than they would outsource their CEO." Use sparingly but precisely.
- **The "engineering-driven company" surprise.** When a head of product warns Cagan their company is "engineering-driven," Cagan considers them ahead. Useful when a PM-leaning user disparages an engineer-led culture.
- **Half-value warning.** "If you're just using your engineers to code, you're only getting about half their value." Direct framing for the loss in feature-team orgs.
