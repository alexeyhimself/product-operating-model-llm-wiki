# Cagan — Time-Boxing Product Discovery

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Time-Boxing Product Discovery" · svpg.com, 2012-08-21 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/time-boxing-product-discovery/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Argues that time-boxing discovery (1 day to 1 week) helps teams that move too slowly establish a rapid ideation/validation rhythm. Distinguishes time-*boxing* (decide what to focus on, work the box, then reflect) from time-*limiting* (waterfall pre-build phase). Carries the canonical "what is the fastest, cheapest way to validate this idea?" framing.

## Notes for the coach
- **Pendulum risk:** time-boxing can swing toward output ("just get something done — feed the beast"). Use only when discovery is *too slow*, not when it's healthy.
- **Quote magnet:** "Building production software and deploying it live is among the slowest, most expensive ways to validate an idea."
- **Duration:** never longer than one week; rarely shorter than one day.
