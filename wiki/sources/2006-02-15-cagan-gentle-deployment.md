# Cagan — Gentle Deployment

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Gentle Deployment" · svpg.com, 2006-02-15 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/gentle-deployment/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Names the gentle-deployment toolkit: opt-in/opt-out windows, parallel versions, regional rollouts, incremental introduction. The earliest (2006!) Cagan piece codifying that "users do not like change" — deployment design is part of UX.

## Notes for the coach
- **Cultural reframe:** even when users *asked* for a feature, they may not want to learn it on your schedule.
- **Engineering cost is real:** parallel versions are expensive to maintain — Cagan acknowledges this is friction worth paying for significant changes.
- The 2006 timestamp matters — this idea precedes most modern feature-flag tooling.
