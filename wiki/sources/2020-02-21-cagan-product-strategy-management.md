# Cagan — Product Strategy: Management

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Strategy — Management" · svpg.com, 2020-02-21 (Pillar 4 of 4) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-strategy-management/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Pillar 4 of the strategy series. "No product strategy survives its initial encounter with the real world" — so leaders must **actively manage** strategy weekly through 1:1s with PMs, removing obstacles, escalating, and circulating learnings. Names this as **servant leadership**, distinct from command-and-control.

## Notes for the coach
- **The five typical obstacles** Cagan names (dependencies, missing tech/knowledge, customer escalations, platform-team contention, stakeholder concerns) form a checklist for what to actively manage.
- **Quote magnet:** "Empowered product teams don't need *less* management, they need *better* management."
- The weekly 1:1 is where strategy management *lives*, not in a quarterly review.
