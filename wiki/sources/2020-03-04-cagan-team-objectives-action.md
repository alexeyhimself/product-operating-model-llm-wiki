# Cagan — Team Objectives: Action

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Team Objectives – Action" · svpg.com, 2020-03-04 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/team-objectives-action/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The "action" step of [[product-strategy]]: **leaders decide which problems go to which teams** (a top-down + bottom-up, iterative process, a function of strategy + [[team-topology|topology]]). Addresses the common misconception that teams pick their own objectives — that produces "lack of direction." Teams *propose* key results; leaders judge whether the aggregate covers the org objectives; **KRs must come from the team**. Also names **"keep-the-lights-on" (KTLO)** work as the other, non-objective demand on a team.

## Notes for the coach
- **Misconception killer:** "let teams set their own objectives" → the Monty Python "which way?" chaos. Selecting problems and assigning teams is *leadership's* explicit job; the *how* (and the KR targets) is the team's.
- **KTLO** is real and must be budgeted — sometimes it consumes a team; then leaders lower expectations, invest to reduce overhead, or grow the team.
