# Cagan — Product Strategy: The Missing Link (Lean Product Meetup)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · talk + Q&A, Lean Product Meetup (Dan Olsen), recorded 2020-02-13 · YouTube, published 2020-02-22 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x4H_gluZI10). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — the spoken origin of the four-element product-strategy framework** (focus → insights → actions → management) that became the strategy pillars and the [[product-strategy-quality]] diagnostic.

## Notes for the coach
- **The four elements of product strategy: focus, insights, actions, management.** Strategy = the bridge from vision to what teams work on. The "underpants gnomes" gap: objectives → ??? → a roadmap of 100 features; strategy is the missing middle.
- **Focus ≠ prioritization** (the Pandora anti-pattern: funny-money to stakeholders → 60+ "must-dos" → IPO with 40 engineers → sold to SiriusXM for salvage; First Round Review's "Pandora prioritization" = "poster child for bad strategy"). Focus = the few needle-movers + WIP-limit logic at org scale. The HP Labs pair-programming performance story (3 hotspots = all the difference).
- **Insights:** qualitative (weekly customer/prototype conversations — Cagan's favorite) + quantitative (data/AB, needs volume) + enabling-tech ("just now possible") + industry. Leaders are a "learning distribution machine" who "connect the dots."
- **OKRs [FLAG]:** "I do NOT recommend OKRs anymore" *unless* you're already an empowered-team org — OKRs came *from* that model (Intel → Google); overlaying on feature teams = "**OKR theater**." Three requisites: (1) empowered teams, (2) team-level objectives only, (3) active leaders — **objectives come from leaders, key results from teams**.
- **Management = servant leadership** (not passive, not micromanage). "We don't need less management, we need better management." **Strategic context = command intent** (vision + strategy + business objectives + product principles). Books: *Good Strategy Bad Strategy* (Rumelt), *The Art of Action* (Bungay).
