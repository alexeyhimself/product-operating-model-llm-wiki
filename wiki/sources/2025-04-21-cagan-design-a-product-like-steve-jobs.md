# Cagan — How to Design a Product Like Steve Jobs (Info-Tech, Digital Disruption)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, interviewed by Geoff Nielson · Digital Disruption (Info-Tech Research Group) · YouTube, 2025-04-21 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QxbbXl5NfFw). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Debunks the "Steve Jobs the dictator" myth, links it to empowered engineers and product discovery, and gives the SVPG "three meta questions" assessment plus the McKinsey / agile transformation anti-patterns.

## Notes for the coach
- **The Steve Jobs myth [strong anti-pattern callout]:** Jobs was *not* a top-down dictator. He didn't know the solutions; he knew what makes a great product and set a very high bar. Teams brought him prototypes weekly; he pointed out where they fell short. Jobs: "we don't hire smart engineers to tell them what to do; we hire them to show us what's possible." The dangerous misconception is "act like Jobs = boss everyone around."
- **[[creative-selection|Creative Selection]]** (Ken Kocienda) = the real account of Apple's product discovery under Jobs: 100% touchscreen → ~100 hard problems to solve. Plus the 1995 Jobs "Lost Interview."
- **Bill Campbell** coached Jobs, Bezos, Larry + Sergey — "the same guy explaining essentially the product model." "You cannot be a good manager without being a good coach." "The single most important thing is empowered engineers."
- **The three meta questions** (SVPG assessment, ~30 min): how do you decide what to work on? how do you solve problems? how do you build/test/deliver? = the three transformation dimensions.
- **Two transformation anti-patterns:** (1) hiring McKinsey/consultancies (do-it-for-you; never worked in the model; millions spent, still stuck 3 years later) — **coaching > consulting** (build skills in-house); (2) "agile = transformation" (only the easy third). 1–3 years for big companies; use pilot teams.
- Case studies: Kaiser Permanente, Almosafer (Saudi), Trainline (biggest UK IPO at the time). Product model in government applies but is harder (stakeholders).
