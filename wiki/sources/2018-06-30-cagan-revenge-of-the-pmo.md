## Notes for the coach
- **Agile's two essences:** (1) small, frequent releases; (2) empower the team to figure out how to solve the problem. The *second* is where the politics ignite — stakeholders resist handing over responsibility, so most teams "use Agile" but still get roadmaps and remain unempowered (delivery teams).
- **The PMO** (Program Management Office) was sidelined by Agile and spent a decade seeking a way back — returning via "Agile at Scale," most heavily marketed as **SAFe**.
- **SAFe critique:** no leading tech product company uses it; all examples are big IT/project-mindset orgs (banks, insurers). "Embrace and extend" marketing that redefines terms (Epic = "mini business case"; "lean governance"; Agile Release Trains ~every 10 weeks). All ten root causes of product failure are present. The dedicated product team is gutted; the core unit becomes a top-down "Program" (PM + architect + release-train manager decide; low-level POs build). Output not outcome; mercenary model.
- **Where it *might* be OK:** fully outsourced engineering; a big re-platforming with a settled architecture; an org without true PMs/designers and weak engineers.
- **Fitbit** tried SAFe and abandoned it (the person who pushed it left); SVPG's *Spotify vs Fitbit* covers it.
