# Cagan — Applying a Product Mindset to Internal Data Products (Experiencing Data)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan on *Experiencing Data* with Brian T. O'Neill (ep. 061) · published 2021-03-23 — [watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9eU6P0u8yrk). Slim provenance card; transcript **not reproduced**.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — root source for [[data-product-management]].** Untangles "data to decide" vs "data to power products," the "is it a product?" litmus test, customer-facing vs customer-enabling vs platform teams, the platform PM, and where ethics sits.

## What it backs
- concepts: [[data-product-management]] (root), [[team-topology]] (facing / enabling / platform; "most of Amazon is enabling teams")
- competencies: [[product-manager]] (value + viability; PM ≠ designer; "you are never your customer"), [[product-ops]] (data analysts as force multiplier)
- concepts: [[the-four-big-risks]] (ethics originates with leaders, first manifests in the team; chief ethics officer / trust & safety)

## Notes for the coach
- **Litmus test:** "if it goes down, does it immediately impact your customer's experience?" → product (even if internal).
- **Pet peeve:** don't confuse internal employees (sales, CS) with customers.
- **AI/ML PM:** the principles are the same across consumer / B2B / native / ML; a good PM in one would be good in all.
- Empowered (the book) was written *for leaders* because leaders were the blocker.
