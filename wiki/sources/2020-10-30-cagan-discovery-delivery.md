# Cagan — Discovery: Delivery

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Discovery – Delivery" · svpg.com, 2020-10-30 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/discovery-delivery/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Tackles the damaging anti-pattern where a single product team devolves into a *discovery team* and a *delivery team*. The canon: one cross-functional product team owns both discovery and delivery continuously. Distinguishes the *time-split-by-role* reality (PM/designer mostly discovery, engineers mostly delivery) from the *team-split* anti-pattern (different *people* doing each). Calls out outsourced engineering and "the PM defines requirements and hands off to designers/engineers" as canonical examples.

## Notes for the coach
- **The anti-pattern in plain English:** one group obtains the learnings, another group executes the build. The receiving group inevitably feels like mercenaries and won't surface better alternatives. This is the same failure mode as outsourced engineering and corporate innovation labs.
- **PMs as solution-definers.** "The product manager believes it's her job to define the product, and then hand-off 'the requirements' to the designer and engineers to implement." Most common manifestation in feature teams. Flag explicitly when a PM describes the role this way.
- **Use this to debunk "two-track" misreadings of Torres/dual-track.** Dual-track discovery is about activities happening in parallel within one team, not two teams.
- **Strong founder/head-of-product exception is still bad.** Even when decisions are correct, the *very high price* in motivation, ownership, and innovation is still paid — Cagan is explicit.
