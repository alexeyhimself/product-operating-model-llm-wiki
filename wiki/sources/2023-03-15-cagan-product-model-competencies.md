# Cagan — Product Model Competencies

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Model Competencies" · svpg.com, 2023-03-15 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-model-competencies/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Companion overview to [[2024-01-17-cagan-product-model-concepts]]. Introduces the **four critical competencies** that the product model requires: [[product-manager]], [[product-designer]], engineers/Tech Lead ([[engineers]]), and [[product-leadership]]. Written for non-experts ("understandable by anyone in your company, from CEO to a sales person") to help readers judge competence and avoid the title-without-skills trap. Cagan-flag: *"if your transformation fails, it will very likely be because you did not establish these competencies."* Also names "other impacted roles" — product ops (user research + data analysis), product marketing, project/delivery management.

## Notes for the coach
- **The risk-ownership table** here is the cleanest source you can cite when assigning accountabilities in the trio:
  - PM → value + viability → outcomes
  - Designer → usability → experience
  - Tech Lead → feasibility → delivery
- **"Adopted the title without the competency" is more severe than it sounds.** Cagan-quote: *"We want to give you the tools to be able to effectively judge competence."* Useful when a user reports being told their org "already has" PMs and designers.
- **Coachable vs. unwilling.** Many existing people can be coached into these competencies but not all. Routes a transformation user toward either coaching or right-staffing — pair with [[pm-competency]] for the diagnosis.
- **Other impacted roles** (product ops, product marketing, delivery management) are not the trio but are significantly affected — useful when an org-design discussion comes up.
