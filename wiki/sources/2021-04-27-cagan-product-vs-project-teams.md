# Cagan — Product vs. Project Teams

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product vs. Project Teams" · svpg.com, 2021-04-27 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-vs-project-teams/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's clarification of the two distinct contrasts often confused: **product vs. feature** teams (about *empowerment* — given problems vs. given features) and **product vs. project** teams (about *ownership* — durable team owning outcomes vs. pool model dispersed after delivery). Names the project team as "the pool model" and dismantles the myth that pool models are more efficient. Identifies CFO + CIO as the root of project-team culture (technology as a cost center) and points to ThoughtWorks/Fowler's *Products over Projects* essay as supporting material.

## Notes for the coach
- **Two axes to remember:** product-vs-feature is about **empowerment**; product-vs-project is about **ownership/durability**. Useful when a user says "we're a product team" but reorganizes every project.
- **"Pool model" tell.** If engineers are reassigned per project (1–2 months) and never see the result, the team is a project team regardless of titles.
- **Durability rule of thumb:** product teams are durable — usually 1–2 years minimum. (Cagan, citing lean-startup lingo.)
- **Root cause flag.** When the root issue is CFO/CIO budgeting by project, transformation requires reaching outside product/technology. Pair with [[2022-09-23-moore-changing-how-you-decide-which-problems-to-solve]] and the leadership gap argument.
