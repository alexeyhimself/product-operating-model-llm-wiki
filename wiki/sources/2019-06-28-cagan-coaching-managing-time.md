# Cagan — Coaching: Managing Time

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Coaching — Managing Time" · svpg.com, 2019-06-28 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-managing-time/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The **four-hours-a-day** rule: a PM needs ~4 solid daily hours for product-discovery work (not email/meetings/slack). Most PMs spend their day on *project* management masquerading as product management. The canonical Cagan case for hiring a **delivery manager** to absorb project-management work so the PM can do their actual job.

## Notes for the coach
- **The four-hours rule** is concrete and observable; if a PM can't carve out four daily hours for discovery, they either work nights or fail.
- **The PM/delivery-manager partnership** is the canon answer; calls out PMs who resist handing off project work.
- Caveats: in a true small startup, project burden is small and falls naturally to the PM; in a stakeholder-driven feature-team org, the article doesn't apply (and is a sign the role is mislabeled).
