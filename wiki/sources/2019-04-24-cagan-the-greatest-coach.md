> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Greatest Coach" · svpg.com, 2019-04-24 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-greatest-coach/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's tribute to **Bill Campbell** ("The Coach of Silicon Valley" — coached Steve Jobs, Larry & Sergey, Jeff Bezos) and grounds the [[trillion-dollar-coach|*Trillion Dollar Coach*]] recommendation. Carries several load-bearing Campbell quotes that ground SVPG's leadership-development philosophy.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet (Campbell):** "Coaching is no longer a specialty; you cannot be a good manager without being a good coach."
- **The yardstick:** "I look at all the people who've worked for me or who I've helped in some way, and I count up how many are great leaders now. That's how I measure success." — the canonical leadership-development metric.
- **"The purpose of a company is to bring a product vision to life. All the other components are in service to product."** — useful when a CEO is treating product as a function rather than the company's reason for existing.
- The "different cultures, same product fundamentals" observation (Apple, Amazon, Google) is the foundation of [[model-maturity]]'s Level-4 frame.
