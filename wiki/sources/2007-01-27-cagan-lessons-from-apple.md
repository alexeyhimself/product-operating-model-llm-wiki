# Cagan — Lessons from Apple

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Lessons from Apple" · svpg.com, 2007-01-27 (written at the iPhone's debut) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/lessons-from-apple/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** An early Cagan classic: why Apple can repeatedly redefine consumer markets (PCs, music players, phones) — and why it is *not* marketing prowess. Four higher-order lessons. Grounds the Jobs-era half of the [[apple-role-of-product]] case study.

- competencies: [[product-designer]] (usability, interaction, visual, industrial design "front and center")

## Notes for the coach
- **The four lessons (the chain):** (1) *The hardware serves the software*; (2) *the software serves the user experience*; (3) *the user experience serves the emotion* — "they understand better than anyone the role emotion plays"; (4) *the product serves a real need* — Apple is "rarely first to market" but speaks to a real, unmet need.
- **"Value" is the through-line.** Apple's hallmark is so much value the premium price is a non-issue — the value framing that recurs in [[the-four-big-risks]] and the 2015 [[2015-08-03-cagan-the-role-of-product-at-apple|Role of Product at Apple]] critique ("I just couldn't find the value").
- **Copiers miss the point** — competitors (Zune) copy functionality, not what matters (UX → emotion → real need). Useful against feature-parity thinking.
- **Pairing:** the optimistic Jobs-era bookend to the cautionary 2015 succession piece — together they are the Apple case arc.
