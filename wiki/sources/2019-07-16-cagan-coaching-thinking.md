# Cagan — Coaching: Thinking

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Coaching — Thinking" · svpg.com, 2019-07-16 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-thinking/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Distinguishes **intelligence** (raw capacity) from **thinking** (applied problem-solving). Product management *is* problem-solving — solving simultaneously for value/usability/feasibility/viability. The written narrative is the favorite tool for developing thinking; resistance to it is the tell that a PM isn't yet thinking through hard problems.

## Notes for the coach
- **"Product management is problem solving."** Single most useful framing in the article.
- **Acquiring knowledge vs. applying it.** Google made knowledge easy; thinking is the differentiator.
- **Interview test:** how does the candidate handle *not knowing* the answer? Not whether they know it.
- **For PMs who can't develop thinking skill:** Cagan recommends Dweck's *Mindset*; sometimes thinking-skill gap is the signal that someone isn't cut out for PM.
