# Cagan — Product vs Feature Teams

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product vs Feature Teams" · svpg.com, 2019-08-29 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-vs-feature-teams/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's most explicit framing of the three team types — delivery teams (rebranded waterfall), feature teams (cross-functional but given roadmaps), and empowered product teams (given problems and outcomes). Establishes the **risk-ownership** model on each side: in empowered teams PM owns value+viability, designer owns usability, Tech Lead owns feasibility; in feature teams, value and viability sit with the stakeholder who requested the feature. Closes with seven indicator questions to place a team on the spectrum.

## Notes for the coach
- **The "completely different job" point:** PM-of-empowered-team and PM-of-feature-team "probably should not even be same job title." Cite this when a user thinks they can adopt empowerment without a PM upgrade.
- **Seven indicator questions** (verbatim): roadmaps vs. problems; PM/designer role confusion; PM/delivery-manager role confusion; PM mostly doing project management; OKR mashup; missionaries vs. mercenaries; level of accountability.
- **Cagan's honesty:** even strong product companies have some feature teams — usually where leadership doesn't yet trust the team, or wants to dictate solutions.
