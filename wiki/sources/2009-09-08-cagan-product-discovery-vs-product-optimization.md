# Cagan — Product Discovery vs. Product Optimization

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Discovery vs. Product Optimization" · svpg.com, 2009-09-08 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-discovery-vs-product-optimization/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Distinguishes **product discovery** (for significant new functionality — qualitative, iterative, prototype-based, hours/days) from **product optimization** (for live products — A/B-test driven, analytics-grounded, weeks). Both required; both have different toolkits; one is not a substitute for the other.

## Notes for the coach
- **Instrumentation is non-negotiable.** Cagan: when an org isn't instrumented, "it's really the top priority to correct this." Use as the immediate next-step when discovery is being attempted without analytics.
- **Discovery iterations are daily; optimization iterations are biweekly to monthly.** Different rhythms.
- **Trap:** teams that learn A/B testing and try to use it as discovery — usually move too slowly and only see incremental improvements.
