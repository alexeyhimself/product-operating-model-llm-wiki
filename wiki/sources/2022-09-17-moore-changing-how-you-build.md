# Moore — Changing How You Build

> **Citation:** Jon Moore (SVPG) · "Changing How You Build" · svpg.com, 2022-09-17 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/changing-how-you-build/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** First of a three-part SVPG sequence defining transformation: changing how you **build**, how you **solve problems**, and how you **decide which problems to solve**. This first article makes the case for small, frequent, reliable releases — the *Build* dimension of the [[svpg-product-operating-model-introduction|SVPG Introduction]]'s three dimensions. Frames three foundational principles for how strong product companies build: (1) protecting customers/revenue/brand/colleagues, (2) responding to market needs, (3) earning customer trust. Argues that the technique that addresses all three is small, frequent, reliable releases (CI/CD). Distinguishes real CI/CD from cargo-culted Agile.

## Notes for the coach
- **Three principles, one technique.** Pair these three principles (protect, respond, earn trust) with the [[principles-of-product-delivery|four product delivery principles]]; the principles in this article are the *purpose*, the delivery principles are the *mechanism*.
- **Releasing yearly/quarterly/monthly = not Agile.** Cagan-line quote to flag when someone calls a quarterly-release org "Agile": "you are *not* Agile in any meaningful sense, you are not getting the necessary benefits, and you will not be able to serve your customers or your business as you need to."
- **Recommended reading from the source:** [[accelerate]] (Forsgren/Humble/Kim) — for the theory and evidence on why smaller/faster releases yield both higher throughput *and* higher quality.
- **Beware fake-Agile.** The article links to *Revenge of the PMO* and Steve Denning's "fake Agile" piece; useful pointers if the user is in an org with heavy "Agile" ritual but no real delivery cadence.
