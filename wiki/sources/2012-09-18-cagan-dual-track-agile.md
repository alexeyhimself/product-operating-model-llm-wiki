# Cagan — Dual-Track Agile

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Dual-Track Agile" · svpg.com, 2012-09-18 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/dual-track-agile/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The original Dual-Track Scrum / Dual-Track Agile framing (later renamed continuous discovery / continuous delivery by Cagan because the *Agile* phrasing made people focus on process not principles). Establishes the parallel-tracks pattern with the trio collaborating in discovery and the delivery track producing releasable software.

## Notes for the coach
- **Cagan's own update:** the term "Dual-Track Agile" was abandoned because it anchored people on process. Use [[2012-10-24-cagan-continuous-discovery|continuous discovery / continuous delivery]] in current conversation.
- **"Mini-waterfalls within Scrum"** is Cagan's term for the common failure pattern — PM does "requirements," designer hands off wireframes, engineers build. Dual-track demands collaboration, not handoff.
- **Lean UX and Dual-Track are "made for each other"** — prototypes serve as validation instrument and as spec for delivery.
