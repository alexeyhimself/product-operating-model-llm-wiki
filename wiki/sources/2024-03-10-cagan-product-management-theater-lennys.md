# Cagan — Product Management Theater (Lenny's Podcast)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, interviewed by Lenny Rachitsky · Lenny's Podcast · YouTube, 2024-03-10 (the *TRANSFORMED* launch interview) — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9N4ZgNaWvI0). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Notes for the coach
- **Theater defined:** title without the job or skills; "dramatically overpaid for the value they provide"; it's a project-management role. Convergent causes: pandemic over-hiring + lowered bar; higher cost of funds; predicted GenAI impact; role bloat (agile coaches, product owners, product ops, business analysts, scrum masters, APMs); SAFe "depressingly popular" outside SV. Ref *Epic Waste*.
- **PM = creator, not facilitator.** "My job is to say why" is ridiculous (the why comes from product strategy). Skills: expert on users/customers (Cagan's coach made him visit 30 customers before taking the PM role), expert on data, owner of compliance/sales/marketing/finance/legal/GTM knowledge. Missing this person → design by committee.
- **Top-down vs bottom-up (Meta/Zuck):** good companies aren't "top-down"; leaders own strategy (focus, bets), teams own solutions. Empowerment ≠ teams picking what to work on (= anarchy).
- **Product ops ≠ product operating model** (name conflict). Fine when it's user research + data analysts under one leader; red flag when it's "process & governance" → run; anti-pattern when it exists to do the PM's or the product leader's job.
- **AI disruption:** backlog administrators are a poor bet; **viability becomes *more* important** (probabilistic vs deterministic software; legal/ethical/mission-critical). Value + viability land more heavily on the PM.
- **TRANSFORMED:** written for non-product execs too; all case studies from *outside* Silicon Valley (Trainline UK, Almosafer Saudi Arabia, Brazil, healthcare/car-sales/fitness). Naming: rejected "product-led"/"product-driven" (reads as a power grab); "product operating model" chosen because it's a model, not a process.
