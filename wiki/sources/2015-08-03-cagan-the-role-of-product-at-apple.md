# Cagan — The Role of Product at Apple

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Role of Product at Apple" · svpg.com, 2015-08-03 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-role-of-product-at-apple/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon), cautionary.** Using the (in Cagan's view sub-par) first Apple Watch as the trigger, Cagan asks what happens when a company **loses its singular product leader** (Steve Jobs) and must **re-establish the product competency** in another form. Grounds the second half of the [[apple-role-of-product]] case study.

## Notes for the coach
- **"The CEO as Head of Product."** Cagan's model for the visionary-founder case: Jobs *was* the effective head of product; product-marketing managers took products to market. He warns this model "could not scale to most companies" and depends on succession planning most companies neglect.
- **The three options** to re-establish product competency post-Jobs: (1) find another superstar product leader (rare); (2) hire/promote product rock-stars per product line (cultural-acceptance risk vs strong eng/design leaders); (3) **introduce the true product role** across all critical products — "the most scalable long-term," contingent on leadership giving PMs "a seat at the table."
- **The true product role ≠ "fill the white space."** Cagan pushes back on the "do whatever needs to be done to get the product out" definition of PM: admirable in startups, but it "misses the primary point of the product role — to ensure the team is building a product that has the necessary **value**."
- **Tone:** unusually critical of a specific shipped product (Apple Watch v1) — a rare cautionary/exemplar-with-caveats piece; pair with the celebratory [[2007-01-27-cagan-lessons-from-apple|Lessons from Apple (2007)]] for the full Apple arc.
