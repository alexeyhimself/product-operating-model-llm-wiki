# Cagan — Beyond Usability

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Beyond Usability" · svpg.com, 2011-01-06 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/beyond-usability/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Argues UX is much more than usability — and "usability testing" is the wrong vocabulary because it narrows the activity. The actual test is **value + usability**: would the user even *want* to use it? And the often-undersold third leg: **visual design** as emotional persuasion (trust, fear-assuagement, willingness to share personal info).

## Notes for the coach
- **Reframe "usability testing" → "prototype testing"** — assesses value first, usability second.
- **Visual design as emotional persuasion** — useful when execs treat visual as "make it pretty."
- **"Quite a few products succeed in spite of pretty awful usability"** — usability is necessary but not sufficient; the big risk is value.
