# Cagan — Product Lessons from Steve Jobs and Elon Musk (20VC #957)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan on *20VC* with Harry Stebbings, ep. #957 · published 2022-12-07 — [watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xC9pgmpfbMw). Slim provenance card; transcript **not reproduced**.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — a root source for [[hiring-and-onboarding]].** The fullest account of the first-product-hire heuristic, hire-for-potential, domain-expertise-vs-dogma, and Cagan's own 30-customer onboarding. Also the clearest "**primary vs secondary risk**" framing for founders.

## What it backs
- frameworks: [[hiring-and-onboarding]] (root: ~25-engineer trigger; potential vs experience; 3 months to competence; the 30-customer story)
- concepts: [[the-four-big-risks]] (primary vs secondary risk; value is usually the hardest), [[product-discovery]] (Elon Musk "find what's wrong," not affirmation; 50–100 iterations), [[product-sense]] (domain dogma; Shreyas Doshi)
- competencies: [[empowered-engineers]] ("using engineers only to code = half their value"; outsourcing = "you're not serious"; Bill Campbell)
- concepts: [[principles-over-process]] (operator PLs: scale-with-process vs scale-with-coaching)

## Notes for the coach
- **Primary vs secondary risk:** founders gravitate to comfortable risks (business model); the primary risk is a *valuable* product. "Unless the product is right, you've got nothing."
- **Sales alignment:** "responsibility for the sales is on the product team" (Andy Grove's original intent for OKRs).
- Netscape/eBay lineage: Andreessen's "know what you can't know" = the essence of discovery.
