# Cagan & Castro — Outcomes Are Hard

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan & Felipe Castro (OKR/outcomes coach) · "Outcomes Are Hard" · svpg.com, 2025-03-17 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/outcomes-are-hard/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The clearest statement that **moving to outcomes is a *consequence* of moving to the product model** — not a thing you can bolt on. The most common mistake (which "fueled the backlash to OKRs"): trying to adopt outcomes/OKRs on top of an output-based roadmap process. Also: don't let existing metrics dictate problems; define a **clear problem before the metric**; **KPIs ≠ outcomes** (the gas-gauge-vs-MPG analogy); outcomes require **telemetry**; and the **product outcomes → business outcomes → impact** framing (crediting Jeff Patton and Teresa Torres).

## Notes for the coach
- **KPIs vs outcomes:** most of a business's hundreds of KPIs are *not* good outcome measures; pick the KPI(s) that truly measure the problem being solved — define new ones if needed.
- **Amazon "inputs not outputs" ≠ the output-vs-outcome distinction.** Amazon means manage the *causes* (selection, price, convenience) not the *effects* (orders, revenue) — a product-strategy point, not a contradiction of "outcomes over output."
- **Not everything is an outcome:** KTLO / punch-list work still exists.
