# Cagan — From Projects to Products

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "From Projects to Products" · svpg.com, 2023-08-17 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/from-projects-to-products/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's culture article making the project→product shift concrete via a "before and after." Argues that the project model is built around dates, output, and dispersed ownership; the product model is built around durable teams, outcomes, and time-to-money. Linked from [[2024-01-17-cagan-product-model-concepts]] as the canonical [[product-culture]] companion. Coins/clarifies the **time-to-market vs. time-to-money** distinction.

## Notes for the coach
- **The "five things wrong with projects"** (no iteration funding, no ownership, no innovation slot, fast tech-debt accumulation, dispersed accountability) is a useful checklist to walk a user through if they suspect they're still in the project model.
- **Time-to-money is the dominant metric** for product teams; time-to-market still matters and high-integrity commitments are the technique for when a date is the priority. Don't let users hear "outcomes over output" as "dates never matter."
- **Cagan's reflection:** "you might think that a time-to-market *project* at least would get done *faster* than a time-to-money *product* team would achieve an outcome. But so often it is just the opposite." Useful when a CFO or stakeholder defends projects on speed grounds.
