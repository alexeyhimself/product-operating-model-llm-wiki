# Cagan — Product Coaching and AI

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Coaching and AI" · svpg.com, 2026-02-04 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-coaching-and-ai/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's explicit reframing of how PMs learn the product model. Reverses two decades of "your manager should coach you" with a new prescription: foundation models (Claude / Gemini / GPT), configured with **project files + project instructions + strategic context**, can serve as a personal product coach. Not a replacement for strong human coaches (still preferred), but the scalable answer for the vast majority of PMs whose managers can't or won't coach. **This wiki is one implementation of the prescription** — the project files are the wiki body, the project instructions are [`CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md).

## Notes for the coach
- **Significant SVPG position shift** — flagged in the article's first line: "this article represents a significant change to what we have been advocating for the past two decades." Surface this when discussing coaching: the prior canon was human coaching by your manager; the new canon is model-as-coach for most PMs.
- **Cagan still prefers strong human coaches** for those who have access. The article's framing: model-as-coach is not as good as a *strong* human coach, but it is "at least as good as most managers" and infinitely more scalable.
- **For product leaders** (especially during transformation), the recommended setup is model-as-coach *plus* a strong human leadership coach. Don't position model-as-coach as the full answer at leadership scale.
- **PM theater anti-pattern.** Using AI to *accelerate the project model* (aggregate feedback, generate PRDs, write user stories) surfaces the theater rather than upskilling. See [[product-management-theater]].
- **Caveats to embed in any coaching session this wiki powers:** foundation models are non-deterministic (answers vary day-to-day); the user should question outputs and seek *critique* over affirmation; the model must be explicitly told to prioritize the **product** operating model (vs project model) — otherwise it averages across competing voices and "appears confused."
- **Cagan's reference setup:** project files + project instructions, with `svpg.com/examples` (footnote 4) as his reference. This wiki maps directly: wiki body = project files, `CLAUDE.md` = project instructions; user adds their own strategic context as a field note.
- **Footnote 3** flags Anthropic Skills as a likely "next step forward" — relevant to how this wiki may be packaged and distributed later.
