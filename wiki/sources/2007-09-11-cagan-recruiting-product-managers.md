# Cagan — Recruiting Product Managers

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Recruiting Product Managers" · svpg.com, 2007-09-11 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/recruiting-product-managers/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's canonical answer to "where do we find PMs?" — the **traits-and-skills checklist**. Personal traits (product passion, customer empathy, intelligence, work ethic, integrity, confidence, communication skills, attitude) + skills (applying technology, focus, time management, written/presentation skills, business skills). Traits matter most — skills can be learned.

## Notes for the coach
- **"They're probably already in your company"** — engineering/design/SE candidates frequently outperform external hires.
- **MBA caveat:** business schools rarely teach product management — don't assume MBA = PM.
- **"As CEO of the product"** — the canonical attitude line.
- The 2007 timestamp is important; this is one of the earliest formal articulations of the PM traits/skills taxonomy that becomes [[pm-competency]].
