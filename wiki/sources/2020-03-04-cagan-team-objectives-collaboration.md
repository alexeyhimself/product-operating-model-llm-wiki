# Cagan — Team Objectives: Collaboration

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Team Objectives – Collaboration" · svpg.com, 2020-03-04 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/team-objectives-collaboration/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here. The ninth part of the Team Objectives series.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The forms of collaboration that autonomy-optimizing orgs get confused about. Distinguishes **shared objectives** (multiple teams pursuing *the same objective together* — co-dependent, e.g. an experience team + a platform team joined by a simple **API "contract"**; or teams temporarily combined, sometimes in a co-located **"swarm"**) from **common objectives** (multiple teams attacking *the same problem in different ways* — largely independent and cumulative, done for **risk management**). Names the **product attribution problem** (isolating one team's contribution when several change things in parallel) and its standard answer: the **A/B test**.

## Notes for the coach
- **Shared vs common — the crisp distinction.** *Shared* = same objective, teams work **together** (co-dependent; API contract or swarm). *Common* = same problem, teams work **separately** in different ways (independent, cumulative; risk management).
- **Swarm** — an intense, co-located, few-days/week collaboration to dive deep on discovery and/or delivery for a hard problem.
- **Product attribution problem** — when multiple teams change things at once, use an **A/B test** to isolate each team's impact (which sometimes helps, sometimes hurts, sometimes does nothing).
- **Coaching cue:** avoiding shared/common objectives "in the name of autonomy or communication" limits an org's ability to solve its toughest, most important problems.
