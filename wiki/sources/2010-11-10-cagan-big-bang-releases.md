# Cagan — Big Bang Releases

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Big Bang Releases" · svpg.com, 2010-11-10 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/big-bang-releases/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Defines the big-bang release anti-pattern (months of work shipped in one event) and its three drivers: marketing wanting a launch event, dates driven by execs, or governance/review processes that force waterfall sequencing. Argues separating **marketing launch** from **product release** — push incrementally and dark even when marketing wants a splash.

## Notes for the coach
- **Quote magnet:** "Don't confuse delivery dates with big bang process."
- **Scrum doesn't save you.** Big-bang can happen inside Scrum if releases are batched at quarter ends.
- **Exec reviews can be navigated** — bring prototypes and user feedback, still ship incrementally.
