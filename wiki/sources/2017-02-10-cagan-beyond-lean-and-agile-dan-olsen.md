# Cagan — Product Management: Beyond Lean and Agile (Lean Product Meetup)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, talk at the Lean Product & Lean UX Meetup (Dan Olsen), delivered 2017-01-24 · YouTube, published 2017-02-10 — [watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7LYLhxb8Vwc). Slim provenance card; transcript **not reproduced**.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** An early, foundational articulation of what became the product model — Cagan's **three themes "beyond lean and agile":** (1) tackle the four risks before building, (2) side-by-side product/design/eng collaboration (not handoffs), (3) focus on outcomes/problems, not roadmaps/output.

## What it backs
- concepts: [[product-discovery]] (dual-track — Jeff Patton — framed across "styles": scrum, lean, customer development, design thinking, YC "build things that don't scale," Google "fake it before you make it"), [[the-four-big-risks]] (value/usability/feasibility/business), [[outcome-based-roadmap]] (roadmaps are output; give teams problems)
- competencies: [[the-product-team-trio]] (collaboration + co-location), [[product-manager]] ("CEO of the product"; ~4 hrs/day of discovery; the group PM as player-coach), [[product-marketing]] (a role to take *off* the PM's plate)
- concepts: [[build-to-learn-vs-build-to-earn]] (MVPs are experiments; reference-customer program)

## Notes for the coach
- **SVPG Manifesto:** never tie yourself too closely to one methodology.
- **Reference customers** = his favorite leading indicator of future success (B2B: ~6 live, referenceable). "Prototype as spec."
