# Cagan — Product Vision FAQ

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Vision FAQ" · svpg.com, 2020-08-10 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-vision-faq/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's question-by-question answer to the most common confusions about product vision: timeframe, scope (single team vs. unit vs. company), ownership (head of product, not delegated), level of detail, how to craft one (the 1-2 day facilitated offsite with the right preparation), refresh cadence, when to abandon (the *vision pivot*), and examples. This is the most concrete operational guidance Cagan has published on vision.

## Notes for the coach
- **Canonical timeframes:** software companies 2–5 years; device companies 5–10 years. Common mistake: a 1-year "vision" that is really a roadmap.
- **The 9 factors Cagan lists for vision-crafting** (business objectives & constraints, customer problems, critical insights, enabling technologies, industry trends, competitive landscape, GTM considerations, own capabilities, organizational impacts). Use as a checklist for vision audits.
- **Ownership rule (canon):** the vision is the *explicit* responsibility of the head of product (or a co-founder in startups). It is not delegated to ICs or individual product teams. The CEO and other leaders must feel ownership too — they'll evangelize it to investors, board, customers.
- **Vision pivot vs. discovery pivot.** Cagan distinguishes them — a *vision pivot* is usually a *good* thing (you found a larger opportunity) but is rare. Far more common: abandoning a vision too early (6–12 months) usually because the team lacks the discovery skills to make progress.
- **The "underpants gnomes"-style empty vision.** "We will make customers' lives better through automation" — flagged as the canonical vision anti-pattern.
- **"Vision sprint" agencies** exist but Cagan suggests a [[product-coach|product leadership coach]] as a first alternative.
