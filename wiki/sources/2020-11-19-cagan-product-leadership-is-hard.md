# Cagan — Product Leadership Is Hard

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Leadership Is Hard" · svpg.com, 2020-11-19 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-leadership-is-hard/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's high-level overview of the product leadership role — the long-form version is the book [[empowered|EMPOWERED]]. Splits leadership into **management responsibilities** (coaching + staffing) and **leadership responsibilities** (providing strategic context — product vision and principles, team topology, product strategy, team objectives, ongoing evangelism). Anchors the canon: empowered teams require *better* leadership, not less. Quotes Bill Campbell ("coaching is no longer a specialty; *you cannot be a good manager without being a good coach*"), Andy Grove, Jeff Bezos, and Netflix ("lead by context, not control").

## Notes for the coach
- **Empowered teams need *better* leadership, not less** — Cagan's headline correction. Use when a leader interprets "empowerment" as backing off.
- **Coaching is ~50% of a first-level manager's week** (canon). Useful expectation-setter for new product leaders surprised by the load.
- **The five elements of strategic context** product leaders own: vision + principles, team topology, product strategy, team objectives, ongoing evangelism. Use as a leadership self-assessment.
- **Topology rule of thumb:** "loosely coupled but highly aligned." Decision authority on topology sits with head of product + head of technology together. Big lever, easy to get wrong.
- **Litmus test for empowerment.** "The product team is able to decide the best way to solve the problems they have been assigned." If leaders are still dictating *how*, empowerment is theater.
- **Long-form reference:** when the user wants to go deeper, point to [[empowered]] — Cagan and Chris Jones, 400+ pages on exactly this.
- **Evangelism is never done.** A useful framing for senior leaders who treat the vision/strategy as "communicated" after a single town hall.
