# Cagan — Transformation as a Project

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Transformation as a Project" · svpg.com, 2024-10-29 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/transformation-as-a-project/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan names a specific transformation anti-pattern: **running the transformation effort itself as a big project** — program managers, RACI models, work streams, dependencies, status reporting, success = "completed the project." The irony: orgs trying to move from project to product orientation manage the transformation in the very mode they're trying to abandon. Cagan's prescription: **use the product model to transform to the product model** — identify the major risks of transformation, then create small prototypes ([[pilot-teams|pilot teams]]) to tackle them.

## Notes for the coach
- **The fork-in-the-road quote:** "as with products, success starts with *knowing what you can't know, and admitting what you don't know*." Captures why a project-style transformation fails: the org assumes it knows what to do; the necessary learning is still in front of them.
- **"It's one thing to say… and quite another to see"** — three specific gotchas Cagan names: (1) upgrading product owners to true PMs is more than a title change; (2) making engineers first-class members changes the dynamics, especially the source of best ideas; (3) saying leaders have new responsibilities ≠ learning which leaders can actually do them.
- **Time perception** — orgs fear pilot teams will take longer to transform than a big-bang project rollout; in practice (per Cagan) prototype-then-scale is "substantially faster" because of time-to-money, the same as for products.
- **Cite this when** the user describes their transformation as "we have a transformation program with workstreams" or "the transformation will be complete by Q4 next year." Both are the named anti-pattern.
