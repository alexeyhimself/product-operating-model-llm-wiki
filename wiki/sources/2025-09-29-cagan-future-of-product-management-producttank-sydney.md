# Cagan — The Future of Product Management (ProductTank Sydney)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, fireside with Malte · Mind the Product / ProductTank Sydney · YouTube, 2025-09-29 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=53bocxCsj2U). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The evolving PM role in the AI era: the decline of "product owner," the rise of the "product creator," and ethics as part of viability.

## What it backs
- competencies: [[product-manager]] (three PM types; product-owner decline; agency + prototyping as "superpowers")
- concepts: [[the-four-big-risks]] (ethics as part of viability; escalating ethical risk in AI), [[team-topology]] (changing team composition in the AI era), [[product-management-theater]]
- concepts: [[model-as-product-coach]] (AI-powered discovery tools)

## Notes for the coach
- **Agency is essential:** high-agency PMs and creators prototype, test, and *show* solutions rather than wait for permission.
- **Moats are built on customer understanding** — deep knowledge and trust with customers are the lasting advantage in a world where features are easily copied.
- **Ethics is part of viability:** PMs are often the first to spot ethical risks in AI and need to escalate them thoughtfully (consistent with "ethics inside viability" on [[the-four-big-risks]]).
- **"Probabilistic products" (AI-driven, learning systems)** are harder/slower to build and need new skillsets and team models — delivery still needs experienced engineers for scale/reliability/performance. Also mentions "forward-deployed engineers" for enterprise discovery.
