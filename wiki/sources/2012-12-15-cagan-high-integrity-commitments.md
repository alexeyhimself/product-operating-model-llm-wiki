# Cagan — High-Integrity Commitments

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Managing Commitments in an Agile Team" (= *High-Integrity Commitments*) · svpg.com, 2012-12-15 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/managing-commitments-in-an-agile-team/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's resolution of the **commitment tension**: stakeholders need dates and deliverables, product teams resist committing before discovery. The answer is **high-integrity commitments** — make commitments *after* enough discovery to validate value/usability/feasibility/viability, then commit with confidence. Names the delivery manager as the tracker of these commitments and dependencies.

## Notes for the coach
- **"Commitments are made too early"** — the single root-cause diagnosis.
- **The "give-and-take"** with stakeholders is the political conversation — discovery time in exchange for confident commitments later.
- The delivery manager's role here is operational — tracking commitments and dependencies, not making the call on whether to commit.
- Resolves the apparent contradiction between [[outcomes-over-output]] and the business need for dates.
