# Cagan — Coaching Tools: The Assessment

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Coaching Tools – The Assessment" · svpg.com, 2019-04-08 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-tools-the-assessment/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Part 1 of Cagan's "Coaching Tools" series — the **assessment** that precedes the [[coaching-the-pm|coaching plan]]. Defines the canonical *people / process / product* PM skills taxonomy and the **gap-analysis** technique (rate each skill on *importance* vs *current capability*, 1–10; coach the biggest gaps first). The root source for the [[pm-competency]] diagnostic and the skills half of the [[product-manager]] competency.

## Notes for the coach
- **Product first, because it's the foundation.** Cagan orders the pillars product → process → people for assessment: "Without competence in product knowledge, the rest doesn't really matter." Product knowledge is table stakes; ~2–3 months to ramp a new PM.
- **Gap analysis = importance vs capability.** Two 1–10 ratings per skill: where the PM *needs to be* (importance, set by the manager/org) and where they *currently are* (capability). The gap, weighted by importance, is where coaching goes. Level expectations rise with seniority (e.g. stakeholder management ≈ 7 importance for a PM, ≈ 9 for a senior PM).
- **Self-assessment encouraged, but not a substitute.** Cagan: a manager who relies only on the PM's self-assessment "is abdicating their responsibilities."
- **Coach the top 3 gaps first**, then move to the next. Sit down weekly to review progress.
- **The taxonomy is a customizable default**, not dogma — adjust for company culture/industry (e.g. media: call out the product↔editorial relationship explicitly).
- **Not a performance review.** Cagan is pointedly dismissive of annual reviews as a development tool — they are "more about HR compliance and pay administration." The assessment + plan is the real development mechanism.
