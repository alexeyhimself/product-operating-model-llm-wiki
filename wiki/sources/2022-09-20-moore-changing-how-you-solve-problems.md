# Moore — Changing How You Solve Problems

> **Citation:** Jon Moore (SVPG) · "Changing How You Solve Problems" · svpg.com, 2022-09-20 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/changing-how-you-solve-problems/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Second of the three-part SVPG transformation sequence — the *Solve Problems* dimension. Establishes the contrast between **feature teams** (set up to serve stakeholders, produce output, accountable for no outcome) and **empowered product teams** (given problems and desired outcomes, accountable for results, addressing all [[the-four-big-risks|four risks]]). Introduces *time-to-money* as the right metric for product teams (vs. time-to-market for projects) and grounds the *single most important source of innovation is engineers* claim ([[empowered-engineers]]). Cites the "10–20%" figure for the share of features on typical roadmaps that produce positive ROI.

## Notes for the coach
- **The "feature factory" diagnosis.** Use the "10–20% of features produce ROI" figure when a user is in a feature-team org and wonders why output isn't generating outcomes.
- **Prototype economics.** "Any idea tested in product discovery should be *at least* one order of magnitude less expensive and faster than using the engineers to build, test and deploy an actual product. In many cases two orders of magnitude less." Quote when justifying discovery investment.
- **Jobs quote ammunition.** "We don't hire all these engineers to tell them what to build; we hire them to show us what's possible." Pair with [[the-most-important-thing|empowered engineer]] discussions.
- **Iteration math.** Feature teams: 3–5 iterations × 1–2 quarters per cycle = 1–2 years to reach desired results. Empowered teams: 3–5 iterations in 1–2 weeks. Useful framing for skeptics.
- **Warning embedded in the source:** transformation will be resisted passively or actively by stakeholders losing control; some current PMs/designers/engineers won't be willing or able to take on the additional responsibility — explicit pointer to needing [[product-coaching|coaching]] and right-staffing.
