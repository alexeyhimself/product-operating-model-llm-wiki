# Cagan — Discovery: Problem vs. Solution

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Discovery – Problem vs. Solution" · svpg.com, 2020-09-04 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/discovery-problem-vs-solution/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Addresses a core misconception about [[product-discovery]] — that it's mainly *problem-space* work. Cagan argues that with technology-powered products, the breakthrough usually comes from the **solution** side (because enabling technology opens up solutions to problems people didn't know they had), and that the vast majority of product discovery time should be spent on the solution. Pushes back hard on the "PM defines the problem, engineering builds a solution" hand-off pattern; insists discovery is the intense interplay of value/usability/feasibility/viability owned collaboratively by the trio.

## Notes for the coach
- **"The vast majority of product efforts fail not because of demand, but because they can't come up with a solution good enough to get people to switch."** Direct Cagan quote — useful when a user is overinvesting in problem-validation and underinvesting in solution work.
- **The "crappy solution looks like no demand" trap.** If you can't sell it, it's much more often a solution-quality issue than a demand issue. Flag this when a user is about to kill a problem area on weak demand evidence.
- **The clean problem/solution split is the wrong model.** Don't let users associate problem with PM/design and solution with engineering. Both belong to the trio.
- **Always bring an engineer to customer visits** (Cagan's repeated advice). The engineer sees what is *just now possible*.
- **Pace yourself.** Once you start a problem, "the clock starts ticking" with management — don't burn all your time in problem space and leave none for the solution.
