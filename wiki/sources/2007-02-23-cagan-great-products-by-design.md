# Cagan — Great Products By Design

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Great Products By Design" · svpg.com, 2007-02-23 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/great-products-by-design/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon, early piece).** Ten truths behind great products — UX design is more important than engineering and harder; engineers are typically terrible UX designers; functionality and UX are intertwined; high-fidelity prototypes are the cheapest spec; the PM's job is finding the minimal possible product that meets the objective.

## Notes for the coach
- **2007 timestamp** — early articulation; many of these have become canon since.
- **"Engineers think in implementation models; users think in conceptual models."** Useful when engineers want to drive UX.
- **The high-fidelity prototype is the spec** — predates "Lean UX" framing but says the same thing.
- The 10 truths form a compact diagnostic checklist for UX-light product teams.
