# Cagan — The Two-Week Rule

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Two-Week Rule" · svpg.com, 2009-08-23 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-two-week-rule/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** A specific, enforceable rule for product discovery: **never go more than two weeks without putting your product ideas in front of real users and customers.** Carries the Steve Blank line "In a startup, no facts exist inside the building, only opinions."

## What it backs
- diagnostics: [[discovery-health]], [[feature-team-vs-empowered-team]]
- concepts: [[product-discovery]]
- principles: [[embrace-rapid-experimentation]], [[assess-product-risks]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Concrete enforcement tool.** Useful when discovery is theoretical — the two-week rule is observable and binary.
- **Manager's role:** enforce the rule and press for learnings after each session. Useful for [[leadership-readiness]] coaching.
- **Trap to flag:** teams that say "we'll show it to customers when it's more polished" — Cagan's exact failure mode.
