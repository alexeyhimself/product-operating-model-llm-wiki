# Cagan — Who Is Product Operating Model For?

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Who Is Product Operating Model For?" · svpg.com, 2023-09-12 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/who-is-product-operating-model-for/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon) — scope clarification.** Answers the most-asked transformation question: "we're not a tech company, so is the product operating model even relevant for us?" Cagan's answer is a reframing: **"tech company" doesn't refer to what the company *sells*, but to *how they believe they should power their business*.** Tesla sells cars; Netflix sells entertainment; Amazon sells everything. The product operating model is for any company that believes it should be powering its business with technology. Cagan's simple heuristic: **"if you have engineers building technology that helps to power your business, then you would benefit from the product model."**

## Notes for the coach
- **"Digital product vs. non-digital product" is the named antipattern** — legacy companies often draw artificial lines around "where they think they should apply technology." The real power is in **holistic** application across the business.
- **Examples Cagan gives of digital+physical blends** — Uber, banking apps, package tracking, menu translation, fitness tracking, telemedicine, navigation. Every one is digital + physical. The framing applies to almost any modern business.
- **Cite when** a user says "we're a [retailer / bank / airline / insurer / healthcare provider], the product model doesn't really apply to us." This is the article. The heuristic ("do you have engineers powering your business?") is usually the conversation-ender.
- **Selection-bias caveat** — Cagan acknowledges his client mix skews toward companies that already see the value. Still, the heuristic stands.
