# Cagan — The Root Causes of Product Failure (MTP San Francisco)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · keynote, Mind the Product / #mtpcon San Francisco · YouTube, published 2018-05-31 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9dccd8lihpQ). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** A holistic enumeration of the **ten root causes** of why most product efforts fail — the "old way of working" (waterfall dressed as agile) — and a closing sketch of how the best teams work. A strong spoken backbone for the failure/theater diagnostics.

## What it backs
- concepts: [[product-management-theater]], [[missionaries-vs-mercenaries]] (John Doerr origin quote), [[the-point-is-outcomes]] ("we don't get points for output, we get points for outcome")
- concepts: [[the-four-big-risks]] (the 2018 three-areas + ethics framing), [[empowered-engineers]] ("using devs only to code = ~half their value")
- diagnostics: [[model-maturity]] (the two inconvenient truths), [[outcome-vs-output-orientation]]
- frameworks: [[prototypes]], [[product-discovery]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Ten root causes:** (1) source of ideas (execs + customers = least valuable; engineers + data = best, absent from the room); (2) the fallacy of business cases (Marc Andreessen: "the most important thing is to know what you can't know"); (3) **roadmaps = the #1 cause** + the **two inconvenient truths** (≥half of roadmap items won't work; the ones that do need several iterations); (4) PM as "requirements gatherer"; (5) design as "lipstick on a pig"; (6) engineers as basement mercenaries; (7) agile delivering only ~10% of its value; (8) project (output) not product (outcome); (9) customer feedback far too late (the opposite of Lean Startup); (10) opportunity cost.
- **Named attributions:** John Doerr — "teams of missionaries not teams of mercenaries"; Jeff Bezos — "be stubborn on vision, flexible on details."
- **Ethics lineage [FLAG]:** in 2018 Cagan frames discovery around three areas (valuable / usable / feasible-incl-stakeholder-support) and floats **ethics as a separate consideration** ("should we build it?"). This *predates* the current canon's "ethics inside viability" (see [[the-four-big-risks]]). Present as lineage, not contradiction.
- Best teams: continuous discovery + delivery; ~500 discovery ideas to ship 100 with evidence; OKRs as the roadmap alternative; MVP = a test, not a product.
