> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Product Model Concepts" · svpg.com, 2024-01-17 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/product-model-concepts/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's pre-TRANSFORMED overview of the **five product model concepts**: [[product-culture|culture]], [[product-strategy|strategy]], [[empowered-product-teams|teams]], [[product-discovery|discovery]], [[product-delivery|delivery]]. Companion to [[2023-03-15-cagan-product-model-competencies|Product Model Competencies]]. Defines each concept in lay terms ("such that you understand the purpose, and you know what you can and should expect from your people") rather than as how-to material. The article closes by mapping the five concepts onto the three transformation dimensions ([[2022-09-17-moore-changing-how-you-build|build]], [[2022-09-20-moore-changing-how-you-solve-problems|solve problems]], [[2022-09-23-moore-changing-how-you-decide-which-problems-to-solve|decide which problems]]).

## Notes for the coach
- **Use this article alongside the [[svpg-product-operating-model-introduction|SVPG Introduction]]** when the user wants the "what is the product model" answer. The Introduction is structured by dimensions and principles; this article is structured by the five product concepts. Together they triangulate the model.
- **Cagan-line definitions** — each concept's paragraph here is the cleanest Cagan-voiced one-pager on that concept. Quote-friendly.
- **"Supporting concepts" callout** at the end: product culture also depends on leadership that understands empowerment vs. command-and-control; good product strategy depends on a strong product vision and effective team topology; discovery depends on understanding both problem and solution discovery (see [[2020-09-04-cagan-discovery-problem-vs-solution]]); delivery depends on the necessary infrastructure. Useful map.
- **Outputs-vs-outcomes lineage.** Cagan dates his own thread on outputs vs outcomes to 2007 (his original product-discovery article); good provenance to cite when this distinction is challenged.
