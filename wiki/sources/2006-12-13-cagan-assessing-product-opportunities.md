# Cagan — Assessing Product Opportunities

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Assessing Product Opportunities" · svpg.com, 2006-12-13 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/assessing-product-opportunities/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The canonical **ten-question opportunity assessment** — the cheapest, fastest way to decide whether to pursue a product idea. Problem, target market, market size, competitive landscape, differentiator, market window, go-to-market, success/revenue metric, critical factors, go/no-go.

## Notes for the coach
- **Question 1 (what problem) is the hardest.** Most PMs answer with feature lists; the coach should press for crisp problem statements.
- **Lightweight on purpose** — replaces MRDs/PRDs that nobody reads.
- **Use even when the CEO has decided.** Cagan: "doing this lightweight assessment is still valuable in that I become much better informed about what this product involves."
- This is the conceptual ancestor of the modern three-question opportunity-backlog entry (problem / persona / outcome) — see [[2012-07-24-cagan-the-opportunity-backlog]].
