# Cagan — The Product Scorecard

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "The Product Scorecard" · svpg.com, 2009-06-01 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/the-product-scorecard/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Defines the **product scorecard** — a small (≤ 5–6) set of prioritized KPIs that each PM and the product org use to make decisions and align with business strategy. The canonical mechanism for translating business strategy into PM accountability *before* OKRs entered Cagan's writing as the system of choice.

## Notes for the coach
- **Scorecard is the alignment artifact.** If the PM can't name 3–5 KPIs they own, the org has an alignment gap.
- **Eliminates backlog** — features that don't speak to a top KPI go off the list.
- **Cited in [[2020-01-09-cagan-coaching-strategic-context|strategic context]]** as the **company scorecard / company dashboard** — strategy management at the org level uses the same primitive.
- Cagan dismisses "Balanced Scorecard" as too heavyweight for most teams.
