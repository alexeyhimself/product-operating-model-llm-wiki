# Cagan — Epic Waste

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Epic Waste" · svpg.com, 2013-12-30 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/epic-waste/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan's claim: the *largest source of waste in technology* isn't startups or even government projects — it's **large legacy enterprises** (banks, insurers, airlines, automakers, telcos, retailers, healthcare, entertainment) running their tech as IT. Names the recurring anti-pattern stack: stakeholder-driven roadmaps, project-based funding/staffing, contractor/outsource reliance, waterfall (even when called Agile), no real product role, minimal UX. Important *because the waste hides behind "savings"* (loaded contractor cost in cheap locations); large companies have enough capital to fund several attempts and don't die fast — death is "by a thousand cuts."

## Notes for the coach
- **Cite when a stakeholder defends the status quo on cost grounds** — the loaded-cost-of-contractor framing is the most common reason orgs persist in the IT/project model. Cagan: the bigger teams + slower velocity + missed opportunities dwarf the apparent savings.
- **"Slow death by a thousand cuts"** — useful for orgs that don't feel acute pain. The cost is opportunity cost (the disruptor you didn't become), not a single failed project.
- **Cagan-line: "they often believe they're actually saving money"** — names the cognitive dissonance directly. When the user's CFO says "we're saving on engineering by outsourcing," this is the line to surface.
