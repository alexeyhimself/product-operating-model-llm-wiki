# Cagan — How to Escape the Project Trap and Agile Theatre (Scandinavian Product Podcast)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, interviewed by Afonso · Scandinavian Product Podcast · YouTube, 2024-04-14 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PIoFW97Lmcc). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** The **agile-theater** sibling of product-management theater, plus the clearest short statement of why product *leadership* is the biggest gap in transformation (Europe-heavy framing).

## Notes for the coach
- **Agile theater** = releasing monthly/quarterly; "waterfall with a marketing name of agile." Agile tackles only **1 of the 3 transformation dimensions** (how you build) — and it's the *easiest*. Cagan blames the agile-coach "industrial complex" / certification programs; Six Sigma suffered the same "process people took over" fate.
- **Product leaders = the biggest change / biggest gap.** First-level leaders spend ~80% coaching + staffing, ~20% strategy; CPO/CTO reversed (~80% strategic context: vision, strategy, team topology, objectives). Feature-team "product leader" = an agency staffing model (no vision/strategy, little coaching).
- **Strategy belongs to leaders, not teams** — rebuts the agile-coach claim that teams should own product strategy ("30 teams → 30 strategies = anarchy"). Teams own discovery + delivery strategy; product strategy is *informed by* team insights but *owned by* leaders.
- **Accountability model:** designer accountable for usability, PM for value + viability, but everyone *contributes* to value (esp. the Tech Lead). The value/designer-vs-PM tension is more common in B2C (user = buyer) than B2B (user ≠ buyer).
- **Product ops:** can be principle-*enabling* (a principal PM spreading the model — Chris Jones's example) or process-*enforcing* (governance) — ask which definition before judging.
