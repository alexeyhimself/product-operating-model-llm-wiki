## Notes for the coach
- **DevOps → SiteOps origin:** DevOps gave engineers tools to safely self-deploy (empowering; Amazon's SiteOps gatekeepers were a top source of team grief). Design ops followed. But **product isn't about the tools** ([svpg.com/tools-and-processes]) — so "product ops" became an empty *placeholder* companies filled with whatever they needed.
- **The two dark forces (the real point of the talk):**
  1. **Scaling with process vs scaling with people/leaders.** The root of most digital-transformation failure. Bezos's "Day 2" warning; Reed Hastings (*No Rules Rules*): the reflex to "add a process so this mistake never happens again" → bureaucracy that "sucks the soul out." SAFe / stage-gate = scaling with process. Good companies scale with *leaders who coach*.
  2. **Splitting the product manager's job.** Cutting the person who connects customers + business + engineers in half destroys innovation. Split off the *non-product* work (QA, project management, design) — never split value + viability. (The two-in-a-box anti-pattern.)
- **Force Multiplier caveat:** some orgs "saw the three pillars in Perri's book" but stuffed their two-in-a-box people into a big product-ops group — "looks like this on an org chart, but it's not this."
- Endorses the **principal PM/designer** for the tools-and-best-practices pillar; **do not staff it junior.**
