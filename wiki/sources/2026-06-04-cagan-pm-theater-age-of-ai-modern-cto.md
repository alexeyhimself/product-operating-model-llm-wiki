# Cagan — Product Management Theater in the Age of AI (Modern CTO)

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan, interviewed by Joel Beasley · Modern CTO Podcast · YouTube, 2026-06-04 — [watch the original](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VGKuhiSYVOg). Slim provenance card; the transcript is **not reproduced** here.

## Notes for the coach
- **Three models:** (1) agile product owner + devs = "software factory," which AI is making irrelevant ("good riddance"); (2) feature team / project model (prevalent, esp. US); (3) product model (the minority).
- **Bottleneck shift:** GenAI moves the bottleneck from engineers ("how long to build") to product ("what to build"). "AI is a tool — you can use it to speed up a bad process."
- **When the product model does NOT apply:** if you're not building with developers. "If you don't have control of the machine generating outcomes, it's just theater." Configuring Salesforce ≠ product model; *building* Salesforce = yes. The riskier/harder (hardware, devices, Neuralink), the *more* important.
- **Bill Campbell:** "the single most important thing in a product company is empowered engineers" — they know what's "just now possible." Framing matters: "give me this feature the competitor has" vs "our customers have this problem; I don't know the best way, hoping you do."
- **Future shape of teams:** average devs-per-team going *down* (smaller teams communicate better); team *scope* going *up* (end-to-end, fewer painful dependencies). Big unknown: more output vs fewer people.
- **Principles > techniques:** every ~6 months Cagan re-checks all 20 principles against new tech — the principles endure. "So much of this is learning to think — that's what coaching is for."
