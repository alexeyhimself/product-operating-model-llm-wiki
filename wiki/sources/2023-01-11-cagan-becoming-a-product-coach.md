## Notes for the coach
- **Experience is the non-negotiable prerequisite.** Cagan: "I do not see how a person can be an effective product coach without actual, relevant product experience." Listening, difficult-conversation, and growth-mindset skills help "only if built on top of a foundation of relevant experience." This is the canonical grounding for *why* a coach (human or model) needs real product craft, not just facilitation technique.
- **Coaching vs contracting is the scalability hinge.** "There is a very big difference between *doing a thing* for a client, versus *coaching them on how to do that thing*." Doing-it-for-them (fractional CPO, deliverable contracts) does not scale and does not develop the person. Directly reinforces the [[model-as-product-coach|PM-theater]] anti-pattern: a coach develops the PM; it doesn't produce the PM's artifacts for them.
- **Three types of coaching, by altitude:** discovery coaching (the foundation; most demand; for product teams), product leadership coaching (vision, topology, strategy, objectives; for heads of product/eng), transformation coaching (mindset/culture/funding/staffing change; for the CEO/GM). See [[types-of-product-coaching]].
- **Focus beats breadth.** Cagan's biggest-decision advice: pick *one* type of coaching for *one* type of client — the intersection of what you love and what you're qualified for.
- **Profession mechanics** (business models — independent / in-house / partnership / agency; marketing-yourself; subscription vs transaction pricing) are summarized on [[product-coach]] for reference, not reproduced here.
