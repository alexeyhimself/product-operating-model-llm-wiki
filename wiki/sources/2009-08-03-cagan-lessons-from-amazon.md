# Cagan — Lessons from Amazon

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Lessons from Amazon" · svpg.com, 2009-08-03 (referencing a Bezos talk) — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/lessons-from-amazon/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon).** Cagan distills four Bezos themes: **obsess over customers** (not competitors); **invent on behalf of customers** ("it's not the customer's job to invent"); **think long-term** (5–7 year payoffs); **always Day 1**. The article that seeds Amazon as an exemplar org throughout Cagan's writing.

## Notes for the coach
- **"Not the customer's job to invent"** — useful pushback against feature-request-driven roadmaps that "listen to customers."
- **Day 1 mindset** — useful for established companies losing innovation throughput.
- **Long-term thinking** — supports the [[product-vision|product vision]] horizon (3–10 years).
