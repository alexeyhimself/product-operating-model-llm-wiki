# Cagan — Avoiding Design By Committee

> **Citation:** Marty Cagan · "Avoiding Design By Committee" · svpg.com, 2008-06-03 — [read the original](https://www.svpg.com/avoiding-design-by-committee/). Slim provenance card; the article is **not reproduced** here.

## Role
**Primary (SVPG canon, early piece).** The original Cagan argument for the **three-person core team** (PM + UX lead + product-dev lead) as the only group that can innovate; every other stakeholder is *represented*, not seated, by one of the three. Establishes the prototype as the stakeholder-review artifact.

## What it backs
- competencies: [[the-product-team-trio]], [[product-manager]], [[product-designer]], [[engineers]]
- diagnostics: [[stakeholder-relationship-health]], [[discovery-health]]

## Notes for the coach
- **Three minds, not more.** Use when teams default to convening every stakeholder for every decision.
- **Each trio role represents many stakeholders** (PM = business owner/sales/marketing/legal/finance/support; UX = interaction/visual/research/content; dev-lead = arch/test/ops/security).
- **The prototype is the review artifact.** Stakeholders see and react to clickable prototypes — qualitatively different from paper specs.
- The 2008 timestamp is important: this is one of the earliest articulations of the trio that later becomes [[the-product-team-trio]].
