## Notes for the coach
- **Enabling-team model** (from *Team Topologies*, Skelton & Pais): product ops as an enabling team that keeps the environment healthy for empowered teams (the "shipyard" metaphor), not a control function.
- **Wary of "methodology police"** in discovery — echoes Cagan's "process becomes the thing." Product ops should support skill-building, analytics, and communication standards without policing.
- **When to hire:** not a checklist hire — a strategic move to fill *specific* gaps; sometimes the right move is to enable HR / data / leadership to step up instead.
- **Career path:** a good route for **senior PMs who don't want people leadership** but still want to shape the org (matches Cagan's principal-PM/rotation framing). The best product-ops people blend a product mindset with another core strength (analytics, L&D, change management).
- **Practitioner references:** *Product Operations* (Melissa Perri & Denise Tilles) — the dedicated book; Petra Wille's *Strong Product People* / *Strong Product Communities*.
