# The Point Is Outcomes

> States, in one place, the hierarchy that the rest of the wiki implies but never says outright: **the point is to move customer and/or business outcomes — to consistently innovate on behalf of customers.** Everything else — shipping, learning, experimentation, agility — is a *means*, not the end. (Captured 2026-07-02 from a coaching conversation; held as debt until a groundable source arrived, now grounded in Cagan's talks.)

## The argument
The Product Operating Model exists to produce **consistent innovation on behalf of customers, in ways that work for the business** — i.e., to move outcomes. Cagan's sharpest spoken version: **"we don't get points for output, we get points for outcome"** ([[2018-05-31-cagan-root-causes-of-product-failure-mtpcon-sf|Root Causes of Product Failure]]); an empowered team is "not done until they actually move that needle… measured not by 'we shipped' but 'yes, that worked'" ([[2026-06-04-cagan-pm-theater-age-of-ai-modern-cto|Modern CTO]]). For executives he reframes it as *[[time-to-money|time to money]]*, not time to market.

Everything else in the model subordinates to that:

- **Shipping / delivery** is a means. "Learn fast and ship safely and often" serves the outcome; the release is not the win.
- **Learning is a means, not the goal.** The "no success/failure — only *what did we learn?*" framing lives on [[learning-over-failure]], and it is scoped to **discovery experiments**: learning is the *currency* of discovery, the thing that lets the team place better bets and get to a solution that moves the needle. It is not the purpose of the work. Treating "we learned something" as the deliverable is how discovery becomes its own kind of theater.
- **Experimentation, agility, empowerment** are means too — they exist because they are the only reliable way to *consistently* reach outcomes ([[innovation-over-predictability]]).

The connective tissue was already spread across [[outcomes-over-output]], [[innovation-over-predictability]], [[learning-over-failure]], [[time-to-money]], and the [[outcome-vs-output-orientation]] diagnostic — this page just names the chain of subordination explicitly.

## Evidence
- [[2018-05-31-cagan-root-causes-of-product-failure-mtpcon-sf]] — "points for outcome, not output"; orphaned software is the cost of chasing output; opportunity cost as the biggest failure.
- [[2026-06-04-cagan-pm-theater-age-of-ai-modern-cto]] — the empowered team "isn't done until it moves the needle"; the measure is "that worked," not "we shipped."
- [[2026-05-07-cagan-deep-dive-into-product-bvssh]] — the explicit theme "why *outcomes* are challenging to sustain" (rituals ≠ the shift to outcomes).
- [[transformed]] — outcomes over output is a first principle; [[empowered-product-teams|empowered teams]] are given problems and measured on solving them.

## Counterpoints
- **Learning genuinely is the point — in discovery.** Reasonable people emphasize learning because, *within discovery*, it's the operative goal. The synthesis here isn't that learning doesn't matter; it's that learning is instrumental to the outcome, not a substitute for it. Both can be true.
- **Sometimes the date is the point.** Occasionally a committed date *is* the outcome that matters (a partnership, a regulatory deadline) — handled via [[high-integrity-commitments]]. This refines, rather than contradicts, "outcomes over output."
- **Keeping-the-lights-on work** doesn't move a headline metric but is still obligatory; not all valuable work shows up as a needle-moving outcome.

## Where this stands
Best current view: the wiki should consistently subordinate shipping and learning to outcomes when reasoning or coaching, while preserving the discovery-scoped role of learning. What would change it: a primary Cagan/SVPG source that reorders this (e.g., elevating learning as an end in itself) — none seen; the talks reinforce the outcome-primary reading.
