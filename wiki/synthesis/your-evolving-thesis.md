# The Wiki's Evolving Thesis

> This is the **wiki's own voice**, not Cagan's — a running thread through the corrections, lineage notes, and unresolved tensions the wiki has accumulated across 30+ ingests since 2026-06-14. A wiki that only ever adds pages compounds *coverage*; a wiki that also tracks where it was wrong, imprecise, or genuinely torn compounds *judgment*. This page is where that second kind of compounding lives, so an agent using the wiki can find not just "what does Cagan say" but "what has this wiki learned about how to read Cagan."

## The argument
The wiki's understanding of the product operating model hasn't been a flat accumulation of facts — it has repeatedly **sharpened through correction**: an early claim proves incomplete, a later source (often a book, once direct ingestion started) supplies the missing precision, and the wiki records both the old and new state rather than silently overwriting. This page collects that thread. It is deliberately not a single essay with one thesis; it's an index of the wiki's live tensions and resolved corrections, because that's what "evolving" actually looks like in a knowledge base that's still being built.

## The thread, chronologically

**Sources must be linked, never reproduced (2026-06-15).** The wiki's very first methodology correction: the initial ingest reproduced too much of its source explainers. User feedback led to rewriting `CLAUDE.md`'s citation discipline from scratch — `wiki/sources/` pages became slim provenance cards, and the root-source-vs-supporting-explainer distinction (attribute ideas to *where they originate*, not to whoever's article you happened to read first) became a standing rule applied to every ingest since. This shaped everything that followed more than any single content correction did.

**The four risks are three risks are four risks (2026-07-12, confirmed by direct book ingestion).** [[the-four-big-risks|The wiki's core concept]] looked stable for weeks — value, usability, feasibility, viability — until direct ingestion of *INSPIRED* revealed the lineage underneath: the 1st edition (2008) named only **three** validations (feasibility, usability, value); viability was added in the 2nd edition (2017); a 2018 talk briefly treated *ethics* as a separate fourth question before Cagan settled on **ethics inside business viability**. None of these are contradictions — they're the same idea sharpening over sixteen years of Cagan's own thinking, and the wiki now carries the lineage explicitly rather than presenting "four risks" as if it had always been four.

**Torres's "outcome" definition was mis-attributed (2026-07-12, confirmed by direct Torres ingestion).** The wiki had been citing *"a change in human behavior that drives business results"* as Teresa Torres's definition of a product outcome. Direct ingestion of *Continuous Discovery Habits* Ch 3 found that line is a quoted **epigraph from Josh Seiden**, not Torres's own words — her actual operational definition ("a metric the trio can influence") is broader and closer to Cagan's own usage than the wiki previously implied. Caught by reading the primary source directly rather than trusting an earlier secondary characterization — the same discipline the sources-as-hyperlinks rule (above) exists to enforce.

**Jeff Patton is not General Patton (2026-07-12).** A near-miss the wiki caught mid-ingest: *INSPIRED* 1st edition Ch 8, "Patton's Advice for Product Managers," quotes **General George S. Patton, Jr.** (the WWII general — "tell them what you need done and let them surprise you with their results"), not Jeff Patton the product coach who coined "build to learn vs. build to earn." An earlier ingest plan had misattributed the chapter; corrected before it propagated into the [[jeff-patton]] entity page.

**Who told which transformation story (2026-07-11, confirmed by direct book ingestion).** The wiki had attributed *TRANSFORMED*'s three first-person case studies to Hickman, Idiodi, and Moore based on the 2023 pre-launch announcement — correct on the *people*, but the specific company-to-person mapping (Adobe↔Hickman, Datasite↔Idiodi, Trainline↔Moore) wasn't confirmed until the book itself was read directly. A small correction, but a reminder that pre-launch marketing copy and the finished book don't always align in the details.

**Moore says "in parallel," Cagan says "Decide last" — both are canon, and the wiki has to pick an operational default (open tension, first flagged 2026-06-21).** Jon Moore's 2022 *Transformation Defined* recommends pursuing all three transformation dimensions together; Cagan's 2024 *Transformation Politics* recommends holding the *Decide* dimension (who chooses which problems) for last in politically constrained orgs, building trust through *Build* and *Solve* wins first. The wiki doesn't resolve this by picking a winner — both are primary SVPG canon, from different moments and different framings of the same underlying problem. The operational rule the wiki uses (Cagan's later, more politically cautious guidance, reserved for orgs with real political constraints) is a **judgment call**, stated as such on [[transformation]] and [[transformation-politics]], not a discovered fact.

**The wiki is org-agnostic — a scope correction that touched dozens of pages at once (2026-07-05).** The most structurally significant correction to date. Early pages occasionally drifted toward holding a specific organization's field notes and assessments. User direction clarified the boundary sharply: this wiki teaches *about* [[strategic-context|an org's strategic-context baseline]] — it never stores one. `case-studies/` are SVPG-published or public exemplars only; "your org" pages are explicitly out of scope. This wasn't a factual correction so much as an architectural one, and it's the rule every ingest since has been checked against.

**Patient debt beats premature synthesis (2026-07-02 → 2026-07-03).** When a coaching conversation surfaced a genuinely interesting connection — that "outcomes are the point, and learning is a means, not the goal" — the wiki explicitly declined to build [[the-point-is-outcomes|a synthesis page]] from that conversation alone, on the grounds that it would be wiki-synthesis masquerading as canon. It sat as flagged debt in `backlog.md` for a matter of days until the Video Ingest supplied primary sources that actually said the thing directly, and only then was the page built, grounded in citation rather than in the coaching conversation that first noticed the pattern. The discipline this models: a good idea earns a page when a primary source backs it, not when it's simply been said out loud.

**Product-leader-driven product management, and the Apple story completing itself (2026-07-12).** Apple's page told an open question for months — Cagan's 2015 piece asked how Apple would re-establish product competency after Jobs, without answering it. A newly-found 2023 article closed the loop directly: Apple's answer was product-leader-driven product management, not individual-contributor PMs. Not a correction so much as a story the wiki had been carrying half-finished, completed once the missing article was found sitting un-ingested in `raw/`.

## Where this stands — open tensions, not yet resolved
- **Moore-vs-Cagan sequencing** (above) — an operational default exists, but it's explicitly a judgment call, not a settled fact. Revisit if a newer primary source directly addresses the tension.
- **EMPOWERED is ~90% un-ingested at the raw-text level** (flagged 2026-07-12) — not a correction, but the wiki's largest known gap between what it could ground and what it currently does. Everything from EMPOWERED Ch 9 onward is reachable only indirectly.
- **The Good Product Manager/Bad Product Manager judgment call** — a non-SVPG classic (Ben Horowitz/a16z) sits in `raw/`, deliberately not yet ingested, because including it changes the wiki's root-source discipline in a way that needs an explicit decision, not a default full-build.
- **Design Thinking's problem-framing techniques are under-addressed** (flagged on [[pom-vs-lean-and-design-thinking]]) — the wiki's critique currently engages the Double Diamond's structure but not empathy mapping, HMW reframing, or other Design Thinking mechanics directly, for lack of a primary SVPG source that does.
