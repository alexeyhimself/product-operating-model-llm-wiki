# The Product Operating Model — Overview

> The big-picture entry point to the wiki. Seeded by the first ingest: the **20 product model first principles** — SVPG's observation of what the world's best product companies share, articulated by **Marty Cagan in *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]*** (2024, the [[transformed|root source]]). Two ProductCompass posts were the supporting material used to populate them.

## What it is
The Product Operating Model is how the strongest product companies work: durable, [[empowered-product-teams|empowered, cross-functional product teams]] are given **problems to solve** (not features to build) and held accountable for **[[outcomes-over-output|outcomes]]**. They decide *what to build* through continuous **[[product-discovery|discovery]]** (testing ideas cheaply against the [[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]]) and **[[product-delivery|deliver]]** it themselves — all guided by one holistic **[[product-strategy|product strategy]]** in service of a **[[product-vision|product vision]]**, inside a **[[product-culture|culture]]** of trust and learning.

Five groups of four from *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]* ([[svpg|SVPG]]/Cagan). The compact labels and the 5×4 grouping come from the supporting explainer posts; the substance is the book's:

- **[[principles-of-product-teams|Product Teams]]** — [[empowered-with-problems-to-solve|Empowered with Problems]] · [[outcomes-over-output|Outcomes over Output]] · [[sense-of-ownership|Sense of Ownership]] · [[collaboration|Collaboration]]
- **[[principles-of-product-strategy|Product Strategy]]** — [[focus|Focus]] · [[powered-by-insights|Powered by Insights]] · [[transparency|Transparency]] · [[placing-bets|Placing Bets]]
- **[[principles-of-product-discovery|Product Discovery]]** — [[minimize-waste|Minimize Waste]] · [[assess-product-risks|Assess Product Risks]] · [[embrace-rapid-experimentation|Rapid Experimentation]] · [[test-ideas-responsibly|Test Ideas Responsibly]]
- **[[principles-of-product-delivery|Product Delivery]]** — [[small-frequent-uncoupled-releases|Small Frequent Releases]] · [[instrumentation|Instrumentation]] · [[monitoring|Monitoring]] · [[deployment-infrastructure|Deployment Infrastructure]]
- **[[principles-of-product-culture|Product Culture]]** — [[principles-over-process|Principles over Process]] · [[trust-over-control|Trust over Control]] · [[innovation-over-predictability|Innovation over Predictability]] · [[learning-over-failure|Learning over Failure]]

## The three dimensions
A useful way to read the five groups (Cagan's Q&A confirms business strategy → product strategy → discovery):

1. **Which problems to solve** — [[product-vision|vision]] + [[product-strategy|strategy]] → [[team-objectives|team objectives]] (Strategy principles).
2. **How to solve them** — [[product-discovery|discovery]] against the [[the-four-big-risks|four risks]] (Discovery principles), done by [[empowered-product-teams|empowered teams]] (Team principles).
3. **How to build them** — [[product-delivery|delivery]] (Delivery principles) — all held together by [[product-culture|culture]] (Culture principles).

## What it replaces
The project / IT / **feature-factory** model: teams handed solutions to build, measured on output and predictability, with discovery and delivery split across groups and strategy reduced to a feature roadmap. The principles are the standard an agent using this wiki holds the user's org against — see [[diagnostics]].

## How the model is taught
Per [[2026-02-04-cagan-product-coaching-and-ai|Cagan, Feb 2026]], the primary way PMs become strong at the product model is [[product-coaching]] — by a manager, an external human coach, and (now) a [[model-as-product-coach|foundation model configured with project files + project instructions + strategic context]]. SVPG's Feb 2026 position shift makes the model-as-coach the scalable answer for most PMs. **This wiki implements that prescription:** the [`wiki/`](.) body is the project files; [`CLAUDE.md`](../CLAUDE.md) is the project instructions; the user supplies the strategic context.

What coaching actually consists of — Cagan's older "Coaching Tools" canon, now in the wiki as the **coaching engine**: a coach develops *capability, not deliverables* ([[coaching-vs-contracting]]); for a PM, that means assessing skills across the people/process/product taxonomy ([[pm-competency]]) and coaching the biggest gaps ([[coaching-the-pm]]). Coaching comes in three altitudes — discovery, leadership, transformation ([[types-of-product-coaching]]) — and every coach, human or model, rests on real product experience ([[product-coach]]).
