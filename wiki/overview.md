# The Product Operating Model — Overview

> The big-picture entry point to the wiki. Seeded by the first ingest: the **20 product model first principles** — SVPG's observation of what the world's best product companies share, articulated by **Marty Cagan in *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]*** (2024, the [[transformed|root source]]). Two ProductCompass posts were the supporting material used to populate them.

## What it is
The Product Operating Model is how the strongest product companies work: durable, [[empowered-product-teams|empowered, cross-functional product teams]] are given **problems to solve** (not features to build) and held accountable for **[[outcomes-over-output|outcomes]]**. They decide *what to build* through continuous **[[product-discovery|discovery]]** (testing ideas cheaply against the [[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]]) and **[[product-delivery|deliver]]** it themselves — all guided by one holistic **[[product-strategy|product strategy]]** in service of a **[[product-vision|product vision]]**, inside a **[[product-culture|culture]]** of trust and learning.

## The three dimensions
A useful way to read the five groups (Cagan's Q&A confirms business strategy → product strategy → discovery):

1. **Which problems to solve** — [[product-vision|vision]] + [[product-strategy|strategy]] → [[team-objectives|team objectives]] (Strategy principles).
2. **How to solve them** — [[product-discovery|discovery]] against the [[the-four-big-risks|four risks]] (Discovery principles), done by [[empowered-product-teams|empowered teams]] (Team principles).
3. **How to build them** — [[product-delivery|delivery]] (Delivery principles) — all held together by [[product-culture|culture]] (Culture principles).

## What it replaces
The project / IT / **feature-factory** model: teams handed solutions to build, measured on output and predictability, with discovery and delivery split across groups and strategy reduced to a feature roadmap. The principles are the standard the coach holds the user's org against — see [[diagnostics]].
