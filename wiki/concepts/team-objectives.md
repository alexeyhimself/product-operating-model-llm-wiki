# Team Objectives

> How an empowered team is assigned work: a **significant problem** (customer or company) plus **clear measures of success** (desired outcomes / key results).

## Why it matters
Team objectives are the hand-off point between [[product-strategy|strategy]] and teams — "the output of product strategy is a set of business or customer problems to solve (team objectives)" (*TRANSFORMED* Ch. 13). They are how [[empowered-with-problems-to-solve|empowerment]] is operationalized without dictating solutions.

## How it works
A team objective = **a significant problem** (a customer or company problem) **+ clear measures of success** (synonymous with desired outcomes / key results). Mapped to **[[okrs|OKRs]]**:

- **Objective** — the *qualitative* problem to solve, and *why it matters* ("reduce subscriber churn"; "reduce parcels delivered to the wrong address").
- **Key Results** — the **outcomes** (business results, not activity/output) that define success. Usually **two**: a primary measure + a quality **"backstop"** so the first isn't achieved by harming something else ("reduce incorrect deliveries *while total deliveries keep growing*"). **The numeric targets come *from the team*** — hand them down and the team won't own the commitment.

The team then runs [[product-discovery|discovery]] to find a solution that moves those measures, owning [[product-delivery|delivery]] too. See the [[okrs]] framework for the full technique.

### How teams get the objective (per [[2022-09-23-moore-changing-how-you-decide-which-problems-to-solve|Moore]] + [[2020-11-19-cagan-product-leadership-is-hard|Cagan]])
- [[product-leadership|Product leaders]] derive 1–2 team objectives per team per quarter from the [[product-strategy|product strategy]].
- Teams are selected by area of responsibility, skills, enabling tech, data, and the team's ideas for the problem.
- The team **proposes the key results** (measures of success) and iterates with leadership.
- **The litmus test for empowerment:** the team is able to decide the *best way to solve* the problem they've been assigned. If leadership is still dictating the *how*, empowerment is theater.

### Outcome-based roadmaps (transitional step)
[[2022-09-23-moore-changing-how-you-decide-which-problems-to-solve|Moore]] notes: for orgs not ready to do strategy yet, the simplest first step is to take the existing roadmap and, for each feature, articulate the underlying problem and the logical measure of success. Doesn't fix priority selection, but does meaningfully improve team agency.

### Running them (the OKR technique)
Cagan's nine-part **Team Objectives** series is the operating manual — captured in full on the [[okrs]] framework page. In brief: leaders set the **level of ambition** (roof shot vs moon shot; confidence levels; a portfolio of risk); some work needs a **[[high-integrity-commitments|high-integrity commitment]]** (discovery first, then a date, tracked separately, the exception not the rule); teams **actively manage** via **weekly check-ins**; and **accountability scales with ambition** (missing a moon shot is expected; missing a roof shot or a commitment is not), with a **team-objective post-mortem** for substantial misses. Objectives are also often **shared** (multiple teams pursue one objective *together* — via an API "contract" or a co-located **swarm**) or **common** (multiple teams attack one problem *different ways*, for risk management; isolate each team's impact with an A/B test — the **product attribution problem**) ([[2020-03-04-cagan-team-objectives-collaboration|Collaboration]]). The prerequisite: OKRs are **an empowerment technique** — bolt them onto feature teams or manager/individual goals and you get "OKR theater" ([[2020-02-24-cagan-team-objectives-overview|Overview]]).

## Anti-patterns & misunderstandings
- Confusing the **problem** with a prescribed **solution** ("ship feature X" is not a team objective).
- Key results that are output/feature counts rather than [[outcomes-over-output|outcomes]].
- **OKR theater** — the technique overlaid on a feature-team culture, or diluted into **manager/individual** objectives instead of *team* objectives ([[okrs]]).
- **KR targets handed down** (kills ownership) — or the inverse, **teams choosing their own objectives** with leadership absent (produces "lack of direction"). Leaders pick the problems; teams set the measures.
- **Wrong altitude of metric ([[teresa-torres|Torres]], [[continuous-discovery-habits|CDH]] Ch 3).** Three-tier altitude test:
  - **Business outcome** as team objective → coordination-heavy, lagging, hard to guide day-to-day work. Escalate the altitude if this is what's been handed down: which *product outcome* would drive it?
  - **Product outcome** as team objective → the right altitude; within the trio's span of control.
  - **Traction metric** as team objective → strips the trio of latitude (they can only push the one workflow). Use for junior teams or mature-product optimization only.
- **Nuance (Torres attribution correction — 2026-07-12).** The "outcome = a change in human behavior" phrasing is Josh Seiden's, quoted by Torres in the CDH Ch 3 epigraph. Torres's own operational definition of a *product outcome* is *"a metric the team can influence"* — matches Cagan's usage; the wiki's older Torres-vs-Cagan tension was overstated. See [[outcomes-over-output]] for the full nuance.
