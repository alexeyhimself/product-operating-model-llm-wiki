# Team Objectives

> How an empowered team is assigned work: a **significant problem** (customer or company) plus **clear measures of success** (desired outcomes / key results).

## Why it matters
Team objectives are the hand-off point between [[product-strategy|strategy]] and teams — "the output of product strategy is a set of business or customer problems to solve (team objectives)" (*TRANSFORMED* Ch. 13). They are how [[empowered-with-problems-to-solve|empowerment]] is operationalized without dictating solutions.

## How it works
A team objective = **a significant problem** (a [customer or company problem]) **+ clear measures of success** (synonymous with desired outcomes / key results). Mapped to **OKRs**:

- **Objective** — inspirational; explains the problem and *why it matters*.
- **Key Results** — the desired outcomes the team aims to achieve.

The team then runs [[product-discovery|discovery]] to find a solution that moves those measures, owning [[product-delivery|delivery]] too.

## Anti-patterns & misunderstandings
- Confusing the **problem** with a prescribed **solution** ("ship feature X" is not a team objective).
- Key results that are output/feature counts rather than [[outcomes-over-output|outcomes]].
- **Nuance:** desired outcomes need not be "changes in human behavior" (Torres) — Cagan generalizes to any measure the team can directly influence (e.g. Customer Effort Score). Watch that the easier-to-read proxy still reflects real value.
