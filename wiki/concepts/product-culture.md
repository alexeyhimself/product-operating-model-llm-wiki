# Product Culture

> The shared values and norms that let the rest of the model actually work — favoring **principles, trust, innovation, and learning** over process, control, predictability, and blame.

## Why it matters
A company can adopt the structures of the product model (teams, objectives, discovery) and still fail if the culture undermines them. Culture is what separates a team that *has* [[empowered-product-teams|empowerment]] on the org chart from one that *lives* it.

## How it works
The four [[principles-of-product-culture|culture principles]] define it: [[principles-over-process]] (scale with leaders + judgment), [[trust-over-control]] (servant leadership, context not control), [[innovation-over-predictability]] ("100% predictability = 0% innovation"), and [[learning-over-failure]] ("what did we learn?"). Together they create the psychological safety and autonomy that [[product-discovery|discovery]] and [[collaboration]] need.

## Anti-patterns & misunderstandings
- "Transformation" as a reorg only — new boxes, same control-and-predictability culture.
- Process and predictability treated as the highest virtues; failure punished, so experimentation dies.
- Confusing trust with absence of accountability, or principles with absence of all process.
