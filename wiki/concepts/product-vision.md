# Product Vision

> The **future you are trying to create** and, crucially, **how it will improve the lives of your customers** — what keeps teams inspired and excited.

## Why it matters
Vision provides the *why* and the destination that makes [[product-strategy|strategy]], [[team-objectives|objectives]], and day-to-day [[trust-over-control|autonomous decisions]] coherent. It's what motivates missionaries (vs mercenaries).

## How it works
Cagan: the product vision is "the future you are trying to create and, most importantly, how this vision will improve the lives of your customers... what keeps teams inspired and excited" (*TRANSFORMED* Ch. 13). [[product-strategy|Strategy]] is then *how you accomplish the vision* while meeting business needs.

## Anti-patterns & misunderstandings
- Confusing vision with an **empty mission slogan** ("organize the world's information"). Cagan distinguishes the two — and in *TRANSFORMED* **drops "product mission" entirely**, which Huryn welcomes.
- Treating vision as a marketing tagline rather than a concrete, customer-improving future.
