# PMM ↔ Sales Partnership

> How PMM enables the direct sales channel to actually sell. *"Any company with a direct sales team can't succeed without the vital partnership of product marketing"* ([[loved|LOVED]] Ch 10). Where the [[pmm-pm-partnership|PM partnership]] is a constant, the sales partnership is where PMM's day-to-day judgment is most visibly tested.

## Why it matters
Ch 10 opens with the paper-data-sheet cautionary tale: midmarket sales team badly missed target; came to the quarterly review with a single request — *paper data sheets*. Digital-only collateral was the norm. Marketing was stunned. The sales team said they needed a leave-behind that stood out. When marketing asked whether the wrong customers were being targeted, the sales lead rebuffed: *"No, we just need paper data sheets."*

Lauchengco's diagnosis: *"The problem was not that one team was right and the other wrong. It was that there was a demand, not a discussion, in which sales presumed the solution (paper data sheet) instead of asking for what they needed: a more memorable prompt encouraging follow-up. It also didn't bode well for the marketing-sales relationship that marketing couldn't ask about how prospects were being targeted without sales getting defensive."*

The lesson generalizes: *"Product marketing does the same for sales and marketing that a product manager does for customers. They take sales input and work with marketing to create the best response for what sales is trying to do. They likewise represent sales needs back to the product team to help prioritize product work."*

## Balancing urgent and important — Ch 10's natural tension
*"A natural tension exists between sales and product marketing. Sales wants to get it done now. Product marketing wants to get it done right."* This tension isn't a bug — it's the productive friction that makes the partnership work.

Sales incentive → close deals to hit numbers each quarter. They need **pipeline** and **training**. Marketing is the means.

PMM's role: *"the gate to the necessary product knowledge key for selling."* PMM shapes it for the market rather than passing raw product-team knowledge; creates the deeper product content and tools; directs across marketing specialties so activities add up to a product-GTM plan that lets sales sell.

## The two most powerful tools (Ch 10)
Ch 10 names two tools as *the* most powerful in the sales relationship:

### 1. Reference customers
Real-world proof the product works as claimed. See [[reference-customer-program]] (grounded in [[inspired|INSPIRED]] 1st ed Ch 15). Reference customers are the **prerequisite** for the sales playbook — they provide the concrete real-life examples that best practices for repeatable success are derived from.

### 2. The sales playbook
Written practices for repeatable sales success. Structure:
- Appropriate actions **by stage**.
- **Next step** guidance.
- Relevant **tools**.
- **Qualification criteria** for a customer to flow from one stage to the next.

Playbook creation:
- **PMM drives it** at earlier-stage companies.
- **Always developed hand-in-glove with sales** as they confirm what repeatable success looks like.
- **Connects PMM-created tools to the sales process.**
- **Impact:** *"When done well — and if followed — a good playbook can make an average sales rep successful in a faster than average amount of time."*

PMM then trains sales on what's most important in the playbook: **a demo · competitive response · how to run a proof-of-concept process**.

## Prioritize funnel adjustments from the bottom up
Ch 10's discipline for pipeline diagnosis:
- **Look at funnel metrics** (conversion rates + time in stage).
- **Prioritize adjustments from the bottom of the customer funnel** before moving to the top stages.
- *"If customers aren't converting at the end of the process, there is no point in more prospective customers starting it."*

## Key activities PMM works on with marketing in support of sales (Ch 10)
Eleven concrete activities that live at the PMM/marketing/sales boundary:

1. Jointly-defined ideal customer profiles (ICPs), target segments, target account lists.
2. Customer journey maps.
3. Sales tools — presentations, call scripts, prospecting-email templates.
4. Product data sheets, videos, website product info.
5. Product demo(s) grounded in product's key messages.
6. Competitive-response tools.
7. Usable customer stories / case studies.
8. Customer advisory board.
9. Identifying new target markets.
10. Identifying key events important for the product's position + target customers.
11. Sales training + enablement — including working with sales on how to *stay on target* in who it sells to.

*"While product marketing sets the strategic framing for this work, the marketing and sales teams do a lot of the execution."*

## Indicators the partnership is working well (Ch 10)
Five signals:

1. **Sales is knowledgeable about the product and targets the right ideal customer segments.**
2. **The sales playbook is being followed.**
3. **Healthy flow of prospects through the pipeline**, and whenever gaps are identified, PMM works with marketing to select activities that close those gaps.
4. **Materials connect with intended audiences and articulate the pain people feel today.** PMM helps marketing stay grounded in what creates urgency while being appropriately aspirational.
5. **Marketing actions and assets are relevant, timely, and compelling** (content pieces, press, analyst reports). PMM ensures sales has a wide array of marketing tools to choose from to push deals forward.

## Set It Up for Success
- **Systematic collaboration, not individual dynamics.** *"Any good sales–marketing partnership doesn't solely rely on individual leadership dynamics to succeed."*
- **PMM at weekly pipeline reviews** to hear what's working and adjust marketing response. As sales matures + becomes more predictable, cadence evolves.
- **Reference customers are required.** Ch 10 is emphatic: *"reference customers that prove the product works as claimed are necessary."* See [[reference-customer-program]].
- **Pricing + packaging make sales possible.** *"Packaging is most often led by product marketing, which also has a heavy hand in pricing (see chapter 17). Both need to be easy for sales to explain with a mental model that is easy for customers to understand. Sales often handles negotiations with procurement departments and needs to know how to speak to value, not just price."*

## Anti-patterns — what better looks like (Ch 10)
*"If product is the engine of growth, sales is the gas. Marketing is the gas station. Product marketing is the gas station attendant making sure sales gets tanked up with enough to get to their next stop."* Three anti-patterns:

| Anti-pattern | Better looks like |
|---|---|
| **Marketing as a service to sales.** If marketing serves sales requests without PMM guidance, activities focus only on immediate needs, not strategic ones. | Examine what moves toward product-GTM goals; use data to drive prioritization. |
| **Lack of awareness before sales engages.** Self-directed customer journey means if a product/problem/company isn't on someone's radar, sales' job is hard. *"'Spray and pray' awareness style campaigns are extremely costly."* | Targeted approaches — marketing focuses specifically on customers/accounts where sales is engaging. Account-based, customer-journey-coordinated approaches. |
| **Lack of adherence to the sales playbook.** Sales taking maverick actions to get deals done. Poor-match customers can't be retained; inconsistent messages damage positioning. | Playbooks + tools work best when created collaboratively. Ideal: select a group of sales reps to work with PMM on tools for a set period before broad rollout. Sales management then holds reps accountable to use playbooks + tools. |

## Best-practice touchpoints (Ch 10 cadence)
- **Weekly or biweekly — Pipeline and marketing reviews.** PMM at weekly sales meetings (or in its own forum). Direct dialogue about how marketing activities can better align with sales efforts. PMM helps determine strategic response: product deficit? positioning/messaging adjustment? information/training gap?
- **Monthly — Joint funnel analysis.** Diagnose whether targeted work (new competitive asset, timely e-book) can improve effectiveness for either function. Examine SLAs between marketing and sales (handoff criteria) — PMM watches for adjustments.
- **Quarterly — Revisit product go-to-market and funnel metrics.** Candid sales + marketing discussion based on data about how much pipeline growth the business needs and how much will come from marketing. PMM ensures this informs product prioritization.
