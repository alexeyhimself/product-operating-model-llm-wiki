# Product Management Theater

> Holding the **title** of product manager (or product leader) while doing a fundamentally different job — usually project management and backlog administration — so the real product work (value + viability, [[product-discovery|discovery]]) goes undone. The *performance* of product management without the substance. Cagan's term; sharpened across his 2024–2026 talks.

## Why it matters
This is Cagan's central diagnosis of the current product-management crisis. In a [[feature-teams-vs-product-teams|feature team]], the product manager is really a project manager — "dramatically overpaid for the value they provide" ([[2024-03-10-cagan-product-management-theater-lennys|Lenny's]]) — because the actual product decisions were already made by stakeholders and handed down as a roadmap. Cagan is blunt that in that model "you don't really need a product manager." The theater is the gap between the cool title (which the world knows, thanks to the empowered-team model) and the absent skills and responsibilities.

The stakes rose sharply with generative AI, which **simultaneously exposes and accelerates** the theater ([[2026-06-04-cagan-pm-theater-age-of-ai-modern-cto|Modern CTO]]): it makes clear the bottleneck was never the engineers ("how long to build") but product ("*what* to build"), while also making it easier than ever to speed up the project model — "more garbage being shipped than I ever remember." Cagan's "reckoning": delivery-team product owners and feature-team PMs are the most vulnerable roles as companies cut, and GenAI compounds it.

## How it works
**Three models, and the theater lives in the first two** ([[2026-06-04-cagan-pm-theater-age-of-ai-modern-cto|Modern CTO]]): (1) the *product owner* model (agile PO + developers — a "software factory," which AI is making irrelevant); (2) the *feature team* / project model (a roadmap of features handed down; the PM gathers requirements and herds cats); (3) the *empowered product team* (problems to solve; the PM owns value + viability). Only the third needs — and produces — a real product manager.

**The tells:**
- **Output over outcome.** You're measured by shipping, not by solving the problem. Cagan reframes it for executives as *[[time-to-money|time to money]]*, not time to market.
- **The "three ways" a PM works** ([[2018-06-07-cagan-behind-every-great-product-mtpcon-london-2016|Behind Every Great Product]]): (a) escalate every decision to your manager; (b) call a meeting for every decision (design-by-committee); (c) *actually do the job*. Only (c) is product management.
- **The mealy-mouthed job definition** — "I facilitate, I communicate, I herd the cats, I say 'why'." Cagan: "the *why* comes from [[product-strategy|product strategy]] anyway." A real PM is a **creator, not a facilitator**.
- **Agile theater** — the sibling ([[2024-04-14-cagan-escape-the-project-trap-agile-theatre|Scandinavian Product]], [[2023-01-16-cagan-master-class-context-not-control|Master Class]]): all the rituals (scrum master, CSPO/PSPO product owner, stand-ups, retros) yet **releasing monthly or quarterly** and not empowered. "Waterfall with a marketing name of agile." Agile only ever addresses *one* of the three transformation dimensions (how you build) — the easiest one.
- **Product leadership theater** — the leader version: a "product leader" who staffs an agency-like org, owns no vision or strategy, and does little coaching.

**Why it proliferated** ([[2024-03-10-cagan-product-management-theater-lennys|Lenny's]]): pandemic over-hiring and a lowered bar; higher cost of funds; predicted GenAI impact; role bloat (agile coaches, product owners, product ops, business analysts, scrum masters, APMs); SAFe "depressingly popular" outside Silicon Valley; and ~90% of online PM advice coming from the feature-team world. Cagan's *Epic Waste* article named the cost a decade earlier.

## Anti-patterns & misunderstandings
- **Not identical to [[feature-teams-vs-product-teams]].** That page is the *structural* axis (problems vs features; durable vs project). Theater is the *role-substance* symptom that rides on it: a person performing product-management activities without the skills or responsibilities. They travel together but name different things.
- **"My team is empowered."** Often said, rarely true — Cagan treats the claim itself as a clue to look harder.
- **Certifications as proof.** Cagan calls the big PM certifications "bogus"; a CSPO/PSPO teaches backlog administration in Jira, "very analogous to learning how to operate Google Docs" — a tool, not the job.
- **Using AI to end the theater by speeding the project model.** Generating PRDs, aggregating feedback into a roadmap — this deepens the theater. The escape is to *learn the product model* (see [[model-as-product-coach]], [[coaching-vs-contracting]]).
- **Fatalism.** Cagan's counter: even an individual contributor has agency — self-assess, raise your skills from product-owner/feature-team PM toward real PM; companies notice and promote.
