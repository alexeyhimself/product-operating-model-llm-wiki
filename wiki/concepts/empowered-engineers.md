# Empowered Engineers

> Engineers given **the problem to solve and the strategic context**, free to use technology to find the best solution. Cagan calls this **"the most important thing"** — if he could change *one* thing about how a feature-team org works, it would be this ([[2020-04-20-cagan-the-most-important-thing|canon]]).

## Why it matters
- Engineers are **the single most important source of innovation** in a strong product company — they work with the enabling technology every day and are best positioned to see what's *just now possible*.
- The Jobs line ([[2022-09-20-moore-changing-how-you-solve-problems|Moore quotes it]]): "We don't hire all these engineers to tell them what to build; we hire them to show us what's possible."
- **[[bill-campbell|Bill Campbell]]'s formulation:** "the single most important thing in a product company is empowered engineers." The legendary coach behind Steve Jobs, Jeff Bezos, and Google's founders taught this directly ([[2026-06-04-cagan-pm-theater-age-of-ai-modern-cto|Modern CTO]], [[2025-04-21-cagan-design-a-product-like-steve-jobs|the Steve Jobs myth]]) — which is why the Jobs quote above is really a statement about empowerment, not genius: Jobs "didn't know the solutions; he just knew what makes a great product," and teams brought him prototypes to iterate on.
- Most breakthrough products you love originated from an empowered engineer (canon claim).
- "If you're just using your engineers to code, you're only getting about half their value."

## The empowered engineer in the wild (case studies)
- **[[amazon-prime|Amazon]]** — engineer **Charlie Ward** connected the DVD-rental team's subscription capability to free shipping and proposed the subscription that became **Prime** (a ~$35B business). The idea came from an *engineer* close to the enabling technology, not from a roadmap.
- **[[spotify-discover-weekly|Spotify]]** — two **ML engineers** (not the CEO) saw the possibility behind **Discover Weekly**, seeded by a hack-week add-on (*Play It Forward*). "The most innovative ideas … often originate from those who engage with the enabling technology daily — the engineers."
- **[[google-product-model|Google]]** — **Tech Leads** are "first among equals," own [[product-delivery|delivery]], and are deeply involved in discovery, which is why many Google PMs "haven't touched a ticket in years."

## What empowerment of an engineer actually means
Empowerment **is**: giving the engineer the **problem** + the **strategic context**, then trusting them (with the trio) to find the best solution.

Empowerment is **not**:
- letting engineers choose *how to code* the solution (they should anyway)
- letting engineers determine the architecture (they should anyway)

## The first-time-at-sprint-planning test
"If the first time your engineers see a product idea is at sprint planning, you are clearly a feature team, and your engineers are not empowered in any meaningful sense." ([[2020-04-20-cagan-the-most-important-thing|Cagan]])

This is the cleanest single diagnostic for engineer-empowerment status.

## What enables empowered engineering
- A compelling **[[product-vision|product vision]]** to attract and inspire them.
- A clear **[[product-strategy|product strategy]]** so they're working on the right problems.
- Concrete **[[team-objectives|team objectives]]** so the problem is well-defined.
- A [[product-manager|PM]] and [[product-designer|designer]] providing value/viability/usability context inside the trio.
- **Direct contact with customers and data** — engineers in the room during discovery and customer visits ([[2020-09-04-cagan-discovery-problem-vs-solution]]).
- **One team** doing both discovery and delivery — no hand-offs from a "discovery team" ([[2020-10-30-cagan-discovery-delivery]]).
- A **dual-track career ladder** so the best engineers stay engineers.

## Anti-patterns
- **Outsourced engineering** — literal mercenaries; Cagan: outsourcing your engineering signals "you're not serious about this — come back when you decide to get serious" ([[2022-12-07-cagan-product-lessons-jobs-musk-20vc|20VC]]).
- **Sprint-planning-first** — engineers see ideas only as Jira tickets.
- **Excluded from customer contact** — kills innovation.
- **"Engineering-driven" treated as a problem** — Cagan: "engineering-driven" orgs are usually the easiest to transform, because they already have the most important part right.
- **Career ladder anchored only on management** — best engineers leave; remaining engineers underperform.
