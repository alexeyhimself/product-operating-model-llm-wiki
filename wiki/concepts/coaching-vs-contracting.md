# Coaching vs Contracting

> "There is a very big difference between *doing a thing* for a client, versus *coaching them on how to do that thing*." — [[marty-cagan|Cagan]], [[2023-01-11-cagan-becoming-a-product-coach|Becoming a Product Coach]]. The single most load-bearing distinction in product coaching, and the one most often blurred.

## The distinction
- **Contracting / doing** — you deliver the artifact: the product strategy, the vision, the filled role, the quarterly planning. The client gets *a* deliverable.
- **Coaching / developing** — you teach the client to produce strong artifacts themselves. The client gets *capability*.

A leader who says "you clearly can create a strong product strategy; we'd like you to provide us one" is asking for a contractor, not a coach.

## Why it matters
- **Scalability.** "You can coach a dozen product leaders on creating an effective product strategy, but you can likely only produce a single strong product strategy in that same amount of time." Coaching compounds; contracting doesn't.
- **It develops people, not just outputs.** The point of [[product-coaching]] is a more capable PM/leader/team — an outcome contracting never produces.
- **Fractional leadership is contracting in disguise.** A fractional CPO/VP "is signing up to actually deliver the critical deliverables" — not to coach. Cagan considers the product-leader role poorly suited to part-time work; it's an immersive role.

## When doing-the-work is legitimate
Cagan doesn't forbid deliverable work — clients often want help filling roles, running a vision/strategy sprint, or facilitating quarterly objectives, and you can take as much of that as you like. Two warnings: underestimating a deliverable's effort is on you (and costs you the testimonial), and the business runs on reputation and word-of-mouth, so anything less than excellent work is expensive in future clients.

## Wiki synthesis — the line that defines model-as-product-coach
This distinction is the same line that separates [[model-as-product-coach|model-as-coach]] from the **PM-theater** anti-pattern. Using a foundation model to *learn to do the work* (develop product sense, prepare for decisions, seek critique) is coaching. Using it to *produce your artifacts* — generate the PRD, write the user stories, aggregate the feedback — is contracting yourself out of the learning, and "an agent … could just as easily do this themselves." A coach (human or model) that does the work for you isn't coaching you. See [[model-as-product-coach]] and [[coaching-the-pm]].
