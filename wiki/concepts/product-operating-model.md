# Product Operating Model

> A **model** — a coherent set of principles, not a process or methodology — describing how the world's best technology product companies consistently work: durable, cross-functional teams given **problems to solve**, held accountable for **outcomes**, operating inside a culture of trust and learning. This page is the term's definitional home; [[overview|the overview page]] is the fuller guided tour through its principles, dimensions, concepts, and competencies.

## Why it matters
Before this term existed, SVPG described the same underlying pattern piecemeal — empowered teams, product discovery, product strategy — without a name that tied it together as one coherent thing an organization either has or doesn't. Naming it turned a scattered set of practices into something an organization could be **assessed against**, **transformed toward**, and **coached on** — which is the whole reason this wiki exists (see [[model-as-product-coach]]).

**Origin of the term.** "Product operating model" was coined at **Atlassian** and adopted by [[svpg|SVPG]] as the name for the pattern Cagan had been describing since *[[inspired|INSPIRED]]* (2008). It is deliberately a *model* — "if your product operating model is broken" is a statement about principles being violated, not about a missing Jira workflow. See [[2025-03-03-cagan-why-your-product-operating-model-is-broken]] for Cagan's own use of the phrase this way.

## What it is, structurally
Per Cagan's own overview articles ([[2024-01-17-cagan-product-model-concepts|Product Model Concepts]] + [[2023-03-15-cagan-product-model-competencies|Product Model Competencies]], both written ahead of *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]* to make the model legible to non-technical audiences), the model can be described four ways simultaneously — these are different cuts through the *same* thing, not four separate things:

1. **20 first principles**, in five groups of four (teams, strategy, discovery, delivery, culture) — the compact statement of belief. See [[overview]] for the full list.
2. **Three transformation dimensions** — how you *decide* which problems to solve, how you *solve* them, how you *build* the solution. See [[transformation]].
3. **Five concepts** — [[product-culture|culture]], [[product-strategy|strategy]], [[empowered-product-teams|teams]], [[product-discovery|discovery]], [[product-delivery|delivery]].
4. **Four competencies** — [[product-manager|PM]] (value + viability), [[product-designer|designer]] (usability), [[engineers|Tech Lead/engineers]] (feasibility), [[product-leadership|product leadership]] (strategic context + coaching). The first three form [[the-product-team-trio|the trio]].

Underneath all four cuts sits the same mechanism: teams given **problems**, addressing the **[[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]]** (value, usability, feasibility, viability) through continuous **discovery**, held accountable for **outcomes**, inside a **culture** that trusts them to find the solution.

## What it is not
- **Not a process, methodology, or set of ceremonies.** You cannot buy or certify your way into it (contrast with [[pom-vs-agile-and-safe|Agile-at-Scale frameworks]]). Cagan: "there is *no one right way*" — the model names outcomes and responsibilities, not steps.
- **Not synonymous with any single practice** — not Agile, not Lean, not Design Thinking, not OKRs, not any one discovery technique. Those are tools some teams inside the model use; none of them, alone or combined, constitutes the model. See [[pom-vs-agile-and-safe]] and [[pom-vs-lean-and-design-thinking]] for the detailed contrasts.
- **Not a title-swap.** Renaming project managers "product managers" and calling it done is [[product-management-theater|product management theater]] — "many people have adopted the new titles of the product model, yet without learning the new competencies" ([[2023-03-15-cagan-product-model-competencies|Cagan]]).
- **Not exclusive to "tech companies."** Applies to any organization whose engineers build the technology that powers the business — see [[2023-09-12-cagan-who-is-product-operating-model-for|Who Is the Product Operating Model For?]] and [[transformation]].
