# Types of Product Coaching

> Cagan ([[2023-01-11-cagan-becoming-a-product-coach|Becoming a Product Coach]]) sees three primary types of product coaching, distinguished by **altitude** — who the client is and what's at stake. They aren't mutually exclusive, but even multi-level coaches have a clear preference. Useful for an agent to locate *what kind of help* a user actually needs.

## The three types
| Type | Client | What it tackles | Who does it well |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Discovery coaching** | A product team | Learning to do [[product-discovery|product discovery]] — discovering a solution worth building; moving from feature team to [[empowered-product-teams|empowered team]]. The foundation of product coaching; most demand. | Former PMs / product designers |
| **Product leadership coaching** | Head of Product and/or Head of Eng/Tech | The big leadership topics: [[product-vision|vision]], team topology, [[product-strategy|strategy]], [[team-objectives|objectives]], and the leader's own staffing & coaching skills. Often for "battlefield-promoted" leaders. | Former heads of product / technology |
| **Transformation coaching** | CEO / GM of a business unit | Guiding senior leadership through the mindset, culture, funding, staffing, and go-to-market changes of moving from a project to a product company. | Former senior leaders the CEO will trust ("been there, done that") |

Why transformation is hardest: most CEOs "won't trust the future of their company to a product coach that has not 'been there and done that.'" The coach must hold their own with the CFO and head of sales, yet engage directly with PMs, designers, and engineers. "It's little wonder why so few transformations succeed — there are just very few people equipped to help."

## Types of client (the other dimension)
- **Startups** — laser-focused on [[product-discovery|discovery]] and product/market fit; fun, but least willing/able to pay (sometimes equity).
- **Growth-stage / scaleups** — past product/market fit; now need serious staffing/coaching, plus topology, vision, and strategy as teams multiply. Benefit from *both* discovery and leadership coaching. Great clients, but rare.
- **Enterprises** — the largest client segment: hundreds of teams, dozens of leaders, real funding and need. (Cagan's reminder: Amazon, Google, Apple, Netflix are enterprises too — "enterprise" isn't synonymous with slow.) Downside: politics and waste cap most engagements at incremental impact.

## The biggest decision — focus
"Just as it's very difficult to create a product that tries to please everyone, it's very difficult to create a product coaching business that tries to please everyone." Cagan's advice: focus on **one type of coaching for one type of client** — the intersection of what you love and what you're qualified for. Common combinations: discovery coaching for enterprises; product leadership coaching for growth-stage; transformation coaching for enterprises; discovery coaching for startups.
