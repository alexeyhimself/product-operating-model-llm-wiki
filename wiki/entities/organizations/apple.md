# Apple

> Cagan's long-time favorite product company — and his case study in the **risk of concentrating product judgment in one leader**. Jobs-era excellence, then the post-Jobs question of who owns product.

## Profile
A small-portfolio, blockbuster-product company (Mac, iPod, iPhone, iPad, Watch) that repeatedly redefined consumer markets. Cagan: Apple's edge was never marketing but **value** delivered through a chain — hardware serves software, software serves the user experience, the user experience serves emotion, and the product serves a real, unmet need ([[2007-01-27-cagan-lessons-from-apple|Lessons from Apple, 2007]]).

## Why it's here
- **The CEO as head of product.** During Jobs's tenure the visionary CEO *was* the effective head of product — the archetype behind [[founder-style-leadership]]. Cagan's caveat: this model "could not scale to most companies."
- **The succession risk.** The [[apple-role-of-product|Role of Product at Apple case study]] ([[2015-08-03-cagan-the-role-of-product-at-apple|2015]]) uses the first Apple Watch to ask what happens when a company loses its singular product leader and must **re-establish the product competency** — via a new superstar, product rock-stars per line, or (most scalable) introducing the **true product role**.
- **Value as the standard.** Apple is the wiki's reference for **[[the-four-big-risks|value]]** as the hallmark of a great product.
- **The alternative-to-PMs model, named explicitly.** [[2023-11-28-cagan-alternatives-to-product-managers|Cagan]]: Apple has world-class designers, world-class engineers, and strong program managers — but on its core consumer devices, it doesn't have individual-contributor product managers. Value + viability are instead covered by an unusually deep bench of **product leaders**, "the most under-appreciated aspect of Apple's version of the product model." The trade-off is real: these leaders can be decision bottlenecks, "and it's not a coincidence that Apple is not known for moving quickly." See [[product-manager]] for the three-alternatives framing this exemplifies.

## Notable / cautionary
- **The true product role ≠ "fill the white space."** Cagan's pushback on defining PM as "do whatever it takes to ship" — the job is to ensure the product has the necessary **value**.
- **⚠️ Point-in-time flag.** The 2015 Apple Watch critique was a 2015 *product* verdict; the Watch later became commercially very successful. The durable lesson is structural (succession + product competency), not the specific product call. See the [[apple-role-of-product|case study]] for the full flag.
