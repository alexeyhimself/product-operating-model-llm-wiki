# Amazon

> One of Cagan's most-cited exemplar product organizations — and the company that institutionalized two practices the wiki draws on heavily: the **written narrative** (the 6-pager / 2-pager pattern) and **customer obsession + invent on behalf of customers**.

## Profile
A consumer-and-platform tech company with multiple distinct business units (Amazon.com retail, AWS, Devices, Studios, etc.), each with its own strategic context ([[2020-01-09-cagan-coaching-strategic-context|canon: AWS vs. Amazon.com are different contexts]]). Cagan treats Amazon as a Level-4 exemplar in [[model-maturity]] alongside Apple, Google, Netflix — different cultures, same product fundamentals.

## Why it's here
- **Customer obsession** — not competitor-obsessed; "invent on behalf of customers" ([[2009-08-03-cagan-lessons-from-amazon|Cagan via Bezos]]).
- **The written narrative as decision-making tool** — the 6-page memo + silent reading is the canon decision-making practice ([[2019-03-11-cagan-coaching-tools-the-narrative|Cagan]] via Brad Porter, Stephenie Landry, Jeff Bezos).
- **Long-term thinking** — projects with 5–7-year payoffs; "always Day 1."
- **Owner mindset compensation** — Bezos's 1997 shareholder letter quote on stock-options-over-cash is the canonical Cagan citation for owner-mindset ([[2019-06-24-cagan-coaching-owner-vs-employee|canon]]).
- **Working Backwards** — the press-release-first product-definition method is foundational; see [[working-backwards|*Working Backwards*]] (the book by Bryar & Carr).

## Notable practices the wiki uses
- **Written narratives** ([[2019-03-11-cagan-coaching-tools-the-narrative]]) — the favorite coaching tool for PM thinking.
- **Working Backwards** — start with the press release, work backwards to the product.
- **Single-threaded leaders** — durable ownership; conceptual relative of [[empowered-product-teams|empowered teams]].
- **Bar Raisers** in hiring — disciplined, high-bar staffing.
