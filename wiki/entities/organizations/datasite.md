# Datasite

> Formerly **Merrill Corporation** (founded 1968 in Minneapolis by Roger Merrill and his wife). **Rebranded to Datasite in 2020** after divesting the legacy financial-printing business to focus on the SaaS platform for the M&A community. Included in the wiki because Merrill → Datasite is *TRANSFORMED*'s clearest transformation of an *old, sales-driven, non-tech legacy company* — the exact profile most people believe cannot transform. Full narrative in [[datasite-transformation]].

## Profile
- Old-line financial services (financial printing, document management, virtual data rooms) → modern SaaS platform for the M&A community.
- Transformation began under CEO **Rusty Wiley**, with [[christian-idiodi|Christian Idiodi]] as head of product (before joining [[svpg|SVPG]]), **Thomas Fredell** (product peer), and **Jeremiah Ivan** (head of engineering).
- **4,000+ employees, 23 countries** at intervention — grown by acquisition without unifying strategy.
- 2019 revenue +30%+; 10,000+ M&A deals annually today.

## Why it's here
Exemplar. Cagan notes: *"another very common misconception in the product community is that innovation only happens in consumer products, but that it's a different game when building products for large companies. It is unfortunately true that many enterprise software companies have been slow to move to the product model, and it's also true that sales-driven organizations can be difficult to change."* Datasite is the counter — including the emblematic **sales-owns-everything** diagnosis and the patented redaction innovation ([[transformed|TRANSFORMED]] Ch 35).
