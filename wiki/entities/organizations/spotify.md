# Spotify

> Cagan exemplar — and the company whose *real* strength (shared product principles) is most often confused with something else ("The Spotify Model" of squads/tribes/chapters/guilds).

## Profile
Stockholm-founded music-streaming company that beat the incumbents to streaming and then held its own against Amazon, Apple, and Google. Cagan: "even though Stockholm is more than 5000 miles from Silicon Valley, the principles behind how Spotify and the world's top product companies work are remarkably consistent."

## Why it's here
- **The four-risks worked example.** The [[spotify-discover-weekly|Discover Weekly case study]] is the wiki's cleanest illustration of the [[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]] tackled in sequence, with responsible, cheap [[product-discovery|discovery]] (live-data prototype → dogfood → 5% rollout → [[high-integrity-commitments|high-integrity business case]]).
- **Empowerment in practice.** Daniel Ek was openly skeptical of Discover Weekly but **gave the team a problem, not a solution** — the canonical illustration of what [[empowered-product-teams|empowered teams]] actually require of leaders.
- **Correcting "The Spotify Model."** What made Spotify strong is the product **principles**, not the squad/tribe/chapter org structure people copied. Cagan wrote the piece partly to set this record straight.

## Notable practices the wiki uses
- **The 5% rule** — any empowered team may roll an experiment to up to 5% of users without asking permission; a concrete marker of real empowerment + [[product-delivery|delivery]] infrastructure.
- **Dedicated A/B / experimentation platform team** — instrumentation, telemetry, monitoring, continuous delivery as an *investment* that lets teams ship [[outcomes-over-output|outcomes]].
- **Lean-forward vs lean-back** and **Moments** — [[powered-by-insights|insight]]-driven segmentation that shaped strategy.
- **High-integrity business case** — build the expensive thing only once evidence justifies it.
