# Google

> Cagan exemplar — one of the earliest product-model companies, and the case that shows how the model **scales** (180,000+ employees; nine 1B-user products) through "experts lead experts" leadership.

## Profile
A multi-division company (platform / consumer / business services) with wide variance in culture and role definition across divisions — yet generally faithful to the core product principles. Won its markets (search, ads, maps, mail, browser, video, mobile OS) not by inventing categories but by **solving hard problems better than incumbents**, often over many years.

## Why it's here
- **How the model scales.** The [[google-product-model|Google case study]] shows the structural answer: **[[product-leadership|"experts lead experts"]]** — the Tech Lead Manager (TLM) and Group Product Manager (GPM) as technically-competent management units, so no coordinator layer is needed. The canonical illustration of "empowered teams need *better* management, not less."
- **OKRs, done right.** Google is the [[okrs|OKR]] poster child; the article's key point is that OKRs were *designed for* product-model companies with empowered teams — and are "incongruous" for feature teams.
- **Strategy at scale.** Leaders pick the hardest problems (PageRank, AdWords, Waymo), sometimes **broadcast** them, and often run **multiple teams on one problem** ([[team-topology]]).
- **The AI-era test.** Invented the transformer; navigated Desktop→Mobile→"AI First"; Gemini at the frontier — Cagan's evidence that a product-model company keeps delivering through disruption.

## Notable practices the wiki uses
- **Tech Lead Manager (TLM) / Group Product Manager (GPM)** — "experts lead experts"; technically-competent management.
- **Tech Lead "first among equals"** — owns [[product-delivery|delivery]]; the ideal complement to a strong [[product-manager|PM]].
- **Broadcast problems + multiple teams on one problem** — a scale luxury; raises the odds of an exceptional solution.
- **Dogfooding + beta + constant experimentation** — evidence over hierarchy/politics.
- **Not all teams empowered** — Cagan's honest caveat: some Google teams are feature teams. A useful antidote to idealizing any exemplar.
