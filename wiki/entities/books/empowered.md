# EMPOWERED

> Cagan & [[chris-jones|Chris Jones]] on **product leadership** — subtitled *Ordinary People, Extraordinary Products*. How leaders create the conditions for *ordinary* people on empowered teams to do extraordinary work (2020). Official page: [svpg.com/books/empowered](https://www.svpg.com/books/empowered-ordinary-people-extraordinary-products/). Directly ingested 2026-07-12.

## Where it sits in the SVPG series
The **leadership volume** of the four-book arc: **[[inspired|INSPIRED]] → EMPOWERED → [[loved|LOVED]] → [[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]**. If INSPIRED is about the PM and the team, EMPOWERED is about the **product leaders** who create the conditions — the "trust gap" between typical companies and Amazon/Apple/Google/Netflix, per Ch 4. TRANSFORMED then generalizes both into a transformation guide.

## What it covers
Product leadership — **coaching + staffing** (management) and **product vision + principles + team topology + product strategy + team objectives + evangelism** (leadership) — as the two-part job that separates strong product companies from weak ones. Audience: **product leaders and aspiring product leaders**: managers/directors/VPs of product management, product design, and engineering.

Cagan's framing (Ch 6 *A Guide to EMPOWERED*): *"the advice in the book is aimed at product leaders. If some advice is aimed at a specific role, such as a product manager, product designer, tech lead, or data scientist, then this will be called out explicitly."* And why they wrote it in first person: *"we want to come as close as possible to the experience of sitting down with you, the reader, for a series of one-on-one coaching sessions, where our only goal is to help you become an exceptionally strong product leader."*

## Key contributions grounded here as primary
- **Empowerment definition — the sentence the wiki leans on.** Ch 53: *"the team is given a small number of significant problems to solve (the team objectives)… The litmus test for empowerment is that the team is able to decide the best way to solve the problems they have been assigned."* And the diagnosis of why it doesn't happen (Ch 4): the reason isn't people — it's **trust**. Ordinary people at Google/Amazon/Apple/Netflix produce extraordinary products because the leaders leverage talent differently, not because they hire different humans. Backs [[empowered-product-teams]] and [[empowered-with-problems-to-solve]].
- **Leadership vs Management as two halves of the role.** Ch 3: leadership = **inspiration** (product vision + principles, team topology, product strategy, evangelism); management = **execution** (staffing + coaching + team objectives). Backs [[product-leadership]]. Also: *"you cannot be a good manager without being a good coach"* ([[bill-campbell|Bill Campbell]] opening Ch 6 of Part I and Ch 7 of Part II).
- **The six elements of strategic context.** Ch 12 states the leader's *"four major explicit responsibilities"* that supply the strategic context teams need (on top of the two that come from senior leaders — mission + objectives): **Product Vision and Principles · Team Topology · Product Strategy · Team Objectives** — plus **Product Evangelism** as the ongoing act that keeps it alive. The wiki's [[strategic-context]] concept uses [[2020-01-09-cagan-coaching-strategic-context|Cagan's 2020 article's list]] as its canonical six; Ch 12 is the **book-length primary source** for the same concept. Together they anchor the `[[product-vision]]`, `[[product-principles]]`, `[[team-topology]]`, `[[product-strategy]]`, `[[team-objectives]]` pages.
- **The coaching engine — mindset · assessment · plan · one-on-one · written narrative.** Part II (Chs 7–11) is the **book-length primary source** for the [[product-coaching]] canon the wiki has been carrying via SVPG articles ([[2019-04-08-cagan-coaching-tools-the-assessment]] · [[2019-04-22-cagan-coaching-tools-the-plan]] · [[2019-02-08-cagan-coaching-tools-the-one-on-one]] · [[2019-03-11-cagan-coaching-tools-the-narrative]]). Ch 7's *"Developing People Is Job #1"* — *"You should measure your own job performance on the successes of your team members, even more than the success of your products"* — is the coaching-mindset canon. Backs [[coaching-the-pm]] and [[pm-competency]].
- **Coaching is not optional — it's the essential responsibility.** Ch 7: *"If you are a manager, you should be spending most of your time and energy on coaching your team."* Backs the ~50% weekly-time guidance on [[product-leadership]].
- **The "most important thing" is empowered engineers.** Ch 80 is a full chapter reinforcing what Cagan calls the single most important thing. Backs [[empowered-engineers]] and [[the-most-important-thing|SVPG canon]].
- **The four "coaching topics" that show up as full chapters.** Sense of Ownership (Ch 13), Managing Time (Ch 14), Thinking (Ch 15), Decisions (Ch 21) — the book-length primary sources for the [[2019-06-24-cagan-coaching-owner-vs-employee|coaching mindset trilogy]] and [[2019-04-08-cagan-coaching-tools-the-assessment|the Assessment's people-skills column]].
- **Team Topology — Platform Teams vs Experience Teams.** Ch 42 canonizes the split; Ch 41's three objectives — **Ownership · Autonomy · Alignment** ("**loosely coupled, highly aligned**") — are the design criteria. Backs [[team-topology]].
- **Team Objectives and OKRs — the book's version.** Part VII covers objective *assignment* (Ch 54), *ambition* (Ch 55), *high-integrity commitments* (Ch 56), *shared vs. common* objectives (Ch 57), *management by weekly tracking* (Ch 58), and *accountability* (Ch 59) — the book-length primary of the [[okrs]] framework the wiki has grounded via the [[2020-02-24-cagan-team-objectives-overview|9-part Team Objectives series]].
- **Stakeholder Management vs Collaboration.** Ch 72 is the origin of the wiki's [[stakeholder-relationship-health]] diagnostic framing — collaboration is not management.
- **Meaningful Transformation.** Chs 77–79 (*Meaningful Transformation · Transformation in Action · TRANSFORMED*) preview what became [[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]. Backs [[transformation]] with a 2020 primary attribution — earlier than TRANSFORMED itself.

## Voice discipline
Ideas grounded in EMPOWERED are attributed inline to specific chapters (Ch 3 / Ch 7 / Ch 12 / Ch 53 etc.). Long text is not reproduced; short attributed quotes only. Where TRANSFORMED refines an EMPOWERED idea (e.g., topology as the joint call of head-of-product + head-of-technology), both are cited. Where EMPOWERED is the earlier primary for something the wiki already grounds via a Cagan/SVPG article, the article is kept as **primary (article)** and EMPOWERED added as **primary (book-length)** — not a demotion of the article.
