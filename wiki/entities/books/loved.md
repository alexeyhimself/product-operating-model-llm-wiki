# LOVED

> *LOVED: How to Rethink Marketing for Tech Products* — [[martina-lauchengco|Martina Lauchengco]], 2022. The **product-marketing volume** of the SVPG four-book arc: **[[inspired|INSPIRED]] → [[empowered|EMPOWERED]] → LOVED → [[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]**. Official page: [svpg.com/books/loved](https://www.svpg.com/books/loved-how-to-rethink-marketing-for-tech-products/). Directly ingested 2026-07-12.

## What it covers
What great **[[product-marketing|product marketing]]** is and does: **positioning, messaging, market fit** (the *market* side of product-market fit), **go-to-market strategy**, and the discipline that connects a valuable product to the customers who should love it. Audience: product marketers, founders, and product managers who need to understand the discipline they most need a partner for.

Lauchengco's core reframing (Ch 1): *"Product marketing's purpose is to drive product adoption by shaping market perception through strategic marketing activities that meet business goals."* She uses **product marketing** (function) rather than **product marketer** (role) intentionally — the work is what matters, and good product marketing can happen when capable people are willing to do it, but *"outcomes always improve with strong product marketers."*

## Structure
- **Part One — The Foundation: Understanding Product Marketing's Fundamentals (Chs 1–6):** Ch 1 When David Beats Goliath (what product marketing is; why it matters now; where it fits) · **Ch 2 The Fundamentals of Product Marketing** (the four fundamentals — see below) · Ch 3 Ambassador · Ch 4 Strategist · Ch 5 Storyteller · Ch 6 Evangelist.
- **Part Two — How to Do the Role: Who Should Do Product Marketing and How to Do It Well (Chs 7–13):** Ch 7 Strong Product Marketing (skills · responsibilities · anti-patterns) · **Ch 8 How to Partner with Product Management** · **Ch 9 How to Partner with Marketing** · **Ch 10 How to Partner with Sales** · Ch 11 Discovering and Rediscovering Market Fit (**the market side of product-market fit**) · Ch 12 Product Marketing in the Age of Agile · Ch 13 The Metrics That Matter.
- **Part Three — Strategist: Guardrails and Levers (Chs 14–20):** Ch 14 When Strategy Guides Product Go-to-Market · Ch 15 What the iPhone Shows Us About Adoption Life Cycles · Ch 16 The Brand Lever · Ch 17 The Pricing Lever · Ch 18 Marketing When It's Not About Product · **Ch 19 The One-Sheet Product Go-to-Market Canvas** · Ch 20 Understanding in Action (early / scaling / mature).
- **Part Four — Storyteller: Clarity and Authenticity (Chs 21–24):** Ch 21 Discover Your Position · Ch 22 How to Listen and Connect (**CAST** — a simple guide) · Ch 23 Understanding in Action (Netflix · Zendesk) · Ch 24 The Balancing Act (create-new-or-redefine-existing category).
- **Part Five and remainder** — additional practical treatments plus appendices (marketing channels glossary; templates).

## Key contributions grounded here as primary
- **The four fundamentals of product marketing (Ch 2).** Root of the wiki's coverage on [[product-marketing]]:
  1. **Ambassador — Connect Customer and Market Insights.** Product marketing brings customer/market/competitive expertise into product decisions, especially GTM.
  2. **Strategist — Direct Your Product's Go-to-Market.** A clear product-go-to-market plan whose strategies align with business goals; iterative — some things won't work.
  3. **Storyteller — Shape How the World Thinks About Your Product.** Positioning (long game) + messaging (shorter game); stories are how brains remember and how others can retell.
  4. **Evangelist — Enable Others to Tell the Story.** Sales enablement · influencer relations · community · press · analysts.
- **Product marketing purpose statement.** Ch 1: *"drive product adoption by shaping market perception through strategic marketing activities that meet business goals."* Not brand marketing, not field marketing, not performance marketing — a **distinct first-class discipline**.
- **The market side of product-market fit (Ch 11).** *"There is no way for a product to stand out and win unless its entire go-to-market engine is carefully coordinated and it holds a clear market position."* Backs [[the-four-big-risks|viability]] and the wiki's grounding of the [[2023-05-17-cagan-10-misconceptions-startups-productx|9th misconception]] ("product love is enough").
- **How to partner with PM, marketing, sales (Chs 8–10).** The three-partnership scaffolding that the wiki's [[product-marketing]] page uses; complements *TRANSFORMED* Ch 23's eight PM ↔ PMM collaboration areas.
- **When David Beats Goliath (Ch 1 — Pocket case).** The book's opening case: strong product marketing lets a small team win a category against much larger competitors — the "why the work isn't optional" argument.
- **The One-Sheet Product Go-to-Market Canvas (Ch 19).** A single-page artifact for GTM planning — the practical tool complement to positioning.
- **The Adoption Life Cycle applied to product go-to-market (Ch 15).** Applies the iPhone story to how GTM must shift by adoption stage.
- **CAST (Ch 22).** Lauchengco's practical guide to positioning-conversation listening.

## The PM ↔ PMM relationship, in LOVED's own terms (Ch 8)
LOVED and TRANSFORMED cover the same relationship from opposite sides — TRANSFORMED Ch 23 lists **eight PM ↔ PMM collaboration areas** from the PM's viewpoint (grounded on [[product-marketing]]); LOVED Ch 8 covers the same partnership from the **PMM's viewpoint**, with the *"Anti-Patterns, What Better Looks Like"* structure the book uses throughout. When coaching a PM–PMM relationship, use both — LOVED for the PMM's constraints, TRANSFORMED for the PM's.

## What the book adds beyond the SVPG-article canon
The wiki previously grounded product marketing via **Cagan's mentions in videos and TRANSFORMED Ch 23** — from the PM's side. LOVED is the **primary book-length source for the PMM side**, including:
- The **four fundamentals** as a coherent framework (no SVPG article restates all four).
- **CAST** and the **One-Sheet Canvas** as practical tools not present elsewhere.
- The **market-side-of-PMF** treatment (Ch 11) with the "probe early, probe often" discipline.
- The **PMM's view of the anti-patterns** in each of the three partnerships.
