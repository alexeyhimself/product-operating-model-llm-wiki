# Creative Selection

> Inside Apple's design process — demos, prototypes, and iteration as the engine of great products (the author built the iPhone keyboard).

## Why it's recommended
On SVPG's **[[svpg-product-operating-model-introduction|Product Operating Model: An Introduction]]** reading list. A vivid illustration of [[product-discovery|discovery]] by demo/prototype — Apple exemplar.

## The Steve Jobs myth
Cagan cites *Creative Selection* as the one book that shows what Apple's product discovery under Steve Jobs was *actually* like ([[2025-04-21-cagan-design-a-product-like-steve-jobs|How to Design a Product Like Steve Jobs]]). Jobs was **not** a top-down dictator handing engineers solutions; he set a very high bar and teams brought him prototypes weekly, iterating toward something good enough to ship. Kocienda's account of the original iPhone — a "100% touchscreen device" → ~100 hard problems for [[empowered-engineers|empowered engineers]] to solve — is the antidote to the dangerous "act like Jobs = boss everyone around" misreading. Jobs "didn't know the solutions; he just knew what would make a great product." Pairs with the 1995 Jobs *Lost Interview* (also a Cagan pick).
