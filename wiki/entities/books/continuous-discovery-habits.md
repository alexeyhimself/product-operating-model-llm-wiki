# Continuous Discovery Habits

> [[teresa-torres|Teresa Torres]]'s **operational manual** for continuous discovery — the industry's most-used how-to. Weekly customer touchpoints, opportunity solution trees, assumption testing. Book URL: [amazon.com/dp/1736633309](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1736633309). Publisher: [Product Talk LLC](https://www.producttalk.org/) (Bend, OR). ISBN 978-1-7366333-0-4. **[[marty-cagan|Cagan]] wrote the Foreword** — the strongest existing endorsement of a non-SVPG book on discovery.

## Where it sits in the canon
**Primary-adjacent.** The book is not SVPG canon, but Cagan endorsed it as the go-to how-to on the SVPG *[[svpg-product-operating-model-introduction|Product Operating Model: An Introduction]]* reading list and personally in the Foreword. In this wiki:
- Attribute *what* discovery is and *why* it matters to **Cagan/SVPG** ([[inspired]] Chs 33–58, [[transformed]] Chs 10, 18).
- Attribute the **specific operational techniques** — opportunity solution tree, weekly-cadence continuous interviewing, assumption testing as a discipline — to **Torres/CDH**.
- Where the two frame the same idea differently (opportunities vs problems-to-solve; ethics as separate vs inside viability; "change in human behavior" outcomes), name the difference explicitly. Neither is wrong.

## What it covers
The **habits** — small, repeatable, weekly-cadence practices — that let a cross-functional product trio do continuous discovery in a sustainable way. Audience: product managers, designers, engineers on a **product trio** (Torres uses Cagan's term). Scope: from setting an outcome through stakeholder management. Not: strategy at the org level, leadership, transformation politics — those are SVPG's lane and Torres links to them.

## Structure
Three parts, 15 chapters:

- **Part 1 — What is Continuous Discovery? (Chs 1–2)**
  - Ch 1 The What and Why of Continuous Discovery — the operational definition; the six prerequisite mindsets.
  - Ch 2 A Common Framework for Continuous Discovery — the **opportunity solution tree** introduced; its seven benefits.
- **Part 2 — The Continuous Discovery Habits (Chs 3–13)** — the 11 habits.
  - Ch 3 Focusing on Outcomes Over Outputs — business/product/traction three-tier; two-way negotiation.
  - Ch 4 Visualizing What You Know — the experience map; individual-first to avoid groupthink.
  - Ch 5 Continuous Interviewing — the **keystone habit**; story-based, not direct-question interviewing.
  - Ch 6 Mapping the Opportunity Space — distinct branches; parent-child / sibling relationships; taming the opportunity backlog.
  - Ch 7 Prioritizing Opportunities, Not Solutions — compare-and-contrast, not whether-or-not.
  - Ch 8 Supercharged Ideation — quantity → quality; individual > brainstorming; individual → share → individual; dot-vote to 3.
  - Ch 9 Identifying Hidden Assumptions — five assumption types (desirability · viability · feasibility · usability · ethical); story-map to surface; the assumption map.
  - Ch 10 Testing Assumptions, Not Ideas — simulate-experience-evaluate-behavior; early signals then larger tests; false positives vs false negatives; unmoderated user testing + one-question surveys.
  - Ch 11 Measuring Impact — traction → product outcome → business outcome; the AfterCollege worked example (Torres's own).
  - Ch 12 Managing the Cycles — loop back when surprised; four case studies (Simply Business, CarMax, FCSAmerica, Snagajob).
  - Ch 13 Show Your Work — the OST as the stakeholder-management artifact; don't lead with conclusions; anti-HiPPO.
- **Part 3 — Developing Your Continuous Discovery Habits (Chs 14–15)**
  - Ch 14 Start Small, and Iterate — for teams whose companies don't support this yet.
  - Ch 15 What's Next? — Product Talk resources.

Two forewords: **[[marty-cagan|Marty Cagan]]** (SVPG endorsement) and **Chris Mercuri** (a coached PM).

## Key contributions

**The operational definition of continuous discovery** (Ch 1):
> *At a minimum, weekly touchpoints with customers · by the team building the product · where they conduct small research activities · in pursuit of a desired outcome.*

**The opportunity solution tree** (Chs 2, 6, 7) — outcome → opportunities → solutions → assumption tests. Compare-and-contrast, not whether-or-not (Heath brothers, *Decisive*). Top-down and bottom-up together. See [[opportunity-solution-tree]].

**Story-based interviewing** (Ch 5) — direct questions produce ideal-self answers ("fit is my #1 factor when buying jeans"); ask instead for a **specific recent instance** ("tell me about the last time you bought jeans"). Grounded in the Gazzaniga "left-brain interpreter" and Kahneman's confidence-≠-truth. See [[continuous-interviewing]].

**Assumption testing over idea testing** (Chs 9, 10) — testing whole ideas is too slow; enumerate the leap-of-faith assumptions each idea depends on and test those. Five categories: **desirability · viability · feasibility · usability · ethical** (Torres keeps ethical separate). Story-map ideas to surface assumptions. Simulate an experience, evaluate behavior. Small tests → iterate to larger. **Cagan is quoted directly in the Ch 9 epigraph** ("teams competent in modern discovery techniques can generally test on the order of 10–20 iterations per week"). See [[assumption-testing]].

**The outcomes taxonomy** (Ch 3) — business outcomes vs product outcomes vs traction metrics. Leading vs lagging indicators. The **two-way negotiation** of the outcome between product leader and trio. **Torres attributes "outcome = a change in human behavior that drives business results" to Josh Seiden** (Ch 3 epigraph) — the wiki previously mis-cited Torres as the origin. See [[outcomes-over-output]], [[team-objectives]].

**"Show your work"** (Ch 13) — the OST is the **stakeholder-management artifact**. Don't lead with conclusions (invites opinion battles the PM loses to the HiPPO); walk stakeholders through outcome → opportunities → solutions → tests. This is Torres's operational answer to the anti-HiPPO problem.

**The "start small, iterate" stance** (Ch 14) — Torres's response to *"my company doesn't work this way"* is agency: pick one habit, next week look better than last week. Aligns with Cagan's *[[transformation-as-a-project|use the model to transform]]* posture, applied to the individual PM.
