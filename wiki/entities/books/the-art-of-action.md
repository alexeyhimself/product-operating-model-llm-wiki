# The Art of Action

> Stephen Bungay draws on military strategy (the Prussian concept of *Auftragstaktik* / "command intent") to explain how leaders close the gaps between plans, actions, and outcomes: give people the intent and the context, not detailed instructions.

## Why it's recommended
The second strategy book Cagan endorses in [[2020-02-22-cagan-product-strategy-the-missing-link|"Product Strategy: The Missing Link"]]. Cagan: "he's really talking about OKRs" — describing how they're *supposed* to be used — even though the book never mentions them. **Command intent = strategic context** (vision + strategy + business objectives + product principles), the empowered-team analogue of a military squad given a mission and the big picture rather than a recipe.
