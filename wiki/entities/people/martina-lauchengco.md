# Martina Lauchengco

> SVPG partner who leads the group's **[[product-marketing|product marketing]]** practice and wrote *[[loved|LOVED]]* (2022) — the product-marketing volume of the SVPG series ([[inspired]] · [[empowered]] · *LOVED* · [[transformed]]). Teaches product marketing at UC Berkeley; earlier led marketing/product on Microsoft Office and Netscape Communicator.

## Role here
The wiki's canon on **product marketing as a distinct, load-bearing competency** — the *market* side of product-market fit — traces to Lauchengco. Cagan repeatedly defers to her on the topic: in the [[2022-03-07-cagan-product-ops-dan-olsen|Product Ops fireside]] he notes "my partner Martina" has "a book coming out… that takes a stab at what great product marketing is," and points to product marketing (not product ops) as the role most product managers actually need a partner for, especially with a direct sales channel.

## Career origins (via *LOVED*)
Lauchengco's LOVED opens by grounding her PMM point of view in specific worked-experience moments — useful when coaching a PM who wonders how a PMM's judgment is earned:

- **Microsoft Word (early 1990s)** — was on the product-marketing team when Windows-release timing forced a half-cycle product schedule; the team pivoted to positioning the smaller release around *"what matters most for how most people use Word"* (grounded in an instrumentation study of keystrokes). Walt Mossberg's review — normally famously unmovable — surprised the team: *"the best writing tool out there."* That version became Word's best-reviewed and most successful up to that time. Grounds Ch 2 (the four fundamentals) and Ch 6 (evangelism activation for reviewers/pundits).
- **Netscape Communicator** — later PMM work Lauchengco references in *LOVED* framing.
- **UC Berkeley** — teaches product marketing.
- **SVPG partner** — leads the group's product-marketing practice.

She's profiled by name in *[[inspired|INSPIRED]]* (Ch 40 — *Profile: Martina Lauchengco of Microsoft*) as one of Cagan's six PMs illustrating the role.
