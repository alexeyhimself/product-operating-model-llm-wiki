# Bill Campbell

> "The Trillion Dollar Coach." The coach behind Steve Jobs, Jeff Bezos, and Larry Page & Sergey Brin — and, Cagan argues, the person who was "essentially explaining the product model" to the founders of the companies the model is drawn from. Former CEO of Intuit; d. 2016.

## Contributions & positions
- **"You cannot be a good manager without being a good coach."** Cagan's most-cited Campbell line; the reason coaching (not process) is the answer to scaling — see [[founder-style-leadership]] and [[product-coaching]].
- **"The single most important thing is empowered engineers."** Campbell's answer to what drives innovation: engineers who are there to figure out the right solution, not just build what they're told (they know what's "just now possible"). Anchors [[empowered-engineers]].
- **Coaching as a management responsibility**, institutionalized. Cagan notes the remarkably consistent leadership principles at Amazon ("dive deep," "hire and develop the best"), Apple ("experts lead experts"), and Google ("develop others through coaching") trace substantially to Campbell.

Cagan never worked for Campbell directly but "worked for three different people who were coached by him," and describes learning these lessons secondhand as "a real gift."
