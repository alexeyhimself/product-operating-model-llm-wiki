# Bill Campbell

> "The Trillion Dollar Coach." The coach behind Steve Jobs, Jeff Bezos, and Larry Page & Sergey Brin — and, Cagan argues, the person who was "essentially explaining the product model" to the founders of the companies the model is drawn from. Former CEO of Intuit; d. 2016. Cagan (via *[[empowered|EMPOWERED]]* Ch 1): *"One surprising common thread among many of the best product companies is the legendary coach, Bill Campbell. During their formative years, Bill literally provided executive coaching to the founders of Apple, Amazon, and Google, as well as several others."*

## Canonical quotes (book-form primaries)
Three quotes anchor the wiki's use of Campbell. All are used repeatedly by Cagan and now grounded in specific book chapters:

- **On leadership** ([[empowered|EMPOWERED]] Ch 1): *"Leadership is about recognizing that there's a greatness in everyone, and your job is to create an environment where that greatness can emerge."* Cagan uses this as the frame for what EMPOWERED is about — identifying and building that environment. Backs [[product-leadership]] and [[founder-style-leadership]].
- **On coaching vs mentoring** ([[empowered|EMPOWERED]] Ch 7, opening the coaching part of the book): *"Coaching might be even more essential than mentoring to our careers and our teams. Whereas mentors dole out words of wisdom, coaches roll up their sleeves and get their hands dirty. They don't just believe in our potential; they get in the arena to help us realize our potential. They hold up a mirror so we can see our blind spots and they hold us accountable for working through our sore spots. They take responsibility for making us better without taking credit for our accomplishments."* Anchors [[coaching-mindset]] and [[product-coaching]] — the book-form primary for what a coach actually does.
- **On the manager-coach fusion** (quoted by Cagan across the SVPG canon; also opens [[transformed|TRANSFORMED]] Ch 34): *"Coaching is no longer a specialty; you cannot be a good manager without being a good coach."* Anchors [[product-coaching]], [[product-leadership]], and the coaching-supply argument that leads to [[model-as-product-coach|model-as-coach]].

## Contributions & positions
- **The manager-coach fusion.** Cagan's most-cited Campbell line ("you cannot be a good manager without being a good coach") is the reason coaching — not process — is the answer to scaling. See [[founder-style-leadership]] and [[product-coaching]].
- **"The single most important thing is empowered engineers."** Campbell's answer to what drives innovation: engineers who figure out the right solution, not just build what they're told (they know what's *"just now possible"*). Anchors [[empowered-engineers]].
- **Coaching as a management responsibility**, institutionalized. Cagan notes the remarkably consistent leadership principles at Amazon ("dive deep," "hire and develop the best"), Apple ("experts lead experts"), and Google ("develop others through coaching") trace substantially to Campbell.
- **The "greatness in everyone" leadership definition** ([[empowered|EMPOWERED]] Ch 1) — the frame for what the book (and this wiki's [[coaching-mindset|coaching-mindset]] page) tries to build. *"This book is all about identifying what makes such an environment."*

Cagan never worked for Campbell directly but "worked for three different people who were coached by him," and describes learning these lessons secondhand as "a real gift."
