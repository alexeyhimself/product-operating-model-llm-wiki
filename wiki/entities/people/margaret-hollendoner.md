# Margaret Hollendoner

> Product coach with 18+ years inside [[google|Google]] before going independent. Profiled in *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]* Ch 34. Stanford mechanical engineer by training; England-based; specializes in product vision + strategy + cross-functional team development.

## Role here
One of the seven product coaches Cagan personally profiles in Ch 34. Represents the *product-leadership-plus-team-development* lane — vision, strategy, stakeholder alignment, cross-functional collaboration.

## Path (per Ch 34)
- Stanford mechanical engineering; almost pursued a PhD in thermodynamics before choosing a more people-facing role.
- Joined **[[google|Google]]** — 18-year tenure spanning Mountain View + London.
- Led PM teams across consumer + B2B in health, video, commerce, and ads. Launched **AdSense**, **YouTube Video Measurement**, **Google Fit**, among others.
- Also led work on discovery efforts that never shipped — learned to *"celebrate success as well as failure and nurture both within the culture."*
- Six-month temporary assignment on Google tech HR to improve cross-functional collaboration.
- Founding member of Google's tech apprenticeship program; built peer-mentoring communities across women PMs, London PMs, and managers of PMs.
- Went full-time as a product coach.

## Themes (per Ch 34)
The role Cagan calls *"the person who crossed the street"* — going between engineering and customer-facing buildings to keep product strategy + discovery close to user needs. As a coach: vision + strategy + stakeholder alignment + cross-functional team development.
