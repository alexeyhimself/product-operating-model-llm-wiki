# Teresa Torres

> Product-discovery coach; founder of **Product Talk LLC**; author of *[[continuous-discovery-habits|Continuous Discovery Habits]]* (2021). The industry's most widely-used how-to for discovery, and the source of the **opportunity solution tree** and **continuous-interviewing** techniques the wiki treats as canon. **Explicitly endorsed by [[marty-cagan|Cagan]]** — he wrote the book's Foreword ("[…] the most scalable solution I know of to this problem is to enlist the help of an experienced product coach. This is how I first met Teresa many years ago. […] I kept recommending her because the companies and teams she was helping were soon doing visibly better.").

## Role here
Torres is **SVPG-adjacent**, not SVPG. Cagan repeatedly refers product organizations to her and wrote the *CDH* Foreword; her method is the operational how-to for the discovery principles the wiki grounds in Cagan/SVPG. Where Cagan articulates *what* discovery is and *why* it matters, Torres provides the weekly habit-level *how*. Attribute discovery concepts to Cagan/SVPG as root; attribute the specific techniques (**opportunity solution tree**, **continuous interviewing**, **assumption testing** as an operational discipline) to Torres.

## Contributions & positions
- **Continuous discovery — the operational definition** (*CDH* Ch 1): *weekly touchpoints with customers · by the team building the product · where they conduct small research activities · in pursuit of a desired outcome.* The four lines are load-bearing — most teams break down at "by the team building the product" (research handed to an agency) or "in pursuit of a desired outcome" (research for research's sake).
- **The six prerequisite mindsets** (Ch 1): outcome-oriented · customer-centric · collaborative · visual · experimental · continuous. Tactics change if the mindset doesn't.
- **The opportunity solution tree (OST)** (Chs 2, 6, 7) — Torres's signature contribution: **outcome → opportunities → solutions → assumption tests**, visualized as a tree with parent-child and sibling relationships. Torres's own origin note: she developed it while coaching a team in 2016 who asked her *"how are we supposed to do this on our own?"* (Ch 2 endnote points to ProductTalk.org/ost-origin). See [[opportunity-solution-tree]].
- **"Opportunities," not "problems to solve"** (Ch 2): Torres deliberately widens Cagan's *problems-to-solve* framing to include **desires** — Disneyland, ice cream, mountain biking are opportunities to *intervene positively in the customer's life*, not problems to fix. Different terminology, same intent.
- **Continuous interviewing as the keystone habit** (Chs 5, 14): weekly customer touchpoints are the **keystone** (Duhigg) — the habit that unlocks all the others. Story-based interviewing (*"tell me about the last time you…"*) replaces direct questions; interview snapshots capture what was learned. See [[continuous-interviewing]].
- **Assumption testing over idea testing** (Chs 9, 10): five assumption categories — **desirability · viability · feasibility · usability · ethical** (kept separate). Story-map ideas to surface assumptions. Simulate an experience, evaluate behavior. Small tests → iterate to larger. See [[assumption-testing]].
- **Outcomes taxonomy** (Ch 3): **business outcomes vs product outcomes vs traction metrics**; leading vs lagging indicators; the **two-way negotiation** of the outcome between product leader and trio. Torres attributes the "outcome = change in human behavior" definition to Josh Seiden (epigraph); her own operational definition is broader (*product outcomes* = metrics the team can influence). See [[outcomes-over-output]], [[team-objectives]].
- **The "show your work" stance** (Ch 13): the OST is the stakeholder-management artifact — don't lead with conclusions ("HiPPO wins the opinion battle"); walk stakeholders through outcome → opportunities → solutions → tests. Anti-HiPPO discipline.

## Debated positions and lineage notes
- **Ethics as a separate assumption category (Torres 2021, Cagan-endorsed).** Torres keeps ethics as its own risk (Ch 9), aligned with Cagan's **pre-2017/2018** framing ([[the-four-big-risks|lineage note]]). Cagan's *modern* canon (*TRANSFORMED* 2024) folds ethics *inside* viability. **Not a contradiction** — a legitimate 2021 alternative categorization; useful lineage data point.
- **"Change in human behavior" nuance on outcomes.** The wiki previously read Torres as *narrower* than Cagan on outcomes ("Torres = changes in human behavior only"). Direct reading of Ch 3 shows this is more measured: Torres attributes the "change in human behavior" wording to **Josh Seiden** (Ch 3 epigraph; *Outcomes Over Output*) and defines *product outcomes* pragmatically as "metrics the team can influence" — close to Cagan's operational usage. Coach take: name the origin correctly (Seiden), then use whichever framing keeps the team honest about influence and impact.
- **Individuals outperform brainstorming groups** (Ch 8) — Torres synthesizes decades of creativity research (Nijstad's "illusion of group productivity") and prescribes an **individual → share → individual → dot-vote** ideation pattern. This aligns with Cagan's *"generate ideas alone before group discussion"* discipline but is more explicit.
