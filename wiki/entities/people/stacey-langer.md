# Stacey Langer

> Product coach specializing in **transformation** at enterprise scale. Profiled in *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]* Ch 34. Led product-model transformations at **Best Buy** (two decades) and the **US Department of Veterans Affairs** (through USDS).

## Role here
One of the seven product coaches Cagan personally profiles in Ch 34. Represents the *transformation coach* lane — the coach type who works directly with senior leaders through the mindset and cultural changes.

## Path (per Ch 34)
- Joined **Best Buy** as it began expanding from physical retail to online.
- 20-year tenure — content creation → product design → product management → testing/optimization → manager → director → **SVP of product**.
- Was one of the early managers asked to lead Best Buy's transformation from outsourced waterfall IT to in-house product teams; staffed and coached across PM/design/engineering.
- Cagan on Best Buy: *"the fact that Best Buy still exists and thrives, while so many other electronic retailers are no longer part of the competitive landscape, is testament to the company's ability to adapt and change."*
- Left to help the **US Digital Service** — served as **digital services director and deputy CTO** of the **US Department of Veterans Affairs**, applying what she'd learned to help millions of veterans discover, apply for, track, and manage benefits.

## Themes (per Ch 34)
*"Creating space for talented teams to build great experiences for their users"* — cross-capability collaboration that produces solutions no individual would have reached alone. As a coach: applies real-world product leadership + transformation experience to help leaders and teams move to the product model, user-centered.
