# Marty Cagan

> The most influential articulator of the modern **Product Operating Model**; founder of [[svpg|Silicon Valley Product Group]] and author of the canonical product books, including *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]* — the root source of this wiki's [[overview|20 first principles]].

## Contributions & positions
Cagan didn't *invent* the first principles — he and the [[svpg|SVPG]] team **observed** that the world's best product companies share the same underlying principles, and **articulated** that set (most fully in *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]*), in contrast to the project/IT/feature-factory model. Core positions running through his work and the [[overview|20 first principles]]:

- **Empowered product teams** over feature teams — give teams problems, not solutions, and hold them accountable for [[outcomes-over-output|outcomes]].
- **Product discovery** (test ideas cheaply against the [[the-four-big-risks|four big risks]]) distinct from **product delivery**; output of discovery is a *validated product backlog*.
- The **PM is explicitly responsible for value and viability** risks (*TRANSFORMED* Ch. 10).
- **One holistic [[product-strategy]]**, owned by product leaders, driven by [[powered-by-insights|insights]] and expressed as [[placing-bets|bets]]; **product mission** dropped in *TRANSFORMED* in favor of [[product-vision|vision]].
- **Missionaries, not mercenaries**; lead with **context, not control** ([[trust-over-control]]); **principles over process** at scale.
- On **risk taxonomy** (Q&A with Huryn, 2024): deliberately keeps it to ~four risks — "once you get to more than 4... it just becomes a checklist and not a way of thinking"; "ethical risk is part of business viability risk."
