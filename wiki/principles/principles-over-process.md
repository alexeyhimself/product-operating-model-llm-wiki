# Principles over Process

> As organizations grow, scale with **strong leaders and good judgment guided by principles** — not with process designed to prevent people from exercising judgment.

## The belief behind it
There are two ways orgs try to scale: with **leaders (managers)** or with **process** ([[2021-10-28-cagan-process-people|Cagan, *Process People*, 2021]] — the primary written source). Cagan favors the former. As Reed Hastings puts it (*No Rules Rules*): "If you give employees more freedom instead of developing processes to prevent them from exercising their own judgment, they will make better decisions and it's easier to hold them accountable" (see [[2024-03-21-huryn-product-model-first-principles-part-2]]). Process that encodes distrust slows everything and de-skills the org.

The mechanism by which process-based scaling fails: every tool/method **embeds beliefs about teams and leadership** — what it makes easy and what it makes hard reveal what its author wants to encourage or suppress ([[2018-07-26-cagan-tools-and-processes|Cagan, *Tools and Processes*, 2018]]). Standardizing a process across a large org therefore institutionalizes *one* belief system as a **poor fit for most teams**. Bezos: "Good process serves you so you can serve customers. But if you're not watchful, the process can become the thing." That "if you're not watchful" is where the [[process-people|process people]] enter — the third contributor type Cagan named in 2021, hired to run the process itself rather than build the product.

## What it looks like when followed
A small set of shared principles guides decisions (a company's written [[product-principles]] are the concrete artifact of this meta-principle); leaders coach judgment rather than writing ever-more rules; [[product-ops|product operations]] supports teams rather than policing them; the org adds managers/coaches, not gates, as it grows.

## What violating it looks like
Heavy stage-gates, approval chains, and mandatory templates that substitute for thinking; "we can't, the process says…"; scaling by adding controls instead of capability; bureaucracy that punishes judgment. Cagan calls this the **"disease of process people"** — Steve Jobs's phrase in his 1995 "Lost Interview," describing the process people who took over and gutted IBM; Bezos, Musk, and Steve Blank warn of the same. The **operator who scales with process rather than coaching** is the modern carrier ([[2022-12-07-cagan-product-lessons-jobs-musk-20vc|20VC]], [[2024-06-24-cagan-pm-and-experimentation-testing-insights|Experimentation]], [[2023-05-17-cagan-10-misconceptions-startups-productx|10 Misconceptions]]). The alternative to process is **leaders who scale with coaching** — see [[product-leadership]].

## Tensions & trade-offs
- Some process is essential (compliance, safety, [[test-ideas-responsibly|responsible testing]]); the principle is about *defaulting* to principles + judgment, not abolishing all process.
- Requires capable, trusted people — pairs with [[trust-over-control]] and presupposes real [[empowered-product-teams|empowerment]].
