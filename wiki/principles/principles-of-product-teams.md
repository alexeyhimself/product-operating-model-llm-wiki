# Principles of Product Teams

> The first of five groups of [product model first principles](overview.md) from *[[transformed|TRANSFORMED]]*. "The most fundamental of all product concepts is the notion of an **empowered, cross-functional product team**" (Cagan).

These four principles define how a product team is set up and behaves. They build on each other: a team **empowered with problems** is measured on **outcomes**, takes **ownership** of solving them end to end, and does so through real **collaboration**.

1. **[[empowered-with-problems-to-solve|Empowered with Problems to Solve]]** — given problems (with desired outcomes), not solutions; held accountable for results.
2. **[[outcomes-over-output|Outcomes over Output]]** — measured on results delivered, not features shipped.
3. **[[sense-of-ownership|Sense of Ownership]]** — the team owns both [[product-discovery|discovery]] and [[product-delivery|delivery]] of its problem.
4. **[[collaboration|Collaboration]]** — PM, design, and engineering solve problems together; collaboration ≠ consensus.
