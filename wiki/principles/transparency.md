# Transparency

> Be open about the **data, the reasoning, and the trade-offs** behind strategic choices — so stakeholders buy in and teams can make good autonomous decisions.

## The belief behind it
Autonomy at scale only works if people understand not just *what* was chosen but *why* (Sinek's "Start with Why"). Transparency about data and reasoning earns stakeholder buy-in and heads off "jealousy or frustration if stakeholders worry that product leaders are pursuing their own agendas" (*TRANSFORMED* Ch. 16). Without it, you cannot lead with context rather than control (see [[2024-03-16-huryn-product-model-first-principles-part-1]]).

## What it looks like when followed
Strategy is shared with its rationale and the insights behind it; affected stakeholders are consulted before choices land; teams can trace their objective back to a strategic "why" and decide accordingly.

## What violating it looks like
Strategy delivered as edict with no rationale; stakeholders surprised by choices that affect them; teams told *what* to pursue but not *why*, so they can't make sensible trade-offs on their own.

## Tensions & trade-offs
- Transparency vs. speed/noise: sharing reasoning widely takes effort and invites pushback — but the alternative (opaque edicts) destroys the autonomy the model depends on.
- Pairs tightly with [[trust-over-control]] (culture) — context is the mechanism that makes trust safe.
