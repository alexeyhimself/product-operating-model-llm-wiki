# Sense of Ownership

> A product team owns its problem end to end — **both** finding the solution ([[product-discovery|discovery]]) **and** building and shipping it ([[product-delivery|delivery]]) — driven by purpose and autonomy.

## The belief behind it
People act like missionaries (not mercenaries) when they own a meaningful problem and have the autonomy to solve it. Huryn aligns this with purpose ("Why are we doing this?") and autonomy — Netflix's "lead with context, not control" (Hastings & Meyer) — and with *Team Topologies*' team-first ownership of a part of the product (Skelton & Pais). Critically, Cagan says the team is responsible for **both** coming up with solutions *and* delivering them to customers (see [[2024-03-16-huryn-product-model-first-principles-part-1]]).

## What it looks like when followed
One team owns a durable problem space and its outcome over time; discovery and delivery are not handed off between separate groups; the team feels personally responsible when the metric stalls and pushes to fix it.

## What violating it looks like
Discovery and delivery split across "design/PM teams" and "dev teams"; teams rented out as feature-delivery capacity with no continuity of ownership; mercenary behavior — "we built what we were told."

## Tensions & trade-offs
- Ownership needs autonomy, which needs [[trust-over-control]] and the [[transparency|context]] to decide well — autonomy without context is abandonment.
- Whole-product ownership can collide with shared platforms/dependencies; resolved via team topology choices, not by stripping ownership.
