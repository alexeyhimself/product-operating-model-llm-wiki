# Powered by Insights

> Product strategy is **driven by insights**, not opinion or politics — and the best insights often come from the product teams themselves.

## The belief behind it
You can't choose the most important problems well without understanding where leverage lies. "Insights can come from anywhere," but Cagan names four main sources: **analyzing the data, talking to customers, new enabling technologies, and the broader industry** (see [[2024-03-16-huryn-product-model-first-principles-part-1]]). Strategy is *discovered, not just crafted* (Huryn) — it takes iterations and measurement. In the Q&A, Cagan confirms learnings/insights from [[product-discovery|discovery]] commonly drive the *next iteration* of strategy, and many of the best insights come from teams (qualitative and quantitative).

## What it looks like when followed
Leaders can name the data, customer, technology, and industry insights behind each strategic choice; strategy is revisited as evidence accumulates; teams' discovery findings feed upward into the next strategy cycle (top-down + bottom-up).

## What violating it looks like
Strategy set in a single offsite by loudest-voice or HiPPO; choices justified by opinion, precedent, or org politics; team learnings never reach the people setting strategy.

## Tensions & trade-offs
- Insight-gathering vs. decisiveness: strategy needs enough signal to choose without waiting for certainty — pairs with [[placing-bets]].
- "Insights from anywhere" still requires [[focus]] to convert into a small set of bets.
