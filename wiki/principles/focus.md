# Focus

> Product strategy means **saying no** to most good ideas so you can make real progress on the few that matter most.

## The belief behind it
Strategy is about which problems are *most important* to solve; spreading effort across everything guarantees meaningful progress on nothing. Cagan quotes Steve Jobs: "focus... means saying no to the hundred other good ideas... Innovation is saying no to 1,000 things." Huryn ties this to deliberate **trade-offs** (Product Strategy Canvas): trade-offs define what *not* to do, create focus, amplify value, and make a strategy hard to copy (see [[2024-03-16-huryn-product-model-first-principles-part-1]]).

## What it looks like when followed
A short list of the most important problems this period; an explicit "not now" list; leaders proud of what they chose *not* to do. Teams aren't fragmented across ten half-funded bets.

## What violating it looks like
Strategy as a wish-list that says yes to every stakeholder; "priorities" so numerous nothing is actually prioritized; thinly spread teams; a roadmap no competitor would struggle to copy because it makes no hard choices.

## Tensions & trade-offs
- Focus vs. [[placing-bets]]: you concentrate on few problems, yet may assign one critical problem to *multiple* teams — focus is about which problems, betting is about odds of solving them.
- Saying no creates stakeholder friction; resolved by [[transparency]] about the reasoning, not by quietly saying yes to everything.
