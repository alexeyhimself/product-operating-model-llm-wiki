# Principles of Product Discovery

> The third group of [product model first principles](overview.md). "Product discovery is the most important area for a product manager" (Huryn). Its job: figure out *what to build* before you build it — cheaply.

These four principles define how teams decide what's worth building: **minimize waste**, **assess the risks**, **experiment rapidly** to address them, and do it all **responsibly** in an established business.

9. **[[minimize-waste|Minimize Waste]]** — solve the problem with minimum wasted time/effort; most ideas won't work.
10. **[[assess-product-risks|Assess Product Risks]]** — consider value, usability, feasibility, viability (see [[the-four-big-risks]]); PM owns value + viability.
11. **[[embrace-rapid-experimentation|Embrace Rapid Experimentation]]** — small, fast, cheap experiments; output is a validated product backlog.
12. **[[test-ideas-responsibly|Test Ideas Responsibly]]** — protect revenue, reputation, customers, and colleagues while experimenting.

See also: [[product-discovery]], [[the-four-big-risks]] (concepts). **Flag:** canon teaches **four** risks with ethics inside viability — see [[the-four-big-risks]].
