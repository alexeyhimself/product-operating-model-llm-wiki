# Principles of Product Strategy

> The second group of [product model first principles](overview.md). "Product strategy is all about which problems are the most important to solve" (Cagan, *TRANSFORMED* Ch. 16). There is **one** holistic product strategy, owned by [[product-leadership|product leaders]]; its output is a set of problems to solve ([[team-objectives|team objectives]]).

These four principles define how good product strategy is made: it is **focused** (says no), **powered by insights** (not opinion), **transparent** (shared with reasoning), and expressed as **bets** (since solving isn't guaranteed).

5. **[[focus|Focus]]** — say no to the many good ideas to make real progress on the few that matter.
6. **[[powered-by-insights|Powered by Insights]]** — driven by data, customers, enabling tech, and the industry; insights often come from the teams.
7. **[[transparency|Transparency]]** — share the data and reasoning so stakeholders buy in and teams can decide autonomously.
8. **[[placing-bets|Placing Bets]]** — play the odds; a critical problem may go to multiple teams.

See also: [[product-vision]], [[product-strategy]] (concepts). Cagan's Q&A clarifies value proposition is *orthogonal* to strategy and that business strategy is the input to product strategy — see [[2024-03-16-huryn-product-model-first-principles-part-1]].
